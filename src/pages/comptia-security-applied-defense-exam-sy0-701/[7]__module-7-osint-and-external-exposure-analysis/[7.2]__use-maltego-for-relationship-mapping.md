## **Course:** CompTIA Security+ Applied Defense (Exam SY0-701)

**What Maltego does**

Maltego is a graphical link analysis tool used to gather, analyze, and visualize relationships between entities. It helps you connect seemingly separate leads and build a map of a target’s digital footprint.

- It supports **OSINT** (open-source intelligence) workflows by mining public data sources.
- It can relate items such as names, email addresses, domains, documents, IP addresses, and social media profiles.
- It helps investigators work with both internal and external data sources in one interface.
- It is useful for fraud analysis, threat investigations, and person-of-interest research.

Maltego is especially valuable when data is scattered across many places. Instead of manually comparing disconnected records, you use graphs to reveal patterns, links, and hidden relationships.

**Core concepts**

Maltego investigations center on a few basic building blocks:

- **Entities** represent things you want to investigate, such as a domain or person.
- **Transforms** are actions that query data sources and generate new related entities.
- **Graphs** are the visual maps that show the relationships between entities.

A typical workflow starts with one entity, then expands outward as transforms discover more connected data. This makes it easier to trace infrastructure, identify weak points, and understand how information spreads.

**Starting a graph**

You begin by creating a new graph and adding an entity from the Entity Palette. From there, you run transforms to populate the graph with related entities.

1. Open Maltego and create a new graph.
2. Drag an entity, such as a domain, onto the graph.
3. Run transforms on that entity to discover related data.
4. Review the new entities and copy useful ones into other graphs if needed.

This process supports iterative investigation. You can keep expanding the graph until you have enough context to support analysis or reporting.

**Relationship mapping use cases**

Maltego supports several practical investigation scenarios:

- **Person-of-interest investigations** help you map a person’s digital footprint and link online identities.
- **Network footprinting** helps you gather infrastructure details for a domain or organization.
- **Attack surface assessment** helps you identify public entry points such as websites, social profiles, network devices, and cloud services.
- **Misinformation analysis** helps you trace internet relationships and understand how content spreads.

These use cases all rely on the same idea: collect related artifacts, then visualize how they connect. That visual model can reveal gaps, dependencies, and possible weak points.

**Working with multiple data sources**

A major strength of Maltego is that it can unify internal and external data sources. This reduces the burden of switching between tools and manually correlating results.

- A unified interface can simplify analyst workflows.
- Pre-built tooling can save time compared with building a custom solution in-house.
- The platform is useful when you need to combine large volumes of disconnected data.

For exam purposes, focus on the operational benefit: relationship mapping becomes faster when you can analyze many sources in one place.

**Transforms and automation**

Transforms are central to Maltego’s value. They let you expand a graph by querying available data and adding new entities automatically.

- Use transforms to uncover hidden relationships within a dataset.
- Use them to spider out links from a web page or domain.
- Use automation features, such as Maltego Machines, to simplify repetitive footprinting tasks.

Transforms do not replace analysis; they support it. You still need to review the graph and decide which relationships matter.

**Best-practice mindset**

Use Maltego responsibly and stay within authorized, lawful activity. The tool is designed for investigation and analysis, not misuse.

> Do not use Maltego to collect data for unlawful actions or spam. Treat generated graphs and collected data as investigative material that must be handled appropriately.

Keep your workflow focused on evidence, context, and traceable relationships. That approach helps you avoid overinterpreting weak links and keeps your findings practical.

**Exam focus**

For Security+ Applied Defense, remember these takeaways:

- Maltego is a **link analysis** tool for relationship mapping.
- It helps you visualize connections across public and internal data.
- Entities, transforms, and graphs are the core workflow elements.
- It supports OSINT, footprinting, and attack surface analysis.
- It is useful when you need to turn scattered data into a coherent investigative picture.
