## **Course:** CompTIA Security+ Applied Defense (Exam SY0-701)

**Why overly permissive policies matter**

Overly permissive policies give identities more access than they need. In cloud environments, that creates a direct path for misuse if credentials are compromised. The core defense is the <abbr title="Principle of Least Privilege">PoLP</abbr>, also called the principle of least authority: each identity, process, or system gets only the minimum permissions required to do its job.

- Excessive permissions can expose sensitive resources.
- Broad access can allow infrastructure changes.
- Weak policy scope can support data exfiltration.
- Overly broad rights can enable privilege escalation.

**What to look for**

You should review both managed and inline policies attached to IAM identities, including roles and groups. The goal is to find permissions that are broader than the intended function.

- Policies that grant admin access are a clear red flag.
- Wildcard permissions often indicate unnecessary breadth.
- Group policies can also be too permissive, not just role policies.
- KMS (Key Management Service) permissions should be scoped carefully to avoid resource exposure.

**How to detect excessive access**

Use the tools available in the cloud platform to inspect effective permissions and identify unused or unnecessary access. AWS provides IAM review paths through the console, CLI, and SDKs, and IAM Access Analyzer can help surface unused permissions and generate recommendations.

1. Review the IAM policies attached to the identity.
2. Check whether any policy grants admin-level access.
3. Look for wildcard actions or resources that are broader than needed.
4. Use access analysis or recommendation tools to find unused permissions.
5. Confirm whether each permission supports a real task.

**How to correct the policy**

Once you identify excess access, reduce the scope until the policy matches the actual job requirement. Prefer service-specific roles and condition-based policies when possible.

```json
{
  "Version": "2012-10-17",
  "Statement": [
    {
      "Effect": "Allow",
      "Action": [
        "kms:Decrypt"
      ],
      "Resource": "arn:aws:kms:us-east-1:123456789012:key/example-key-id"
    }
  ]
}
```

- Remove policies that grant admin access when they are not required.
- Replace wildcard permissions with specific actions and resources.
- Use condition-based controls to narrow when access applies.
- Recheck the policy after changes to confirm it still supports the task.

**Operational best practices**

Regular review is part of keeping permissions aligned with real use. Automated policy analysis and periodic access reviews help you catch drift before it becomes a security issue.

- Perform recurring access reviews.
- Use automated policy analysis where available.
- Prefer least privilege for new policies from the start.
- Validate that group policies are not broader than intended.

**Exam focus**

For Security+ applied defense, connect the symptom to the fix:

- Symptom: access is broader than the role or group needs.
- Risk: unauthorized access, data exposure, or privilege escalation.
- Fix: apply least privilege, remove admin rights, and narrow policy scope.
- Tooling: IAM review methods and IAM Access Analyzer recommendations.
