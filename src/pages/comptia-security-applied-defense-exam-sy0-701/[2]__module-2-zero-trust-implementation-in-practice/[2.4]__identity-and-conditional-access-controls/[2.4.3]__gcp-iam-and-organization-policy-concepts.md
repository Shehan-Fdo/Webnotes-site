## **Course:** CompTIA Security+ Applied Defense (Exam SY0-701)

**IAM fundamentals**

Identity and Access Management (IAM) controls who can access Google Cloud resources and what they can do. In practice, IAM is a framework of identities, policies, and permissions that helps you grant access at the right time for the right reason.

Least privilege is the core security principle here. You give each user, service account, or application only the permissions needed to do the job. This reduces the risk of misuse, accidental damage, and over-permissioned accounts.

- Prefer the most limited **predefined roles** or custom roles that meet the need.
- Avoid **basic roles** in production unless there is no alternative.
- Use role recommendations when you need help replacing a broad role.
- Use the Policy Simulator before changing access so you can check impact.

> Basic roles can include thousands of permissions across Google Cloud services, so they are usually too broad for production use.

**Allow policies**

Google Cloud grants access through **allow policies** attached to resources. A resource can have only one allow policy, and that policy can apply to the resource itself and to descendants that inherit it.

An allow policy is a collection of role bindings and metadata. A role binding connects principals to a single IAM role, and it can include conditions that change when or how the role is granted. Metadata includes items such as an `etag` and `version`.

- An allow policy is attached to a resource.
- One resource has only one allow policy.
- Role bindings map principals to roles.
- Conditions can make access context-specific.
- Policies can be viewed in JSON, and the Google Cloud CLI can retrieve them in YAML.

**Roles and permissions**

Google Cloud provides an index for finding roles and permissions by service, role name, or permission name. This is useful when you need to identify the narrowest role that fits a task.

The practical workflow is to search for a service, inspect the predefined role details, and verify which permissions are included. You can also search by permission to see which roles contain it.

| Item | What it helps you do |
|---|---|
| Service | Find all roles and permissions for that service |
| Role | Review the role description and included permissions |
| Permission | See which roles include that permission |

**Least privilege in practice**

Least privilege is not just a design goal; it is an operational habit. Cloud environments grow quickly, and permissions often accumulate faster than they are used. That makes periodic review important.

Use IAM Recommender to identify permissions that are likely unnecessary, especially when you want to remove high-risk access safely. This supports right-sizing permissions without guessing.

- Review granted permissions regularly.
- Remove access that is not needed.
- Use recommendations to reduce manual analysis.
- Treat broad access as a temporary exception, not a default.

**Organization policy and access control mindset**

Organization policy supports a more controlled cloud posture by helping you enforce guardrails across resources. In Security+ terms, this aligns with zero trust thinking: do not assume access is safe just because a user or workload is already inside the environment.

Zero trust means you verify access each time a resource is requested. In Google Cloud, that mindset pairs well with narrow IAM roles, policy checks, and careful inheritance of access.

- Verify access instead of assuming trust.
- Limit permissions at the smallest practical scope.
- Use policy controls to reduce accidental exposure.
- Reassess access as workloads and teams change.

**Exam focus**

For exam prep, remember these high-value points:

- **Least privilege** means minimum necessary access.
- **Basic roles** are broad and usually avoided in production.
- **Predefined roles** and custom roles are preferred when they fit.
- **Allow policies** attach to resources and control inherited access.
- **Role bindings** connect principals to roles, sometimes with conditions.
- **IAM Recommender** and **Policy Simulator** help you refine access safely.
