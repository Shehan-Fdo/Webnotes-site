## **Course:** CompTIA Security+ Applied Defense (Exam SY0-701)

**Documentation and access review purpose**

Documentation and access review deliverables help you prove that access is reviewed, justified, and corrected over time. In a Zero Trust model, access is not assumed to be safe just because a user or device is already inside the network. You verify access repeatedly and record the outcome.

These deliverables support access governance by showing who has access, why they have it, and what action you took if the access was not appropriate. They also reduce manual effort by making reviews repeatable and easier to audit.

- **Access review** deliverables document permissions across systems, applications, and data repositories.
- **Zero Trust** reinforces the idea that access must be checked each time, not granted once and forgotten.
- **Templates** help standardize review steps, reduce human error, and speed up compliance cycles.
- **Remediation** records show what changed after a review, such as removal or adjustment of access.

**What to include in review documentation**

A useful access review package captures the current state of access and the decision made for each item. The goal is to make the review easy to understand later, whether for internal governance or an audit.

Common elements include:

- User identity and role
- System, application, or data set being reviewed
- Access level or privilege assigned
- Review decision
- Follow-up action or remediation status

A structured format matters because it keeps the review consistent across teams and review cycles. It also helps you compare results over time and spot patterns such as repeated overprovisioning.

**Template-driven review workflow**

A template gives you a repeatable way to collect and evaluate access data. Instead of gathering information manually from many sources, you use one format to document the review and the decision.

1. Identify the users, roles, and resources in scope.
2. Compare assigned access against business need and job function.
3. Record whether the access is approved, adjusted, or removed.
4. Document any remediation steps and the person responsible.
5. Retain the completed review for future reference.

This workflow supports both security and compliance because it creates a clear record of what was checked and what changed. It also makes it easier to show that access governance is active rather than assumed.

**Zero Trust connection**

Zero Trust is a security strategy where nothing is trusted by default. Each user, device, process, and request must prove itself before gaining access to a resource. Documentation and access review deliverables fit this model because they help you verify that access remains appropriate over time.

- Access is evaluated continuously, not only at onboarding.
- Authentication and authorization decisions are tied to current need.
- Review records help show that access controls are being enforced.
- Remediation closes the loop when access no longer matches the requirement.

> In a Zero Trust environment, the review record is as important as the decision itself because it proves the control was performed.

**Practical review outputs**

The exact format can vary, but the output should be clear enough for another reviewer to understand without extra context. Good deliverables are concise, repeatable, and traceable.

Useful outputs include:

- A completed user access review template
- A list of approved, changed, and removed permissions
- Notes explaining exceptions or special cases
- Evidence of remediation completion
- A retained record for audit or governance use

When you build or evaluate these deliverables, focus on clarity and consistency. A strong record shows the access state, the decision, and the action taken, which is the core of effective access governance.

**Key takeaways**

- Use documentation to prove access was reviewed, not just assumed.
- Use templates to standardize review data and reduce errors.
- Tie review decisions to business need and current role.
- Record remediation so access changes are traceable.
- Treat access review as an ongoing Zero Trust control.
