## **Course:** CompTIA Cybersecurity Analyst (Exam CS0-003)

**Email security logs in the investigation workflow**

Email security logs help you identify suspicious messages, understand how they moved through the environment, and determine whether a message is part of a broader attack. In Microsoft Defender for Office 365, reports in the Microsoft Defender portal provide visibility into how email security features are protecting the organization, and they can be viewed or downloaded when you have the required permissions.

These logs are useful because phishing rarely arrives as a single isolated message. Investigation tools can analyze the original email and look for related messages that may be part of the same campaign. That broader view helps you scope impact faster and reduce the chance that you miss a connected message.

- Use **reports** to review email security activity and protection outcomes.
- Use **permissions** to control who can view and download report data.
- Use **analysis** to connect one suspicious email to related messages.

**Phishing indicators to look for**

Phishing indicators often show up in message content, sender identity, and attachment or link behavior. Security tools and analysts look for unusual attachments, malicious links, suspicious email addresses, and signs that a message is trying to impersonate a trusted source. These indicators help you separate likely phishing from routine spam or unwanted mail.

A practical investigation also checks whether controls were bypassed and whether anyone interacted with the message. That includes clicks, attachment downloads, and other user actions that can expand the blast radius.

- **Attachments** may be unusual for the sender or context.
- **Links** may lead to malicious or deceptive destinations.
- **Sender addresses** may be spoofed, unfamiliar, or slightly altered.
- **User interaction** can show whether the message caused exposure.

**How email analysis supports investigations**

During automated investigation, Microsoft Defender for Office 365 analyzes the original email for threats and identifies related messages that may be part of the same attack. This matters because attackers often send multiple messages as part of one campaign rather than relying on a single delivery attempt.

Email analysis can help you identify clusters of related messages and understand the attack pattern. That makes it easier to trace the root cause, identify compromised accounts, and see how the attacker tried to reach users.

<details>
<summary>What to verify during a phishing review</summary>
<p>
Check the message source, content, attachments, links, and whether the email appears in a larger cluster of related messages. Then confirm who received it and whether anyone clicked or downloaded content.
</p>
</details>

**Investigation approach**

1. Review the email security report or investigation result in the Microsoft Defender portal.
2. Inspect the original message for suspicious content, links, attachments, and sender details.
3. Look for related messages that may belong to the same campaign.
4. Determine whether users interacted with the message.
5. Use the findings to scope the incident and decide on remediation.

**Why this matters for analysts**

Email security logs give you evidence, while phishing indicators give you clues. Used together, they help you move from a single alert to a fuller incident picture. In practice, that means faster triage, better scoping, and more confident containment decisions.

> If you only review one message in isolation, you can miss the rest of the campaign.
