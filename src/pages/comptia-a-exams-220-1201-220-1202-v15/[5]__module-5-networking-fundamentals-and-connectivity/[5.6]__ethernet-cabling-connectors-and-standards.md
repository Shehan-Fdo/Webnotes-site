## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202) — V15

**Ethernet cabling basics**

Ethernet over twisted pair is a common wired networking method in modern LANs (local area networks). The cable contains one or more pairs of insulated copper wires twisted together to help reduce electromagnetic interference and limit crosstalk with nearby cables.

- **Twisted pair** is the core cable type used for Ethernet networking.
- Twisting helps minimize external interference and improves signal reliability.
- Ethernet twisted-pair cabling is commonly used in office networks and other short-distance network runs.

**Connectors and cable types**

The standard Ethernet connector for twisted-pair cabling is **RJ-45**. It is an 8-pin connector and is larger than the telephone-style RJ-11 connector. In practice, you match the connector and cable to the device and task.

- **RJ-45** is used with Ethernet network cables.
- **Cat 5e**, **Cat 6**, and **Cat 6a** are common twisted-pair cable categories.
- **UTP** means unshielded twisted pair.
- **STP** means shielded twisted pair.

> Use the correct cable type and connector for the job; mismatches can prevent a reliable connection.

**Cable categories and standards**

Twisted-pair cable categories indicate different performance levels and supported speeds. Higher categories generally support better performance and higher throughput, but the exact speed depends on the full network design and equipment in use.

- **Cat 5e** is a common Ethernet category for basic network connectivity.
- **Cat 6** and **Cat 6a** are also common in modern installations.
- Category selection matters when you need to meet speed and transmission requirements.

**Practical identification**

When you are troubleshooting or installing equipment, identify the cable by its connector shape, labeling, and intended use. Ethernet cabling is only one part of the broader cable landscape, so you should distinguish it from peripheral and storage cables.

- Ethernet network cables typically terminate in **RJ-45**.
- Telephone connectors are smaller than RJ-45 and are not the same standard.
- Cable category markings help you confirm the intended Ethernet use.

**Quick comparison**

| Item | What it is | Common use |
|---|---|---|
| RJ-45 | 8-pin Ethernet connector | Twisted-pair network cabling |
| UTP | Unshielded twisted pair | General Ethernet runs |
| STP | Shielded twisted pair | Environments needing more interference protection |
| Cat 5e | Twisted-pair category | Common Ethernet connectivity |
| Cat 6 / Cat 6a | Higher-category twisted-pair cabling | Higher-performance Ethernet installations |

**Exam focus**

For A+ Core 1, you should be able to recognize Ethernet cabling by connector and category, and explain why twisted-pair construction is used. The key idea is simple: choose the right cable type, connector, and category for the network task.

- Identify **RJ-45** as the Ethernet connector.
- Distinguish **UTP** from **STP**.
- Recognize **Cat 5e**, **Cat 6**, and **Cat 6a** as twisted-pair Ethernet categories.
- Connect twisted-pair design to reduced interference and reliable communication.
