## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202) — V15

**Network scope and geography**

Network types are usually categorized by the size of the area they cover and where the devices are located. For A+ purposes, the key idea is simple: the farther apart the devices are, the more likely you are dealing with a wider network type and lower throughput than a local connection.

- **LAN** stands for *Local Area Network* and usually covers a building or a group of nearby buildings.
- **WAN** stands for *Wide Area Network* and spans a large geographic area, such as different cities, states, countries, or regions around the world.
- **WLAN** stands for *Wireless Local Area Network* and is a local network that uses wireless connectivity such as `802.11`.
- **PAN** stands for *Personal Area Network* and connects personal devices over a short range.
- **MAN** stands for *Metropolitan Area Network* and covers a city-sized area.

A network can use wired or wireless communication, but the defining factor is still its scope. When you see a question about distance, location, or coverage area, identify the network type before focusing on the technology used.

**LAN and WLAN**

A LAN is the local network you use inside a building or across nearby buildings. It commonly uses Ethernet or local wireless access, and it usually provides the fastest speeds available in a typical office or home environment.

A WLAN is still local in scope, but it uses wireless networking instead of a cable connection. In exam questions, `802.11` is a strong clue that the connection is wireless and local.

- **Ethernet** is a common wired LAN technology.
- **802.11** is a common wireless LAN technology.
- Local connections usually provide higher bandwidth than wider-area connections.
- Slower-than-local speeds often indicate that the connection is not a LAN.

**WAN and MAN**

A WAN connects locations that are not nearby. It can link offices across a city, across the country, or across the world. Because the signal must travel farther, WAN speeds are generally slower than LAN speeds.

A MAN sits between a LAN and a WAN in geographic scope. It covers a metropolitan area, so it is larger than a local building network but smaller than a wide-area network.

- **WAN** is used when the connection crosses long distances.
- **MAN** is used for city-scale coverage.
- Distance and infrastructure complexity usually reduce speed compared with a LAN.
- A WAN may connect to a remote site that is not local to you, even if that site is only down the street.

**PAN**

A PAN is a short-range network for personal devices. It is typically used for communication between devices that are very close together, such as a phone, tablet, or computer.

- **Bluetooth** is a common PAN technology.
- PANs are designed for close proximity and small device groups.
- A PAN is much smaller in scope than a LAN.
- PANs are often used for simple data sharing between personal devices.

**Quick identification guide**

| Network type | Full name | Typical scope | Common clue |
|---|---|---|---|
| LAN | Local Area Network | Building or nearby buildings | Ethernet, local `802.11` |
| WLAN | Wireless Local Area Network | Building or nearby buildings | Wireless `802.11` |
| PAN | Personal Area Network | Very short range | Bluetooth, personal devices |
| MAN | Metropolitan Area Network | City-sized area | Metropolitan coverage |
| WAN | Wide Area Network | Large geographic area | Remote sites, long distance |

**Exam approach**

When you answer network-type questions, start with the physical distance between devices. Then match the scope to the acronym. If the question mentions local devices and high bandwidth, think LAN or WLAN. If it mentions a city, country, or global connection, think MAN or WAN. If it mentions personal devices in close proximity, think PAN.

> If the connection is slower and spans a long distance, it is usually not a LAN.

**Memory cues**

- **LAN**: local, fast, nearby.
- **WLAN**: local and wireless.
- **PAN**: personal and very short range.
- **MAN**: metropolitan, city-sized.
- **WAN**: wide area, long distance.
