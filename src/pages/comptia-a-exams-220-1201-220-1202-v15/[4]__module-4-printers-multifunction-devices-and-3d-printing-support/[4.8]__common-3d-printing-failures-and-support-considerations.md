## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202) — V15

**Common 3D printing failures**

3D printing problems often show up early in the print and usually point to setup, maintenance, or environmental issues. The most common failures in the evidence include poor adhesion to the print bed, clogged nozzles, warping, stringing, under-extrusion, and over-extrusion.

The first layer matters most because it establishes the base of the object. If the first layer is not correct, the print may fail to start or may detach during the job. Careful troubleshooting helps you identify the issue before the print wastes more time and material.

- **Poor bed adhesion** means the print does not stick well to the build surface.
- **Warping** happens when the print lifts or bends, often from temperature or surface issues.
- **Stringing** leaves thin strands between printed areas.
- **Under-extrusion** means too little material is being deposited.
- **Over-extrusion** means too much material is being deposited.
- **Clogged nozzles** restrict filament flow and can stop extrusion.

**First-layer and adhesion checks**

Bed leveling is a core support task because uneven spacing between the nozzle and bed affects adhesion and print quality. A level bed helps the first layer bond evenly across the surface.

You can reduce adhesion problems by keeping the printer maintained and by checking the print environment. The evidence specifically points to heated beds, clean surfaces, and environmental conditions as practical ways to keep prints flat and secure.

1. Check that the bed is level before starting the print.
2. Confirm that the build surface is clean.
3. Use a heated bed when the material and printer support it.
4. Review the surrounding environment for conditions that may contribute to warping or poor adhesion.

> The first layer is the most important layer for print success. If it fails, the entire print is at risk.

**Extrusion-related failures**

Extrusion problems usually come from maintenance or calibration issues. The evidence highlights nozzle cleaning, extruder calibration, and temperature adjustment as the main corrective actions.

Under-extrusion can happen when the printer does not push enough filament through the nozzle. Over-extrusion happens when too much filament is pushed out, which can distort the print and reduce quality.

- Clean the nozzle regularly to prevent flow restriction.
- Calibrate the extruder so the printer feeds the expected amount of filament.
- Adjust temperature settings to support consistent extrusion.
- Watch for signs of inconsistent output during the first layers.

**Support considerations for complex prints**

Support structures help hold overhanging or unsupported parts during printing. They are especially useful when the model has geometry that cannot be printed cleanly in midair.

Support planning is part of troubleshooting because poor support placement can create surface defects or make removal difficult. You should consider where the model needs temporary backing before the print begins.

- Add supports where the model has overhangs or bridging areas that need help.
- Place supports carefully so they stabilize the print without damaging visible surfaces.
- Remove supports after printing and inspect the supported areas for cleanup needs.

**Troubleshooting approach**

A practical troubleshooting guide helps you identify the issue early and match it to the likely cause. Real-world print defects are easier to solve when you compare the failed print to known failure patterns and then adjust the relevant setting or maintenance step.

Use a simple, repeatable process:

1. Identify the visible defect on the print.
2. Match the defect to a likely category such as adhesion, extrusion, or warping.
3. Check the printer setup and maintenance items tied to that category.
4. Make one change at a time so you can verify the result.

**Key takeaways**

- First-layer quality is the foundation of a successful print.
- Bed leveling, clean surfaces, and stable environmental conditions improve adhesion.
- Nozzle cleaning and extruder calibration help prevent extrusion issues.
- Support structures are useful for overhangs and other unsupported features.
- Early troubleshooting reduces wasted material and improves print quality.
