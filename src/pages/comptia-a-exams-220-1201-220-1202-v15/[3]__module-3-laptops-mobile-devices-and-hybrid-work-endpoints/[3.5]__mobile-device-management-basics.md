## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202) — V15

**Mobile device management overview**

<abbr title="Mobile Device Management">MDM</abbr> is the process used to bring a device under centralized management so you can apply security and configuration controls. In Microsoft Intune, device enrollment is the step that enables management, and Intune works with Microsoft Entra ID to register and enroll devices for access to internal resources.

- **Enrollment** is the point where management begins.
- **Intune** uses an MDM certificate on the device to communicate with the service.
- **Policies** can then be enforced on the enrolled device.

A practical way to think about MDM is that registration identifies the device in the identity system, while enrollment gives the management platform control over settings and compliance.

**Enrollment and management flow**

During enrollment, Intune installs an MDM certificate on the device. That certificate lets the device communicate with the Intune service and receive management instructions. Once enrolled, the device can be governed by organizational rules such as enrollment limits and compliance requirements.

- Devices and users are first registered in Microsoft Entra ID, also called a tenant.
- Intune then uses that identity foundation to manage the endpoint.
- The enrolled device can receive configuration profiles, apps, and compliance rules.

> Enrollment is the step that turns a device from merely recognized into actively managed.

**What MDM can enforce**

MDM supports policy-based control over enrolled devices. The evidence specifically calls out enrollment policies and compliance policies as examples of what Intune can enforce after enrollment.

- **Enrollment policies** can limit the number or type of devices a user can enroll.
- **Compliance policies** help define whether a device meets organizational requirements.
- **Configuration profiles** and apps can be delivered after enrollment.

These controls help organizations keep devices aligned with security and access expectations without managing each device manually.

**Common enrollment scenarios**

Enrollment applies to both company-owned and personal devices used for work. The exact method can vary, but the core goal stays the same: bring the device under management so it can receive policy and configuration.

- Corporate-owned devices usually receive fuller management control.
- Bring Your Own Device (<abbr title="Bring Your Own Device">BYOD</abbr>) scenarios often require more careful policy boundaries.
- Different enrollment methods may exist for Windows, Android, iOS, and macOS.

**Why MDM matters for support work**

For entry-level support roles, MDM is important because it connects device setup, security, and policy enforcement. It helps you understand how a device becomes manageable and why a device may be blocked, restricted, or required to meet compliance before it can access resources.

- You troubleshoot enrollment when a device cannot join management.
- You verify compliance when access is denied.
- You confirm that the device has the expected management certificate and policy state.

**Key terms**

<dl>
<dt><strong>Microsoft Entra ID</strong></dt>

<dt><strong>MDM certificate</strong></dt>
<dd>The certificate Intune installs during enrollment to enable communication and management.</dd>

**Quick review**

- MDM manages devices through centralized policies.
- Enrollment is the process that enables management.
- Intune uses Microsoft Entra ID and an MDM certificate to manage enrolled devices.
- Policies can limit enrollment and enforce compliance.
