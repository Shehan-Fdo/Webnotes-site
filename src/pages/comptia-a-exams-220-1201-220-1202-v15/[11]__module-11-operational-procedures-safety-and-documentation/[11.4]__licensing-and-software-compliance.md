## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202) — V15

**Software licensing compliance overview**

Software license compliance means using software only in ways the license allows. In practice, you compare how an organization uses software with the licenses it has purchased and the terms attached to those licenses. This includes tracking installations, monitoring usage, and keeping records that prove compliance.

Compliance matters because vendors can audit usage, sometimes without much warning. Audits often require a detailed inventory, usage data, and evidence that the organization is authorized for what it has installed and deployed.

- **License terms** define where, how, and by whom software can be used.
- **Compliance records** help you show that installations match purchased rights.
- **Audit readiness** depends on current inventory and usage evidence.

**Common licensing models**

Software vendors use different licensing models, and each model changes how you count and control usage. You need to understand the model before you can verify compliance.

| Model | What it generally tracks | Compliance concern |
|---|---|---|
| Per-user | Named or assigned users | Too many users assigned or active |
| Per-device | Individual devices | More devices than licensed |
| Subscription-based | Time-limited access | Expired renewals or lapsed coverage |
| Consumption-based | Amount of use or resources consumed | Usage beyond purchased limits |
| Concurrent | Number of simultaneous users | More active sessions than allowed |

These models can include specific terms about where software may be used, how long the license remains valid, and what penalties apply if the agreement is breached.

**What compliance work involves**

Compliance is not just a one-time check. It is an ongoing process of matching actual use to purchased rights and keeping the supporting data organized.

- Maintain a centralized software inventory.
- Track installations and usage over time.
- Keep contract and renewal information together.
- Review compliance regularly to reduce audit and legal risk.
- Remove outdated or unused applications to reduce waste.

A reliable inventory helps you avoid duplicate purchases and makes it easier to answer vendor questions during an audit. Centralizing contracts and renewal dates also helps you plan ahead and avoid missed renewals.

**Audit and risk considerations**

Vendors such as Microsoft, Oracle, and Adobe may conduct license audits. These audits can be stressful because you may need to gather proof of compliance quickly. If records are incomplete, the organization may face financial penalties, legal exposure, or unexpected remediation costs.

> If you cannot prove compliance, assume the vendor will treat the gap as a risk until you can document otherwise.

For support roles, the practical goal is to keep software usage visible and defensible. That means you should know what is installed, who uses it, what license covers it, and when that license expires.

**Practical compliance workflow**

1. Build a centralized inventory of software installations.
2. Map each product to its license type and purchase record.
3. Check usage against the allowed scope of the license.
4. Review renewals, expirations, and contract terms.
5. Remove unused software and correct mismatches.
6. Keep evidence ready for internal review or vendor audit.

**Why this matters in A+ support work**

CompTIA A+ emphasizes operational procedures, documentation, and software troubleshooting. Licensing and compliance fit that mindset because they require careful recordkeeping, controlled changes, and attention to vendor terms. Good compliance habits reduce waste, lower risk, and make support work easier when software issues or audits arise.

**Key terms**

- **Software license**: Permission to use software under defined terms.
- **License compliance**: Using software within the rights granted by the license.
- **Audit**: A vendor review of software use and entitlement.
- **Inventory**: A current list of installed software and related details.
- **Renewal**: The process of extending a subscription or license term.
