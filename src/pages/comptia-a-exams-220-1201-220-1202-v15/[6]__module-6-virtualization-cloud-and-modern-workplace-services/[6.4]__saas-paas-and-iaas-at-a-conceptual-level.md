## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202) — V15

**Cloud service models at a glance**

Cloud computing delivers services over the internet, and the three most common service models are Infrastructure as a Service (<abbr title="Infrastructure as a Service">IaaS</abbr>), Platform as a Service (<abbr title="Platform as a Service">PaaS</abbr>), and Software as a Service (<abbr title="Software as a Service">SaaS</abbr>). The main difference is how much control you keep versus how much the provider manages for you.

In traditional IT, you purchase, install, manage, and maintain infrastructure on-premises. In cloud computing, the service provider owns and maintains the underlying assets, and you consume them through an internet connection, often on a subscription or pay-as-you-go basis.

- **IaaS** gives you virtualized infrastructure such as compute, storage, and networking.
- **PaaS** gives you a managed platform for building and deploying applications.
- **SaaS** gives you ready-to-use software that you access directly.

**Responsibility and control**

A useful way to compare the models is by responsibility. As you move from IaaS to PaaS to SaaS, the provider manages more of the stack and you manage less.

| Model | What you get | Your focus | Provider focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| IaaS | Virtualized infrastructure | Operating systems, apps, and configuration | Hardware, virtualization, core infrastructure |
| PaaS | Managed application platform | Your code and app logic | Runtime, platform, and underlying infrastructure |
| SaaS | Complete application | Using the software and managing your data | Application, platform, and infrastructure |

This progression helps you match the service to the task. If you need more flexibility and control, IaaS fits better. If you want faster development and deployment, PaaS is a better fit. If you want the simplest user experience, SaaS is the most direct option.

**Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS)**

IaaS provides on-demand access to cloud-hosted compute, storage, and networking. It is the most flexible of the three models because you control more of the environment.

- You use IaaS when you want to build and manage your own systems in the cloud.
- You avoid buying and maintaining physical hardware on-premises.
- You still handle more administration than with PaaS or SaaS.

IaaS is often described as the foundation layer because it supplies the backend resources needed to run workloads.

**Platform as a Service (PaaS)**

PaaS provides a managed environment for application development and deployment. The provider handles more of the operational work, so you can focus on building software.

- You use PaaS when you want to develop and deploy applications without managing the full infrastructure.
- You gain a balance of control and convenience.
- You reduce the amount of maintenance needed for the underlying platform.

PaaS is useful when speed matters and you want to spend less time on setup, patching, and platform management.

**Software as a Service (SaaS)**

SaaS delivers complete applications that are ready to use. You typically log in and start working without installing or maintaining the software yourself.

- You use SaaS when you want immediate access to an application.
- You do not manage the infrastructure or the platform behind it.
- The provider handles the application operation and maintenance.

A common example is email delivered as a cloud application: you access the service through a browser or client, while the provider manages the software and supporting systems.

**How to choose the right model**

Choose the model based on how much control you need and how much management you want to avoid.

1. Choose **IaaS** if you need the most control over your cloud environment.
2. Choose **PaaS** if you want to build applications faster with less platform management.
3. Choose **SaaS** if you want a finished application with minimal setup.

> The models are not mutually exclusive. Many organizations use a mix of IaaS, PaaS, and SaaS to meet different business and technical needs.

**Key exam takeaway**

The phrase “as a service” means the provider delivers and manages part of the IT stack for you. The higher you move from IaaS to SaaS, the less you manage and the more the provider handles.
