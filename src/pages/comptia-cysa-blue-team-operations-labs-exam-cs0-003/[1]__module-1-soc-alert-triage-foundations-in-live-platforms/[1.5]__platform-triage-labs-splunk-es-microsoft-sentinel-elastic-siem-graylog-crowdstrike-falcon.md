## **Course:** CompTIA CySA+ Blue Team Operations Labs (Exam CS0-003)

**Platform triage overview**

Platform triage means you move from alert to evidence quickly, using the tool’s incident or investigation view to confirm what happened and what needs action. In these labs, the common pattern is the same even when the interface changes: review the alert, inspect related entities, follow drill-down paths, and decide whether the event needs escalation, containment, or closure.

- **Splunk ES** centers triage around notable events and analyst workflows.
- **Microsoft Sentinel** organizes evidence into incidents for case management.
- **Elastic SIEM** emphasizes search and analytics across mixed environments.
- **Graylog** is commonly used for log review and filtering during investigation.
- **CrowdStrike Falcon** supports endpoint-focused triage and response.

> Use the platform’s native investigation view first. It usually preserves the alert context better than starting with a fresh search.

**Splunk Enterprise Security triage**

Splunk Enterprise Security (ES) uses notable events and analyst workflows to help you move through investigations. The evidence highlights Incident Review and the analyst queue as the operational entry points for handling notables. ES also supports customizable workflows, which can reduce manual work and speed up decisions.

- Review the notable event in **Incident Review** before branching into searches.
- Use the **analyst queue** to manage work and keep investigations organized.
- Follow drill-down searches from correlation rules when you need narrower scope.
- Use workflow improvements to reduce repeated manual query building.
- Treat the notable as a starting point, not the final answer.

A practical triage flow in ES is to confirm the notable, inspect the related context, and then decide whether the event needs deeper investigation or can be dismissed. Multiple drill-down searches can help you pivot from a single alert into several focused questions without rebuilding the investigation from scratch.

**Microsoft Sentinel incident triage**

Microsoft Sentinel treats incidents as collections of evidence. Each incident aggregates relevant alerts, and those alerts can come from analytics rules or third-party security products. Incidents inherit entities and alert properties such as severity, status, and MITRE ATT&CK tactics and techniques.

- Start with the **incident** as the case container.
- Check the linked alerts to understand why the incident exists.
- Review inherited entities to identify users, hosts, or other involved assets.
- Use severity and status to prioritize your next action.
- Look at tactics and techniques to understand the likely attack pattern.

Sentinel gives you a full case management experience in the Azure portal, so the incident details page becomes your central triage location. That makes it useful when you need to move from detection to investigation without leaving the platform.

**Elastic SIEM triage**

Elastic Security is described as an AI-driven security analytics solution built to be open and transparent. The evidence also emphasizes its role in multi-cloud and hybrid monitoring, especially where organizations combine on-premises, Azure, and other environments.

- Use Elastic when you need broad visibility across mixed infrastructure.
- Treat search and analytics as the core triage method.
- Expect the platform to support investigations across multiple environments.
- Use it to complement other SIEM tools when the estate is distributed.

Elastic is a good fit when triage depends on correlating activity across different sources rather than staying inside a single product ecosystem. In practice, that means you often use it to widen the investigation after an initial alert points to a larger pattern.

**Graylog triage**

Graylog is part of the platform triage lab set, so the main skill is efficient log review. The evidence provided does not specify a unique Graylog workflow, so keep your approach conservative and focus on the general investigation pattern: filter, inspect, and correlate.

- Narrow the event set with targeted filters.
- Review timestamps, source systems, and message content.
- Correlate related log entries before deciding on escalation.
- Use Graylog as a log investigation layer when you need quick visibility into raw events.

When the platform-specific workflow is not explicit, rely on repeatable triage habits: confirm the source, validate the timeline, and separate signal from noise.

**CrowdStrike Falcon triage**

CrowdStrike Falcon is included in the lab set as an endpoint security platform. The evidence here does not provide detailed Falcon-specific steps, so the safest study approach is to focus on endpoint triage concepts: identify the affected host, review the alert context, and determine whether response action is needed.

- Start with the endpoint or host tied to the alert.
- Review the event context before taking response action.
- Look for signs that the activity is isolated or part of a broader pattern.
- Escalate when the endpoint evidence suggests active compromise.

For exam prep, think of Falcon as the endpoint side of the triage workflow, where the goal is to move from detection to host-level confirmation and response.

**Cross-platform triage habits**

Even though the tools differ, the investigation logic stays consistent. You want to preserve context, reduce noise, and make a decision based on evidence rather than on the alert title alone.

1. Confirm the alert or incident source.
2. Review related entities, timestamps, and severity.
3. Follow drill-down paths or linked evidence.
4. Decide whether to close, escalate, or contain.
5. Document the outcome so the next analyst can continue cleanly.

- Preserve the original context whenever possible.
- Use the platform’s case or incident view before creating new searches.
- Keep your notes tied to observable evidence.
- Escalate when the platform shows linked indicators across multiple assets.

**What to remember for labs**

The lab objective is not memorizing every menu path. It is recognizing how each platform structures triage work and how to move from alert to decision efficiently.

- **Splunk ES**: notable events, analyst queue, drill-down searches.
- **Microsoft Sentinel**: incidents, alerts, entities, case management.
- **Elastic SIEM**: search-driven, multi-cloud and hybrid visibility.
- **Graylog**: log filtering and correlation.
- **CrowdStrike Falcon**: endpoint-focused investigation and response.
