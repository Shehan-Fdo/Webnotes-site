## **Course:** Comptia A+ 220-1201

**Networking Overview**

Networking hardware helps you connect devices and move traffic across home and business networks.

- **Routers** connect networks and manage traffic between them.
- **Switches** expand wired connections on a local network.
- **Access points** provide wireless coverage, especially in larger or multi-floor environments.
- **Modems** and optical network terminals (`ONTs`) support internet access, including fiber connections.

**Core Network Devices**

You use different devices for different network roles.

- A **firewall** filters traffic to help protect the network.
- A **patch panel** helps organize cables in larger installations.
- A **network interface card (NIC)** gives a device network connectivity.
- Each NIC uses a unique **media access control (MAC)** address for identification on the network.

**Wired and Wireless Connectivity**

You choose wired or wireless components based on coverage and layout needs.

- Wired connections usually rely on switches and cabling.
- Wireless coverage usually relies on access points.
- Larger environments often combine both for better reach and manageability.

**Practical Device Roles**

Use the device role to guide troubleshooting and design decisions.

- If you need to connect separate networks, use a router.
- If you need more wired ports, use a switch.
- If you need broader wireless coverage, add an access point.
- If you need traffic filtering, place a firewall in the path.

**Network Planning Notes**

Keep the network layout simple and organized.

- Match the device to the job.
- Use patch panels to keep cabling cleaner in larger setups.
- Verify that each connected device has a working NIC.
- Check MAC address-based identification when you confirm device presence on the network.
