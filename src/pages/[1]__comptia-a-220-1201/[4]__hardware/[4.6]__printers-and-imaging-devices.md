## **Course:** Comptia A+ 220-1201

**Printer and Imaging Device Overview**

Printers and imaging devices combine output, scanning, and sharing features in one platform.

- **Multifunction devices (MFDs)** can act as a printer, scanner, copier, and more.
- MFDs may connect through wired network, wireless network, phone line, or mobile printing paths.
- Office models can be larger and harder to service than home units.

**Printer Types**

Different printer technologies use different output methods and consumables.

- **Laser printers** use a toner cartridge and a photosensitive drum.
- Laser printing relies on toner transfer rather than liquid ink.
- **Thermal printers** use heat to create output on thermal paper.
- Thermal printers are quiet and often print receipts or shipping labels.

**Multifunction Device Setup**

You configure MFDs by matching device features to the environment.

- Set up the device for the required connection type.
- Configure the printer language when the device supports it.
- Set individual print features as needed.
- Enable badging or secure printing when the workflow requires it.

**Scanner Features**

MFDs often include scanning hardware and document handling features.

- A flatbed scanner supports direct document scanning.
- An automatic document feeder (ADF) supports multi-page scanning.
- Network scanning services help route scans to the right destination.

**Printer Drivers and Software**

You need the correct software to make the printer work with the host system.

- Install the proper printer driver for the device.
- Use vendor software when the printer requires extra features.
- Verify wireless printing setup when the printer uses a wireless connection.

**Printer Connectivity and Sharing**

Printer access depends on how the device connects and how users share it.

- A printer may be publicly shared in an organization.
- Device settings control how users reach the printer.
- Wireless printing can support mobile and remote workflows.

**Troubleshooting Printers**

Printer troubleshooting starts with the device, the connection, and the job path.

1. Check power, paper, and consumables.
2. Verify the connection type and network access.
3. Confirm the correct driver and printer settings.
4. Test scanning, printing, or copying based on the reported issue.
5. Replace or service the failing component if the problem remains.

**Common Service Areas**

Some printer parts and features often need attention during maintenance.

- Feed assemblies move paper through the device.
- Heating elements support thermal output.
- Toner, drums, and paper handling parts affect print quality and reliability.
