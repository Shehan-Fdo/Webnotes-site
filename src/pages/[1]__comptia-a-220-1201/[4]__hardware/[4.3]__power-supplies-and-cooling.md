## **Course:** Comptia A+ 220-1201

**Power and Heat Basics**

Computers need stable power and constant heat removal to operate safely.

- **Power supply unit (PSU)** converts AC power to DC power for internal components.
- Hardware load affects power demand.
- Heat is a normal by-product of computing.

**Power Supply Considerations**

Choose a power supply that matches the system’s needs.

- Check the required output for installed devices.
- Use a PSU with enough capacity for current and future hardware.
- Consider **redundant power supplies** in systems that need higher availability.
- Use **modular** power supplies when you want fewer unused cables inside the case.
- Review efficiency ratings when comparing units.

**Power Safety**

You must disconnect power before working inside a computer.

- Unplug the system from the power source first.
- Capacitors can store electrical charge after shutdown.
- Discharge capacitors only with proper knowledge and procedure.
- Do not connect yourself to a building electrical ground.

> Working inside a powered system can cause electric shock and equipment damage.

**Cooling Methods**

Cooling systems move heat away from components and out of the case.

- Fans pull cool air through the system.
- Hot air exits the case after it absorbs heat from components.
- **Passive cooling** uses no moving fan parts.
- **Heat sinks** increase surface area for heat transfer.
- **Thermal paste** improves contact between a chip and a heat sink.
- **Thermal pads** provide a similar heat-transfer interface in some designs.
- **Liquid cooling** moves heat through fluid-based components.

**Airflow and Case Layout**

Good airflow depends on how parts are arranged inside the case.

- Keep wires out of the airflow path.
- Place components so air can move from one side of the case to the other.
- Case design affects cooling performance.
- Adapter card placement can also affect airflow.

**Practical Cooling Checks**

Use simple checks when a system runs hot.

1. Verify that fans spin normally.
2. Check for blocked vents or cable clutter.
3. Confirm that heat sinks are seated correctly.
4. Inspect thermal interface material if a cooler was removed.
5. Review whether the case layout supports airflow.
