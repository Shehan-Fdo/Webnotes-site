## **Course:** Comptia A+ 220-1201

**Display Types**

Display technologies vary by panel design and use case.

- **LCD** (Liquid Crystal Display) uses liquid crystals to control light passage.
- **TN** (Twisted Nematic) panels favor fast response times.
- **IPS** (In-Plane Switching) panels provide better viewing angles.
- Choose the display type based on how you use the screen.

**Display Attributes**

Display specifications help you match a monitor to the task.

- **Pixel density** measures how many pixels fit in 1 inch of display area.
- **Refresh rate** affects how often the image updates.
- **Screen resolution** describes the number of pixels on the screen.
- **Color gamut** describes the range of colors a display can show.
- Match the specification to the intended use, such as gaming, video, or presentations.

**Video Connectors and Cables**

Monitors and displays connect through several common video interfaces.

- **HDMI** (High Definition Multimedia Interface) carries audio and video over one digital connection.
- **DisplayPort** is a common digital display interface.
- **DVI** (Digital Visual Interface) supports single-link and dual-link connections.
- **VGA** is an older analog display connector.
- **USB-C** can carry video on supported devices.
- Connector choice depends on the display and the computer ports available.

**DVI Connection Types**

DVI includes multiple signal and connector variants.

- **DVI-A** carries analog signals.
- **DVI-D** carries digital signals.
- **DVI-I** supports integrated analog and digital signaling.
- **Single-link** DVI carries less data than dual-link DVI.
- **Dual-link** DVI supports higher bandwidth for higher resolutions or frame rates.

**Practical Display Selection**

You use display specifications and connectors together when choosing hardware.

1. Identify the display task.
2. Check the needed panel type and attributes.
3. Verify the available video ports on the computer and monitor.
4. Select a compatible cable or adapter.
5. Confirm the display works at the expected resolution and refresh rate.
