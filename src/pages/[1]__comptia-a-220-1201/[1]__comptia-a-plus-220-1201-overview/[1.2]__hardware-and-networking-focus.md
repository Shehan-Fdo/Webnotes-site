## **Course:** Comptia A+ 220-1201

**Core 1 Scope**

CompTIA A+ Core 1 is the first of two exams required for the A+ certification.

- It focuses on **hardware**, networking, mobile devices, and troubleshooting.
- It covers foundational skills for entry-level IT support roles.
- It prepares you for the second A+ exam, Core 2.

**Hardware Skills**

Core 1 expects you to install, configure, and maintain hardware components and devices.

- You work with PCs and mobile devices.
- You support common hardware replacements and upgrades.
- You use troubleshooting tools to diagnose technical problems.

**Networking Basics**

Core 1 includes networking basics and SOHO network setup.

- **SOHO** means small office/home office.
- You configure wired and wireless networks.
- You troubleshoot connectivity issues at the device and network level.

**Troubleshooting Focus**

Troubleshooting is a major part of the exam objectives.

- You identify hardware and network symptoms.
- You use practical tools to isolate faults.
- You resolve common connectivity and device issues.

**Career Relevance**

A+ is a widely recognized entry-level IT certification.

- It supports roles such as help desk technician and IT support specialist.
- It validates a foundation in hardware and networking.
- It can support later study in Network+ and Security+.

**Exam Notes**

The current Core 1 version is `220-1201`.

- CompTIA identifies it as exam version `V15`.
- Core 1 and Core 2 must be taken from the same version.
- The exam series code is `220-1201`.
