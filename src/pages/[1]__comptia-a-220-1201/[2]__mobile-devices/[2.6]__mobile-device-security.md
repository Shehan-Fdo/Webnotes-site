## **Course:** Comptia A+ 220-1201

**Mobile Device Security Overview**

Mobile devices store sensitive personal and business data, so you need controls that protect data if the device is lost or stolen.

- **Screen locks** limit unauthorized access.
- Common unlock methods include:
  - Fingerprint
  - Facial recognition
  - PIN
  - Passcode
  - Swipe pattern
- Devices may limit repeated failed unlock attempts.

**Full Device Encryption**

Encryption protects data stored on the device if it leaves your control.

- **Full device encryption** is a key mobile security control.
- On Apple iOS and iPadOS, the passcode provides the decryption key.
- On Android, a login pattern, PIN, or passcode can support encryption.
- Encryption is often enabled by default on many devices.

**Remote Wipe and Device Recovery**

Remote wipe helps you remove data from a device that is lost or no longer trusted.

- Use remote wipe when you cannot recover the device safely.
- Pair remote wipe with other controls to reduce exposure.
- Treat lost devices as a data security incident.

**Mobile Device Management**

**Mobile Device Management (MDM)** lets you centrally control mobile devices.

- MDM supports company-owned devices and personally owned devices.
- Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) means users bring personal devices to work.
- Corporate Owned, Personally Enabled (COPE) means the organization owns the device and allows limited personal use.
- MDM lets you set policy for allowed applications.
- MDM can enable or disable features such as camera or Global Positioning System (GPS).
- MDM helps you manage many devices from one place.

**Practical Security Focus**

You should combine device controls with policy enforcement.

- Require a screen lock on all mobile devices.
- Enable encryption where available.
- Use MDM to apply consistent rules.
- Restrict features that do not support business needs.
- Plan for lost or stolen devices before an incident occurs.
