## **Course:** Comptia A+ 220-1201

**Troubleshooting Methodology**

CompTIA uses a structured troubleshooting methodology to guide problem solving.

- **Identify the problem**.
- Establish a theory of probable cause.
- Test the theory to determine the cause.
- Establish a plan of action to resolve the problem and identify potential effects.
- Implement the solution or **escalate** as necessary.
- Verify full system functionality and, if applicable, implement preventive measures.

**Documentation**

Documentation helps you track issues, actions, and outcomes.

- Record the symptoms you observe.
- Note the steps you test.
- Save the final resolution.
- Use documentation to support future troubleshooting.

**Ticketing Systems**

Ticketing systems help you organize support work and keep a record of issues.

- Document all reported issues in the system.
- Assign tickets to the correct team or individual.
- Use ticket data to help resolve problems.
- Generate reports from stored ticket information.
- The help desk usually manages and maintains tickets.

**Escalation**

Escalate when you cannot resolve the issue within your role or authority.

- Escalate after you test the likely cause and confirm the issue needs additional support.
- Escalate when the fix may have broader effects.
- Escalate to the appropriate team, such as network or server support.
- Include clear notes so the next person can continue quickly.

**Practical Workflow**

Use a consistent process so you do not lose information.

1. Gather the problem details.
2. Log the issue in the ticketing system.
3. Follow the troubleshooting steps.
4. Document what you test and what changes.
5. Escalate if the issue remains unresolved.
6. Verify the result after the fix.
