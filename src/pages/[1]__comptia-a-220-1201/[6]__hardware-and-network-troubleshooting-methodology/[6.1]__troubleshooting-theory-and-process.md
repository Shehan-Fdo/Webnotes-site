## **Course:** Comptia A+ 220-1201

**Troubleshooting Methodology**

Troubleshooting uses a structured process to find the cause of a problem and restore service.

- The CompTIA methodology is a practical framework for IT support.
- It helps you work from symptoms to root cause.
- It supports consistent problem solving across different issues.

**Six-Step Process**

CompTIA describes a six-step troubleshooting methodology.

1. Identify the problem.
2. Establish a theory of probable cause.
3. Test the theory to determine the cause.
4. Establish a plan of action to resolve the problem and identify potential effects.
5. Implement the solution or escalate as necessary.
6. Verify full system functionality and, if applicable, implement preventive measures.

**Identify the Problem**

Start by defining what is wrong.

- Gather symptoms from the user and the system.
- Confirm the scope of the issue.
- Note any recent changes that may relate to the failure.

**Establish a Theory of Probable Cause**

Form a likely explanation for the issue.

- Use symptoms and context to narrow possibilities.
- Consider common failure points first.
- Keep the theory specific enough to test.

**Test the Theory**

Check whether the likely cause is correct.

- Use targeted tests to confirm or reject the theory.
- Move to a new theory if the first one fails.
- Avoid changing multiple variables at once.

**Plan the Fix**

Choose a solution before you make changes.

- Define the steps you will take.
- Consider possible side effects.
- Escalate if the issue is outside your scope or access.

**Implement the Solution**

Apply the chosen fix.

- Follow the plan in order.
- Make only the changes needed to resolve the issue.
- Escalate when you cannot safely complete the repair.

**Verify and Prevent Recurrence**

Confirm that the system works normally after the fix.

- Test the affected function again.
- Check for related issues.
- Apply preventive measures when appropriate.

**Practical Use**

You use the methodology as a repeatable support workflow.

- It reduces guesswork.
- It improves consistency during incident response.
- It supports efficient diagnosis in exam scenarios and real support work.
