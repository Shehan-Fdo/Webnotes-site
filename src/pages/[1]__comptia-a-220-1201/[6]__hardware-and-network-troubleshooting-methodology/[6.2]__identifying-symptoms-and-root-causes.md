## **Course:** Comptia A+ 220-1201

**Troubleshooting Overview**

Troubleshooting uses a structured method to move from a reported issue to a verified fix.

- **Identify the problem** first.
- Build a theory of probable cause.
- Test the theory to confirm the cause.
- Plan the fix and note possible effects.
- Implement the solution or escalate.
- Verify full system functionality.
- Apply preventive measures when appropriate.

**Identifying Symptoms**

Symptoms are the visible signs of a problem.

- Look for patterns that match common issues.
- Use symptoms to narrow the list of likely causes.
- Common symptoms can include hardware failures.
- Common symptoms can also include software errors.
- Examples include unresponsive peripherals.
- Examples also include frequent application crashes.

**From Symptoms to Root Cause**

A symptom is not the same as the root cause.

- The root cause is the underlying reason the problem happens.
- A good root cause explains why the issue recurs.
- Keep digging until you reach a cause you can fix.
- Avoid stopping at the first plausible explanation.
- Confirm the cause with testing before you act.

**Practical Troubleshooting Approach**

Use a consistent process when you investigate an issue.

1. Gather the reported symptoms.
2. Compare them with known problem patterns.
3. Form a likely cause based on the evidence.
4. Test the theory with a safe check.
5. Choose the fix that matches the confirmed cause.
6. Verify the system works after the change.

**Common Support Mindset**

You improve accuracy when you stay methodical.

- Focus on evidence, not assumptions.
- Separate the symptom from the cause.
- Use each test to reduce uncertainty.
- Escalate when the issue is outside your control.
- Recheck the system after repair to confirm stability.
