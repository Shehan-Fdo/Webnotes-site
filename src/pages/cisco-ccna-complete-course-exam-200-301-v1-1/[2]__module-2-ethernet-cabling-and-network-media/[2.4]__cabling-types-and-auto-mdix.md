**Ethernet cabling basics**

Ethernet cabling is a core Layer 1 skill for CCNA. You need to recognize common cable types and understand why specific wiring patterns exist. In practice, the main focus here is twisted-pair copper cabling with RJ-45 connectors, plus the role of Auto-MDIX in simplifying connections.

- **Twisted pair** uses pairs of wires twisted to reduce electromagnetic interference and crosstalk.
- **UTP** (Unshielded Twisted Pair) is the most common Ethernet cabling type in LANs.
- RJ-45 is the common eight-wire connector used for Ethernet copper links.
- Cable categories such as Cat 5e and Cat 6 are part of the broader cabling picture, but the exam focus here is on how devices connect.

**Straight-through and crossover cables**

A straight-through cable keeps the same pinout on both ends. A crossover cable swaps the transmit and receive pairs so devices with the same send/receive behavior can communicate directly.

| Cable type | Wiring pattern | Typical use |
|---|---|---|
| Straight-through | Same pins on both ends | Connect different device roles, such as computer to switch |
| Crossover | Transmit and receive pairs are swapped | Connect similar device roles, such as computer to computer |

- **Straight-through** wiring is identical end to end.
- **Crossover** wiring crosses the relevant pairs between ends.
- Older Ethernet designs relied more heavily on choosing the correct cable type.
- If you use the wrong cable in a non-Auto-MDIX environment, the link may not come up.

> Use conservative exam language: cable choice matters most when the ports do not automatically adapt.

**Auto-MDIX**

Auto-MDIX stands for Auto Medium Dependent Interface Crossover. It lets a port automatically adjust for straight-through or crossover wiring, which reduces the need to remember cable pair rules for every connection.

- **Auto-MDIX** simplifies cable selection.
- It helps modern switches and routers accept either straight-through or crossover cabling on supported ports.
- It reduces setup errors in labs and real networks.
- It does not remove the need to understand the underlying cable types for exam questions.

**How to think about device connections**

When you study cabling, focus on the relationship between the devices and the port behavior. Traditional Ethernet used different transmit and receive pair expectations on different device types, which is why straight-through and crossover cables existed as separate choices.

1. Identify the device roles on each end of the link.
2. Decide whether the environment depends on manual cable selection.
3. Check whether Auto-MDIX is available on the ports.
4. Use the appropriate cable type or let Auto-MDIX handle the crossover function.

**Exam takeaways**

- Know the difference between **straight-through** and **crossover** cabling.
- Remember that twisted-pair Ethernet commonly uses **RJ-45** connectors.
- Understand that **Auto-MDIX** reduces the need to manually match cable type to device role.
- Be ready to identify when cable selection still matters in older or non-adaptive environments.

**Quick memory aid**

- Same wiring on both ends: straight-through.
- Swapped transmit/receive pairs: crossover.
- Automatic adaptation at the port: Auto-MDIX.
