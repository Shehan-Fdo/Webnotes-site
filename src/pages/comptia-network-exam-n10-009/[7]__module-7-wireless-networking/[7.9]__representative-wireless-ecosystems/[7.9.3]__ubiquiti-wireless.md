## **Course:** CompTIA Network+ (Exam N10-009)

**Ubiquiti and UniFi overview**

Ubiquiti’s UniFi platform centers on wireless networking that is managed as part of a broader network environment. For Network+ purposes, focus on how wireless standards, planning, and documentation support reliable connectivity rather than on vendor-specific marketing language.

- **IEEE 802.11** is the wireless standards family you should recognize first.
- The **Wi‑Fi Alliance** is another organization associated with wireless networking.
- Wireless standards evolve, so you should verify the specific generation in use.
- Ubiquiti’s UniFi WiFi offering fits into this broader wireless ecosystem.

**Wireless standards and naming**

The IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) maintains the 802.11 committee for wireless networking standards. You may see standards written in the older IEEE form or in the newer Wi‑Fi naming scheme.

| IEEE standard | Common Wi‑Fi name |
|---|---|
| `802.11ac` | Wi‑Fi 5 |
| `802.11ax` | Wi‑Fi 6 |
| `802.11be` | Wi‑Fi 7 |

- Use the IEEE name when you need the formal standard.
- Use the Wi‑Fi name when you need the consumer-friendly label.
- Treat both as references to the same wireless generation.

**Wireless planning and coverage**

Wireless environments depend on careful planning behind the scenes. Coverage, channel use, and regulatory constraints all affect how well a deployment performs.

- **Band steering** helps guide clients toward an appropriate band.
- **Frequencies and channels** shape performance and interference behavior.
- **Regulatory impacts** can limit how wireless gear operates in a given region.
- A **heat map** shows the effectiveness of wireless coverage in a building.

> A heat map is a planning and documentation tool, not a live performance metric by itself.

**Documentation and site survey context**

Good wireless deployments rely on documentation. Network maps and site surveys help you understand where equipment sits and how coverage behaves in the real world.

- A **physical network map** shows equipment layout and cabling.
- Site surveys help you evaluate wireless coverage before or after deployment.
- Documentation tools such as Visio, OmniGraffle, and Gliffy can support map creation.
- Use documentation to trace connectivity and validate design choices.

**Practical exam focus**

For exam prep, connect the vendor name to the underlying wireless concepts rather than memorizing product branding alone. If you see Ubiquiti or UniFi in a scenario, think about wireless standards, coverage planning, and documentation.

1. Identify the wireless standard in use.
2. Check whether the scenario involves coverage, channels, or band steering.
3. Use documentation artifacts such as maps or heat maps to interpret the environment.
4. Apply the most practical wireless planning choice for the situation.

**Key takeaways**

- Ubiquiti UniFi belongs in the broader wireless networking conversation.
- IEEE 802.11 is the core standard family to remember.
- Wi‑Fi names like Wi‑Fi 5, Wi‑Fi 6, and Wi‑Fi 7 map to IEEE generations.
- Heat maps and network maps help you plan and validate wireless coverage.
