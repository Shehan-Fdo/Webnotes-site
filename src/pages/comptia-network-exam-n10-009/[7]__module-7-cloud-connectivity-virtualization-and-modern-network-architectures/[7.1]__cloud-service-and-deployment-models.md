## **Course:** CompTIA Network+ (Exam N10-009)

**Cloud service models overview**

Cloud computing delivers IT resources over a network on demand. Instead of buying, installing, and maintaining everything on-premises, you consume services from a provider over an internet connection and typically pay by subscription or pay-as-you-go. The main service models differ by how much of the stack the provider manages versus how much you manage.

- **IaaS** means Infrastructure as a Service.
- **PaaS** means Platform as a Service.
- **SaaS** means Software as a Service.
- Cloud services are often described by the level of management responsibility they shift to the provider.

**IaaS, PaaS, and SaaS**

These three models are the core cloud service offerings. They are useful to compare by the amount of control you keep and the amount of infrastructure you must manage.

| Model | What you consume | What the provider manages | Typical fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| IaaS | Virtualized compute, storage, and networking | Physical infrastructure and underlying cloud resources | You want flexibility and control over operating systems and applications |
| PaaS | A managed platform for building and running applications | Infrastructure plus the platform layer | You want to develop and deploy apps without managing the underlying platform |
| SaaS | Complete software delivered as a service | The application and supporting infrastructure | You want to use software without installing or maintaining it yourself |

IaaS gives you on-demand access to cloud-hosted compute, storage, and networking. It is the closest model to traditional infrastructure, but the provider still owns and maintains the underlying assets. PaaS removes more operational work by giving you a managed environment for application development and deployment. SaaS is the most complete service model because you simply use the application through the cloud.

- **IaaS** supports scaling resources as needed.
- **PaaS** reduces the need to manage the platform layer.
- **SaaS** minimizes your maintenance burden.
- The more managed the service, the less direct control you have over the underlying stack.

**Cloud deployment models**

Deployment models describe where the cloud runs and who can access it. These models focus on infrastructure ownership, access, and sharing rather than on the service layer delivered to users.

- **Public cloud** serves multiple customers over shared provider infrastructure.
- **Private cloud** is dedicated to a single organization.
- **Hybrid cloud** combines two or more cloud environments, often to balance flexibility and control.
- **Multicloud** uses services from more than one cloud provider.

Cloud deployment choices affect cost, control, and operational complexity. Public cloud is common when you want broad access and elastic scaling. Private cloud is useful when you need more dedicated control. Hybrid cloud helps you place workloads where they fit best. Multicloud can reduce dependence on a single provider and let you mix services.

**Service model vs. deployment model**

Service models and deployment models answer different questions. Service models describe how cloud resources are delivered to you. Deployment models describe how the cloud environment is organized and shared.

- **Service model** focuses on responsibility for management.
- **Deployment model** focuses on ownership and access.
- You can combine a deployment model with different service models.

| Category | Question it answers | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Service model | What level of service is delivered? | IaaS, PaaS, SaaS |
| Deployment model | Where and how is the cloud hosted? | Public, private, hybrid, multicloud |

**Practical exam takeaways**

For Network+ purposes, focus on the relationship between control and convenience. As you move from IaaS to PaaS to SaaS, the provider manages more of the environment and you manage less. As you move across deployment models, the key differences are access, ownership, and how environments are combined.

- **IaaS** is best when you need infrastructure flexibility.
- **PaaS** is best when you want to build and deploy applications quickly.
- **SaaS** is best when you want ready-to-use software.
- **Public cloud** emphasizes shared provider resources.
- **Private cloud** emphasizes dedicated organizational control.
- **Hybrid cloud** blends environments for workload placement.
- **Multicloud** spreads services across multiple providers.

> Cloud computing commonly provides on-demand access, network-based delivery, shared resources, elastic scaling, and measured service. These traits help explain why cloud models differ from traditional on-premises IT.

**Quick memory aid**

- **IaaS**: you manage the most.
- **PaaS**: you manage the app, not the platform.
- **SaaS**: you manage the least.
- **Public/private/hybrid/multicloud**: deployment choices, not software delivery choices.
