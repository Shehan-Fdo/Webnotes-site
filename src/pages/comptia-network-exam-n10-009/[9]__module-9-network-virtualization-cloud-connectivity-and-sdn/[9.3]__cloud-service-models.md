## **Course:** CompTIA Network+ (Exam N10-009)

**Cloud service model overview**

Cloud computing gives you on-demand IT resources that scale to business needs without requiring you to maintain all physical hardware and software on-premises. The main service models differ by how much control you keep and how much the provider manages for you.

- **IaaS** provides virtualized infrastructure such as servers, storage, and networking over the internet.
- **PaaS** provides a managed platform for building and deploying applications.
- **SaaS** provides ready-to-use software delivered over the internet.

These models are often described as a spectrum from raw infrastructure to fully managed software. As you move from IaaS to SaaS, provider management increases and your direct control decreases.

**Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS)**

IaaS stands for <abbr title="Infrastructure as a Service">IaaS</abbr> and gives you the most control of the three models. You rent the underlying compute, storage, and network resources, then configure the operating systems and applications yourself.

- You use IaaS when you need flexibility and custom control over the environment.
- The provider handles the physical infrastructure and virtualization layer.
- You manage the software stack that runs on top of the infrastructure.

This model is useful when you want to build from a base layer rather than start with a prebuilt platform or application.

**Platform as a Service (PaaS)**

PaaS stands for <abbr title="Platform as a Service">PaaS</abbr> and sits between infrastructure and software. The provider supplies the platform so you can focus on application development instead of managing the underlying servers and runtime environment.

- You use PaaS when you want to develop and deploy applications without managing the full infrastructure.
- The provider manages more of the environment than with IaaS.
- You keep control over your application code and data.

PaaS reduces operational overhead and helps you focus on building software rather than maintaining the supporting platform.

**Software as a Service (SaaS)**

SaaS stands for <abbr title="Software as a Service">SaaS</abbr> and delivers complete software applications over the internet. You access the application directly, usually through a browser or client, without managing the underlying platform or infrastructure.

- You use SaaS when you want a ready-to-use application.
- The provider manages the application, platform, and infrastructure.
- You typically handle user access and configuration within the software.

SaaS offers the least administrative burden because the service is already built and maintained for you.

**Comparing control and responsibility**

The key difference across the models is who manages what. A practical way to remember them is to think about how much of the stack you are responsible for.

| Model | What you get | Your level of control | Provider responsibility |
|---|---|---|---|
| IaaS | Virtualized infrastructure | Highest | Physical hardware and virtualization |
| PaaS | Managed application platform | Moderate | Infrastructure and platform services |
| SaaS | Complete software application | Lowest | Application, platform, and infrastructure |

This comparison helps you choose the right model based on how much control, flexibility, and management you need.

**How to choose the right model**

Use the model that matches your operational goals and the amount of management you want to keep in-house.

1. Choose IaaS when you need maximum flexibility and want to manage the software stack yourself.
2. Choose PaaS when you want to build applications quickly without managing servers and runtime components.
3. Choose SaaS when you want immediate access to a finished application with minimal administration.

> Cloud service models are not about one being universally better. They are about matching responsibility and control to the task.

**Exam focus**

For exam prep, focus on the relationship between control and management:

- IaaS gives the most control.
- PaaS reduces infrastructure management while supporting development.
- SaaS gives the least control but the most convenience.

If you can identify who manages the infrastructure, platform, and application in each model, you can answer most cloud service model questions confidently.
