## **Course:** 200-301 CCNA

**Wireless LAN basics**

- A **WLAN** (wireless local area network) lets devices connect without wires.
- WLANs support mobility in homes, offices, and campus environments.
- Wireless access helps users move while staying connected.
- Wireless networks can reduce costs when you relocate equipment or users.

**IEEE 802.11 standards**

- **IEEE 802.11** defines WLAN MAC (Media Access Control) and PHY (Physical) protocols.
- 802.11 standards form the basis of Wi-Fi networks.
- Wi-Fi devices use 802.11 amendments to communicate over wireless links.
- Newer standards improve speed, latency, and efficiency compared with older ones.

**Wi-Fi generations**

- **Wi-Fi 6E** extends Wi-Fi into the 6 GHz band.
- **Wi-Fi 7** is IEEE 802.11be.
- Wi-Fi 7 operates across the 2.4 GHz, 5 GHz, and 6 GHz bands.
- Wi-Fi 7 uses techniques such as **MU-MIMO** (multi-user, multiple input, multiple output), **OFDMA** (orthogonal frequency-division multiple access), and **TWT** (target wake time).
- Wi-Fi 7 aims for higher throughput, lower latency, and better efficiency.

**Frequency bands**

- The 2.4 GHz band is a common Wi-Fi frequency range.
- The 5 GHz band is another common Wi-Fi frequency range.
- The 6 GHz band supports newer Wi-Fi technologies.
- Using more bands can reduce interference and increase available channel options.

**Practical comparisons**

- Lower-frequency bands generally travel farther and penetrate obstacles better.
- Higher-frequency bands usually offer more capacity and less congestion.
- Band choice affects range, interference, and performance.
- You should match the band to the coverage and speed needs of the network.

**Key takeaways**

- Wireless standards define how devices communicate over the air.
- IEEE 802.11 is the core WLAN standard family.
- Wi-Fi generations add performance and efficiency improvements.
- Frequency bands shape coverage, interference, and throughput.
