## **Course:** 200-301 CCNA

**NAT and PAT overview**

- **NAT** means Network Address Translation.
- NAT translates one IP address to another IP address.
- **PAT** means Port Address Translation.
- PAT uses port numbers to let many inside hosts share one public address.
- CCNA lab tasks may ask you to configure and verify static NAT.
- CCNA lab tasks may also include NAT, PAT, and troubleshooting.

**Address terms**

- Cisco uses **local** and **global** NAT terms.
- An inside local address is the address used on the inside network.
- An inside global address is the translated address seen on the outside.
- Use the terms carefully when you compare inside and outside addressing.
- Address confusion often comes from mixing local and global terms.

**What NAT does**

- NAT maps private addressing to public addressing.
- NAT helps you present internal hosts with different external addresses.
- PAT extends this idea by reusing one public address for multiple sessions.
- NAT and PAT are part of IP services in the CCNA exam scope.

**Lab focus**

- Verify the translation result after you configure NAT or PAT.
- Check that the inside and outside roles match the topology.
- Confirm that the correct address type appears on each side of the translation.
- Troubleshoot by reviewing the configured NAT behavior and the observed traffic flow.

**Exam and practice notes**

- Expect NAT-related tasks in CCNA practice labs.
- Be ready to identify the translation type from the scenario.
- Be ready to distinguish NAT from PAT by the use of ports.
- Keep the inside and outside terminology consistent during configuration and verification.
