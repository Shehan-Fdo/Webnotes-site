## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Payment processing basics**

- Payment processing is the workflow and technology that moves money from buyer to seller.
- It supports online, in-person, and contactless payments.
- Common payment methods include credit cards, debit cards, bank transfers, and digital wallets.
- The process happens in seconds, but it includes multiple coordinated steps.
- Digital payment systems help businesses accept payments on phones, laptops, and physical terminals.

**Core transaction flow**

- A customer initiates the transaction.
- The **payment gateway** secures and transmits payment data.
- The **payment processor** verifies the transaction.
- The card issuer authorizes the transaction.
- The merchant account holds the funds.
- The processor settles and transfers the funds.

**Key components**

- A **payment gateway** connects the customer-facing checkout to the payment system.
- A **payment processor** handles verification and settlement tasks.
- A **merchant account** is the holding account for card transaction funds.
- These parts work together, but they serve different roles.
- Without both a gateway and a merchant account, you cannot offer card payment options.

**Checkout and customer experience**

- Checkout quality affects conversion and trust.
- Long or error-prone checkout flows can increase basket abandonment.
- A smooth payment experience reduces friction and supports customer satisfaction.
- Businesses should offer a variety of digital payment methods.
- Customers expect to pay easily across devices and channels.

**Payments orchestration**

- **Payments orchestration** helps manage multiple payment methods and providers.
- It becomes more useful as payment options expand.
- It can simplify complexity across cards, digital wallets, bank transfers, and cryptocurrencies.
- It helps merchants coordinate payment options in one flow.

**Business considerations**

- Efficient payment processing supports cash flow.
- It also supports fraud prevention.
- Integrated payment systems can improve scalability.
- Payment infrastructure should match the business’s selling channels.
- Businesses should choose solutions that support secure and easy transactions.

**Implementation focus**

- Start by mapping the payment methods you need to accept.
- Confirm that your gateway integrates with your website, app, or point-of-sale system.
- Verify how funds move into the merchant account.
- Review fees before you commit to a provider.
- Test the checkout flow for errors and unnecessary steps.

**Practical priorities**

- Keep the checkout process simple.
- Reduce points of friction.
- Support secure payment handling.
- Offer payment methods your customers already use.
- Revisit the setup as payment technology and customer preferences change.
