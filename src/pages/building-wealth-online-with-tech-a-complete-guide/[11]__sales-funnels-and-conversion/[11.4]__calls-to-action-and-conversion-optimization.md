## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Calls to action**

- A **call to action** (CTA) asks visitors to take a specific next step.
- Common CTAs include `buy now`, `sign up now`, `get your free guide`, and free trial offers.
- CTAs appear as short phrases or buttons on a website or landing page.
- A CTA should point clearly to the action you want visitors to take.
- CTAs support goals such as email signups, content downloads, and purchases.
- Every landing page needs a CTA that guides visitors toward conversion.

**CTA button optimization**

- CTA buttons matter because they are often the last text a visitor reads before deciding to convert.
- Improve CTA performance with the right **copy**, location, and design.
- A strong button creates a clear path to the conversion goal.
- Treat the CTA button as a critical part of the landing page strategy.
- Focus on making the action simple and easy to understand.
- Test button wording and placement when you want better results.

**Conversion funnels**

- A **conversion funnel** guides people through the buying journey.
- Funnels direct flow so visitors move where you want them to go.
- A sales funnel can turn someone who has never heard of you into a buyer.
- Funnel optimization helps you improve how effectively you move leads toward conversion.
- Conversion funnels help you spot problems and improve website efficiency.
- Funnel analysis supports continuous improvement over time.

**Conversion optimization**

- Conversion rate optimization is part art and part science.
- Use proven strategies to increase subscribers and sales through your funnel.
- Optimize each step of the funnel instead of only the final offer.
- Look for friction that slows visitors down or breaks the path to conversion.
- Improve the experience so visitors can move forward with less effort.
- Keep the goal focused on converting new leads into sales.

**Practical approach**

1. Define the action you want visitors to take.
2. Place a clear CTA where visitors can see it.
3. Write CTA copy that matches the next step.
4. Review the funnel for points where visitors drop off.
5. Adjust copy, design, or placement to improve conversion.

**Key takeaway**

- CTAs and funnels work together to guide visitors toward a purchase or other goal.
- Better conversion comes from clear direction, simple actions, and ongoing optimization.
