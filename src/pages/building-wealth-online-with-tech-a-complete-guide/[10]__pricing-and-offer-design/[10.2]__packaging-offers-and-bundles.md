## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Packaging offers**

- Package the same service to change how buyers perceive it.
- Use clearer naming to move from a generic service to a more specific system.
- Position the offer around the outcome it helps create.
- Treat packaging as part of the sale, not just the delivery.

**Why packaging matters**

- Strong packaging can shift attention from price to value.
- Better framing can support premium pricing.
- Poor packaging can delay or stall a deal.
- Too many options can create choice overload and reduce trust.

**Bundling basics**

- Bundle two or more products or services together.
- Use bundles to encourage cross-sells and upsells.
- Offer a discounted suite when the combined package makes sense.
- Keep an eye on margins before you bundle aggressively.

**When bundling helps**

- Use bundles when you want to increase customer stickiness.
- Use bundles when you want to raise switching costs.
- Use bundles when you want to leverage existing distribution.
- Use bundles when the combined offer is easier to buy than separate items.

**Packaging decisions**

- Keep the offer easy to understand.
- Reduce unnecessary feature clutter.
- Match the package to the buyer’s problem.
- Avoid overcomplicating the choice set.

**Practical takeaways**

1. Name the offer around the result it delivers.
2. Group related items into a simple package.
3. Check whether the bundle improves clarity and value.
4. Avoid bundling if it weakens revenue or margins.
5. Test whether the new packaging improves sales response.
