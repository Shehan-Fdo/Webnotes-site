## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Lead magnet purpose**

- A **lead magnet** is a free, high-value resource offered in exchange for an email address.
- It helps you build an owned channel that is less exposed to algorithm changes.
- It can lower acquisition costs and warm up buyers for courses, coaching, or digital products.
- It should attract subscribers who are likely to become customers.

**What makes a lead magnet work**

- It must deliver real value.
- It should be simple and actionable.
- It should feel specific to the buyer’s problem.
- It should support your main offer instead of acting as disconnected content.

**Common mistakes**

- Avoid low-value filler.
- Avoid generic ebooks with little editing or substance.
- Avoid lead magnets that do not tie into your hero product.
- Avoid using one offer for every audience segment.
- Avoid creating freebies that people download but never use.

**Strategic fit**

- Map the magnet to a funnel stage, angle, or objection.
- Match the offer to a specific avatar.
- Use different magnets for different pain points.
- Treat the magnet as a bridge to the next purchase.

**Practical creation workflow**

1. Gather your best customer reviews.
2. Paste them into an AI tool.
3. Ask the tool to surface the single promise your ideal buyer wants most.
4. Turn that promise into multiple formats, such as a quiz, checklist, or mini-course.
5. Choose the format that best fits the promise and the audience.

**Types of lead magnets**

- Educational resources teach a useful concept or process.
- Templates and tools help the user act faster.
- Interactive offers can qualify interest or segment leads.
- Exclusive offers can create urgency or special access.

**Why email matters**

- Your email list is an owned channel.
- It gives you a direct line to people who care about your work.
- It can keep working even when social platforms change.
- It supports selling, booking clients, and long-term growth.

**Promotion and conversion**

- Promote the magnet where your target audience already pays attention.
- Use the magnet to start a relevant buyer journey.
- Keep the signup path simple.
- Make the value clear before the opt-in.

**Trust and retention**

- New subscribers need to benefit from the freebie.
- Trust matters more than raw traffic over the long haul.
- Useful magnets improve list quality.
- Poor magnets can attract signups that do not convert.

**Key takeaway**

- Build lead magnets that solve a real problem, fit a specific audience, and point naturally to your main offer.
