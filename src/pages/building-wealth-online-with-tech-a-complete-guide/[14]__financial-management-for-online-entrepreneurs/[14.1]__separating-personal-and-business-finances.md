## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Why separation matters**

- Keep personal and business money separate to reduce **co-mingling** of funds, accounts, and assets.
- Separation helps protect the integrity of the corporate veil for incorporated businesses.
- A distinct boundary can support personal asset and credit protection.
- Clear separation makes money management simpler and less stressful.

**What to separate**

- Use different bank accounts for personal and business funds.
- Keep business receipts separate from personal receipts.
- Track shared expenses instead of mixing them into one pool.
- Record when you use personal items for business purposes.
- Pay yourself from the business instead of using the business account like a personal account.

**Practical setup steps**

1. Open a small business bank account.
2. Use a separate personal checking account for personal spending.
3. Get a business debit or credit card for business purchases.
4. Keep business records organized from the start.
5. Review every transaction and assign it to the correct side.

**Business structure and liability**

- A sole proprietorship is not a separate business entity.
- Partnerships, limited liability companies (LLCs), and corporations can offer varying degrees of personal protection from liability and debt.
- An LLC generally helps shield personal assets, but structure alone does not replace good financial separation.
- Treat the business as its own financial unit.

**Good habits to maintain**

- Educate employees and partners about the separation rule.
- Keep receipts and supporting documents for business spending.
- Use a consistent method for shared costs.
- Reconcile accounts regularly so errors do not spread.
- Avoid using personal accounts for business deposits or bills.

**Common mistakes to avoid**

- Depositing business income into a personal account.
- Paying personal bills from a business account.
- Mixing receipts without labeling them.
- Ignoring shared expenses.
- Treating the business account as extra personal cash.

**Key takeaway**

- Separate accounts, records, and spending habits make your finances easier to manage.
- Clean separation supports better bookkeeping, clearer taxes, and stronger business discipline.
