## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Budgeting basics**

- Budgeting helps you plan how money moves over a period.
- A budget gives you a practical view of expected income and spending.
- Use budgeting to support better financial decisions and reduce stress.
- Keep the budget simple enough to review regularly.

**Cash flow management**

- Cash flow is the money coming in and going out of a business.
- Effective cash flow management means you keep more money coming in than going out.
- Cash flow is critical for smooth operations and long-term financial sustainability.
- Poor cash flow can contribute to business failure.

**Why cash flow matters**

- Cash flow helps you keep the business operating smoothly.
- It supports planning and cash flow statement analysis.
- It helps you forecast potential issues before they become urgent.
- It can reveal opportunities for growth.

**Common cash flow problems**

- Late customer payments can strain available cash.
- Paying vendors too early can reduce flexibility.
- Paying vendors too late can create relationship or supply issues.
- Weak visibility into money movement makes problems harder to spot.

**Practical management habits**

- Keep a close eye on the money.
- Make it easier for customers to pay you.
- Avoid paying too early or too late.
- Use the right technology to improve tracking and control.

**Forecasting and planning**

- Build an accurate forecast.
- Review expected inflows and outflows against actual results.
- Adjust plans when conditions change.
- Use forecasting to prepare for shortfalls and timing gaps.

**Operational best practices**

- Improve invoicing so customers can pay faster.
- Optimize inventory management to avoid tying up cash.
- Consider financing when you need extra flexibility.
- Grab early payment discounts when they improve cash position.

**Cash flow mindset**

- Stay agile when conditions shift.
- Use cash flow data to guide day-to-day decisions.
- Treat cash flow as a core part of financial planning.
- Focus on steady control rather than one-time fixes.
