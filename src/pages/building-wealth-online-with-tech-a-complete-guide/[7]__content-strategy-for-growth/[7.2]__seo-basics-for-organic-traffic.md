## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**SEO fundamentals**

- **SEO** stands for search engine optimization.
- SEO helps search engines understand your content and helps users find your site.
- Organic traffic is visitors who come from search engines without paid ads.
- Higher search visibility can increase organic visits.
- Search engines discover, understand, and organize content to show relevant results.

**Why organic traffic matters**

- Organic traffic supports sales, brand visibility, and customer engagement.
- Organic traffic is a sustainable source of website visits.
- Search-driven journeys often start with a query.
- Ranking for relevant keywords helps attract qualified traffic.
- SEO can improve both traffic and engagement.

**Core SEO pillars**

- **Technical SEO** focuses on crawlability, indexing, speed, and security.
- **On-page SEO** helps search engines and users understand page content.
- **Content SEO** centers on useful content that matches search intent.
- **Off-page SEO** supports authority and visibility beyond the site itself.
- These pillars work together as part of a broader SEO strategy.

**Content strategy basics**

- Build a content roadmap around business goals.
- Align content with user intent.
- Use keyword research to guide topics.
- Optimize content structure for search engines and readers.
- Promote content and review performance over time.

**How search engines work**

- Search engines **crawl** pages to find content.
- Search engines **index** pages to store and organize content.
- Search engines **rank** pages to decide search result order.
- If a page is not visible to search engines, it cannot appear in results.
- Following search engine guidance improves eligibility to appear in search.

**Practical SEO habits**

1. Check that your site is crawlable.
2. Create content around relevant search terms.
3. Improve page structure and clarity.
4. Review performance and adjust your approach.
5. Keep learning as search guidance changes.

**Key takeaways**

- SEO is a long-term approach, not a quick fix.
- Strong SEO supports organic growth over time.
- A solid foundation matters more than chasing short-term changes.
- User-focused content and technical health both matter.
- Consistent improvement helps maintain search visibility.
