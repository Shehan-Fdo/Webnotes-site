## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**What demand validation means**

- **Market validation** tests whether real people in your target audience will engage with or pay for your idea.
- It helps you reduce risk before you spend time and money building.
- It is useful when your business is still only an idea.
- It combines customer research with evidence of demand.

**Why you validate before building**

- Building first can waste months on something nobody wants.
- One example shows a simple pre-build video can attract a large waitlist and confirm a real problem.
- Another example shows that expensive products can still fail if demand is weak.
- Validation helps you avoid relying on optimism alone.

**What to look for**

- Check whether there is a real **desire** for the product or service.
- Gather demographic information to understand who your potential customers are.
- Look for limits and opportunities in the market.
- Compare your idea with alternatives to find a competitive advantage.

**Practical validation methods**

- Use market research to confirm and improve the idea.
- Talk to potential customers before you build.
- Test interest with a landing page or waitlist.
- Use no-code methods to collect early signals.
- Measure purchase intent when possible.

**Signals that matter**

- Sign-ups can show early interest.
- Waitlist growth can show demand.
- Conversion rate can show how strongly people respond.
- Cost-per-click can help you judge ad interest.
- Pre-orders can show willingness to pay.

**A simple validation workflow**

1. Define the target audience and the problem.
2. Research the market and competitors.
3. Run a small test with a landing page, ad, interview, or waitlist.
4. Review the response for real demand signals.
5. Decide whether to continue, pivot, or park the idea.

**Common mistakes to avoid**

- Do not confuse excitement with demand.
- Do not build a full product before testing interest.
- Do not rely on a single signal.
- Do not ignore competitive alternatives.
- Do not skip customer research.

**How to use validation results**

- Treat strong early signals as evidence to keep going.
- Treat weak signals as a reason to adjust the idea.
- Use the results to reduce uncertainty before launch.
- Keep the test small until the evidence is clear.

> Validate demand before you build the full product, especially when the cost of a wrong bet is high.
