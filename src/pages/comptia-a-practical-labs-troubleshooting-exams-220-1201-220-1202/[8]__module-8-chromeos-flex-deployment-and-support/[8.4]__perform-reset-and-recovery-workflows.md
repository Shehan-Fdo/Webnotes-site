## **Course:** CompTIA A+ Practical Labs & Troubleshooting (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**Reset and recovery overview**

A factory reset on a Chromebook is also called **Powerwash**. It returns the device to a like-new state by erasing user data on the Chromebook’s hard drive, including files in the `Downloads` folder. It also removes most non-user device information, while preserving a few diagnostic and operational pieces of data.

Before you reset, back up anything important to Google Drive or an external hard drive. Files stored in Google Drive or on external storage are not deleted by the reset.

- Use a factory reset when the device shows `Reset this Chrome device`.
- Consider it when user profile or settings problems persist after a restart.
- Use it when the Chromebook no longer works normally after basic troubleshooting.
- Use it if you need to change the owner of the device.

> If the Chromebook is used at work or school, you cannot reset it yourself. Contact the administrator to wipe the device and re-enroll it on the organization’s network.

**When to try other steps first**

A reset is not the first move in every case. The evidence recommends trying simpler checks before you wipe the device, such as disabling Chrome extensions one at a time and testing after each change.

- Start with the least disruptive fix.
- Test after each change so you can identify the cause.
- Escalate to reset only when the issue remains unresolved.

**What the reset does and does not do**

A factory reset clears local data, but it does not affect cloud-stored or external files. That distinction matters when you are helping a user avoid unnecessary data loss.

| Item | Result after factory reset |
|---|---|
| Files in `Downloads` | Erased |
| Files on Google Drive | Preserved |
| Files on external storage | Preserved |
| Most device information | Erased |
| Some diagnostic/operational data | Preserved |

**Recovery and administrative handling**

For managed devices, recovery is not a self-service action. If the Chromebook belongs to a work or school environment, the administrator handles the wipe and re-enrollment process. This keeps the device aligned with the organization’s management controls.

- Do not attempt an unsupported reset on a managed Chromebook.
- Use the administrator path for wipe and re-enrollment.
- Treat recovery as part of device lifecycle management, not just troubleshooting.

**Practical exam takeaway**

For A+ Core 2, focus on the decision point: reset only after basic troubleshooting fails, and always protect user data first. The key workflow is simple: back up, verify whether the device is managed, and then choose the correct reset or administrator escalation path.
