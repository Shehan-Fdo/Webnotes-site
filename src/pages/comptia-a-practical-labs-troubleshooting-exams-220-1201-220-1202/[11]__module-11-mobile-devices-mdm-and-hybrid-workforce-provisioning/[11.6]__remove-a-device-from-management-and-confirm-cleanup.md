## **Course:** CompTIA A+ Practical Labs & Troubleshooting (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**Device removal overview**

Mobile device management (MDM) lets an organization control enrolled devices from a central console. When a device is no longer in use, you remove it from management to reduce stale records and keep compliance reporting accurate.

In Microsoft Intune, inactive or orphaned devices can remain in the admin center until they are cleaned up. A device may also still appear briefly after a remote action until it checks in again.

- **Retire** removes company data, managed apps, settings, and profiles while preserving personal data.
- **Delete** removes the device record from Intune immediately when you need it gone from the console.
- **Cleanup rules** automatically remove inactive device records after a set number of days.

**Intune retire vs. cleanup behavior**

The retire action is useful when a device leaves organizational control but you do not want a full factory reset. It unenrolls the device from Intune and removes management-delivered content, but it does not erase user content.

Cleanup rules target devices that have not checked in recently. The available inactivity window is configurable between 30 and 270 days. This helps you remove stale records created by test devices, workforce changes, or replaced hardware.

| Action | What it does | When to use it |
|---|---|---|
| `Retire` | Removes company data and MDM-delivered items, keeps personal data | A personally owned device or a device leaving management |
| `Delete` | Removes the device record from Intune | You need the record removed right away |
| Device clean-up rule | Automatically deletes inactive records after a set time | You want ongoing cleanup of stale devices |

> The device may still appear in the admin center until it checks in or the delete action completes.

**How to remove a device from management**

1. Open the Microsoft Endpoint Manager admin center.
2. Go to `Devices`.
3. Select `Device clean-up rules` if you want automatic removal of inactive records.
4. Enable the rule and choose the inactivity threshold between `30` and `270` days.
5. Use `Retire` when you want to remove management data but keep personal data.
6. Use `Delete` when you want the device record removed immediately.

**Confirm cleanup**

After you remove or retire a device, verify that the record no longer appears as an active managed device.

- Check whether the device still shows in the admin center.
- Confirm the device has stopped checking in.
- Review compliance reporting for stale entries that should no longer count.
- Use the affected-devices view, when available, to see what the cleanup rule will remove before saving changes.

**Practical exam takeaway**

For A+ troubleshooting and lab scenarios, focus on the outcome you need:

- Choose **retire** when you want to remove organizational control without wiping personal data.
- Choose **delete** when you need the console record removed now.
- Use **cleanup rules** to automate removal of inactive devices and keep reports clean.
