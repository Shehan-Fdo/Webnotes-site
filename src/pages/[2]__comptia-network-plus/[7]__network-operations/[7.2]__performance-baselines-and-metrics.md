## **Course:** Comptia Network Plus

**Performance baselines**

- A **baseline** is a reference point for normal network performance.
- You collect performance data over time to represent normal operating conditions.
- You compare current behavior to historical data to spot changes.
- Baselines help you identify anomalies before they affect users.
- Baselines also support capacity planning and trend analysis.
- Historical data can reveal slow growth that may lead to future bottlenecks.

**Performance metrics**

- Metrics give you measurable details about network health.
- Interface statistics help you assess performance and availability.
- You can watch for increasing **CRC errors** as a sign of cable or interface problems.
- You can track utilization to see whether congestion is getting too high.
- You can measure response times and resource usage as part of monitoring.
- Error rates can help you narrow down performance degradation.

**Monitoring methods**

- You can view many metrics in the device operating system.
- In larger environments, you can use **SNMP** (Simple Network Management Protocol) to query devices remotely.
- SNMP lets you gather metrics over time from multiple devices.
- Continuous monitoring helps you detect small issues early.
- Comparing live data to baseline data helps you isolate likely causes faster.

**Practical troubleshooting use**

- Use baselines to identify bottlenecks by comparing current performance with historical information.
- Focus on metrics that matter to the service you are checking.
- Application teams may care most about response time.
- Network teams may care most about utilization and resource usage.
- Trend data helps you predict when an upgrade may be needed.

**Key takeaways**

- Baselines describe normal behavior.
- Metrics show current conditions.
- Monitoring reveals deviations from expected performance.
- Historical comparison helps you find problems sooner.
- Good baselines make troubleshooting more precise.
