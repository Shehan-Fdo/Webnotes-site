## **Course:** Comptia Network Plus

**Networking standards overview**

- Standards help different vendors build compatible network technologies.
- **IEEE** defines many local networking standards.
- **IETF** develops Internet standards and related technical documents.
- CompTIA Network+ covers network connectivity, documentation, protocols, and architecture.

**IEEE and Ethernet**

- **Ethernet** is a wired networking technology defined by the IEEE 802.3 working group.
- IEEE 802.3 supports LAN operation across speeds from `1 Mb/s` to `400 Gb/s`.
- The standard uses a common **MAC** (Media Access Control) specification.
- It supports shared-medium half duplex and full duplex operation.
- IEEE 802.3 includes media-independent interfaces for coaxial, twisted pair, fiber optic cables, and electrical backplanes.
- The standard also covers repeaters and selected power over twisted pair capabilities.
- Ethernet continues to evolve to meet rising bandwidth demand.

**IEEE and Wi-Fi**

- **Wi-Fi** is based on the IEEE 802.11 series of wireless connectivity standards.
- IEEE 802.11 supports wireless local area network (**WLAN**) communication over radio waves.
- The 802.11be standard is an active wireless LAN medium access control standard.
- Wi-Fi standards help enable broad wireless access for devices in homes, businesses, and public spaces.

**IETF and Internet standards**

- The **Internet Engineering Task Force** (IETF) is a standards development organization for the Internet.
- The IETF was founded in `1986`.
- The IETF produces voluntary standards that are often adopted by users, operators, and vendors.
- Its mission is to make the Internet work better.
- The IETF publishes protocol standards, best current practices, and informational documents.

**Practical exam focus**

- Match the standard body to the technology it governs.
- Associate IEEE 802.3 with Ethernet.
- Associate IEEE 802.11 with Wi-Fi.
- Associate IETF with Internet protocols and related guidance.
- Remember that standards improve interoperability across networks and devices.
