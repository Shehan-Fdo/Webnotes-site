## **Course:** Comptia Network Plus

**Wireless media basics**

- Wireless networking uses radio frequency signals instead of Ethernet cables.
- 802.11 networks commonly use the `2.4 GHz` and `5 GHz` frequency ranges.
- IEEE groups frequencies into **channels** for easier wireless configuration.
- Choose channels that do not overlap when you want nearby access points to coexist.

**Antenna types and behavior**

- A wireless network depends heavily on its antennas.
- **Omnidirectional** antennas radiate signal broadly around the access point.
- **Directional** antennas focus signal toward a specific area.
- High-gain antennas can hear better than lower-gain antennas.
- Antenna type affects how far and how well a signal reaches.

**Power levels and placement**

- Set wireless power as low as practical to cover the needed area.
- Lower power helps keep signals inside the intended space.
- Test coverage at the perimeter of the office or building.
- Adjust power after you observe real-world coverage.
- Placement and surrounding materials affect wireless performance.
- Some objects absorb wireless frequencies.
- Some objects reflect wireless frequencies.

**Wireless design considerations**

- Frequency choice affects how a wireless network behaves.
- Bandwidth is the amount of frequency used at a given time.
- Different 802.11 standards use different bandwidths.
- Coverage planning should account for antenna type, power, and environment.
- Wireless design is a balance between reach, containment, and usability.

**Exam focus**

- Know the difference between `2.4 GHz` and `5 GHz` usage.
- Know why non-overlapping channels matter.
- Know when to use **omnidirectional** versus **directional** antennas.
- Know why transmit power is often reduced instead of maximized.
- Know how the physical environment changes wireless signal behavior.
