## **Course:** Comptia Network Plus

**Power safety**

- Disconnect equipment from the power source before you touch internal components.
- Treat energized parts as a hazard even after unplugging a device.
- Some devices store charge in capacitors.
- Older **CRT** (cathode-ray tube) monitors and laser printers can still shock you after power removal.
- Do not connect yourself to a ground wire or any path that could carry current.

**Power protection**

- Use a **UPS** (uninterruptible power supply) to protect against power interruptions.
- Use a **PDU** (power distribution unit) to distribute power to equipment.
- Plan for reliable power as part of the technology infrastructure.

**Environmental factors**

- Data centers run continuously and need environmental control.
- Manage **humidity** to reduce condensation and static discharge risk.
- Keep humidity in a practical range around `40%` to `60%` when possible.
- High humidity can lead to condensation.
- Low humidity can increase static discharge.
- Control **temperature** to support equipment operation.
- Use fire suppression options appropriate for data center environments.
- Environmental controls consume significant power in data centers.

**Physical installation considerations**

- Place equipment with the surrounding environment in mind.
- Account for the conditions where the technology resides.
- Protect hardware from moisture, heat, and other environmental stressors.
