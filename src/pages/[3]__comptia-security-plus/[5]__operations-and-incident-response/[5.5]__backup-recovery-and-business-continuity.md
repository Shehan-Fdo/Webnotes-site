## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**Backup and recovery basics**

- A **backup** gives you a way to restore data after loss or corruption.
- Plan backups before an incident happens.
- Choose backup scope based on how much data you need to protect.
- Select backup media based on your environment.
- Store backup media securely after the job completes.
- Use **encryption** to protect backup data.
- Use **snapshots** when you need point-in-time recovery.
- Use **replication** when you need a copy of data available elsewhere.
- Test recovery so you know the backup works.

**Disaster recovery**

- **Disaster recovery** restores IT operations after a major disruption.
- Disruptions can include cyber threats, natural disasters, or system failures.
- Recovery planning helps reduce downtime and data loss.
- Recovery planning supports overall cybersecurity readiness.
- Measure recovery performance with **MTTR** (mean time to recover).
- Measure acceptable data loss with **RPO** (recovery point objective).
- Measure acceptable downtime with **RTO** (recovery time objective).
- Use these metrics to compare recovery options and priorities.

**Incident response and continuity**

- **Incident response** handles abnormal system operation.
- Incident response focuses on what happened, why it happened, and where it happened.
- Response actions depend on information criticality.
- Response actions also depend on whether other business operations are affected.
- **Continuity of operations (COOP)** keeps essential functions running during disruption.
- COOP uses alternative business practices when normal operations fail.
- COOP planning identifies critical processes.
- COOP planning sets recovery priorities.
- COOP planning includes testing.

**Business impact analysis**

- A **business impact analysis (BIA)** identifies critical business functions.
- BIA helps you decide what to restore first.
- BIA supports recovery planning.
- BIA helps you understand which functions matter most to the mission.
- BIA improves planning for remote work and other alternate operations.

**Planning and testing**

- Build plans before the event, not during it.
- Document incident types and category definitions.
- Use categories so responders know how to react.
- Test plans to validate that they work.
- Review and improve plans after testing.
- Practice alternate operations so the organization can continue under stress.

**Key exam focus**

- Security+ connects backup, recovery, and business continuity to availability.
- The goal is to keep critical services running with minimal interruption.
- Strong planning improves resilience during both small incidents and major disasters.
