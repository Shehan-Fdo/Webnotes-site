## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**Disaster recovery planning overview**

- Disaster recovery handles a **major disruption** while keeping operations going.
- Plan before an event happens.
- Preparation improves recovery speed and reduces impact.
- Incident response focuses on abnormal system operation and identifying what, why, and where.
- Disaster recovery focuses on restoring services after a larger event.
- Continuity of operations keeps essential business functions running during disruption.

**Planning and testing**

- Build recovery plans before an incident occurs.
- Document response steps for abnormal system events.
- Define incident categories and types so responders know how to react.
- Use scheduled exercises to test people and processes.
- Include tabletop exercises, walkthroughs, and simulations.
- Test communication plans as part of preparation.
- Avoid affecting production networks during exercises.
- Train IT staff and front-line employees on response actions.
- Use a Computer Security Incident Response Team (**CSIRT**) when the response scope requires it.

**Recovery objectives and metrics**

- Use **RTO** (Recovery Time Objective) to define acceptable downtime.
- Use **RPO** (Recovery Point Objective) to define acceptable data loss.
- Use **MTTR** (Mean Time to Repair) to estimate repair duration.
- Use **MTBF** (Mean Time Between Failures) to assess reliability.
- Use **MTTF** (Mean Time to Failure) to estimate lifespan until failure.
- Use these metrics to evaluate incidents and plan recovery.
- Pair RTO and RPO when deciding how much service and data loss is acceptable.
- Quantify outage impact so you know when service must return.

**Recovery planning considerations**

- Perform risk analysis to understand recovery options.
- Identify single points of failure.
- Determine the impact of an outage on the organization.
- Consider functional recovery plans and redundancy.
- Restore IT operations after cybersecurity threats, natural disasters, or system failures.
- Aim to minimize downtime and data loss.
- Match recovery strategy to the service level you need.

**Site readiness levels**

| Site type | Readiness | Typical recovery speed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cold | Empty space with power and cooling | Days to weeks |
| Warm | Hardware installed, current data still needed | Hours to a day |
| Hot | Fully operational with real-time replication | Minutes to hours |

**Practical exam takeaways**

- Recovery planning starts before the disaster.
- Testing validates the plan and the team.
- RTO answers how fast you must restore service.
- RPO answers how much data loss you can accept.
- Strong planning supports business continuity.
