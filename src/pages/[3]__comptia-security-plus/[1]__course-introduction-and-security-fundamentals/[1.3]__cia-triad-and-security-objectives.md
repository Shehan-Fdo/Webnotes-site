## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**CIA triad overview**

- The **CIA triad** is the foundational model for IT security.
- CIA stands for **confidentiality**, **integrity**, and **availability**.
- Some sources call it the **AIC triad** to avoid confusion with the Central Intelligence Agency.
- The model helps you think about security as three separate objectives.
- It supports security design, assessment, and improvement.

**Confidentiality**

- **Confidentiality** protects information from unauthorized access.
- You use it when you need to keep private data private.
- It focuses on limiting who can view or obtain information.
- Access controls and encryption are common ways to support confidentiality.

**Integrity**

- **Integrity** means data stays accurate and unchanged unless authorized.
- It ensures the recipient receives exactly what was sent.
- It helps you trust that information is complete and not improperly altered.
- Integrity matters when data moves between systems or users.

**Availability**

- **Availability** means systems and data remain accessible when needed.
- It focuses on keeping services up and running.
- It supports business operations and everyday use.
- Resilience and redundancy help maintain availability.

**How the triad works together**

- The three goals work as a single security framework.
- Strong security requires balancing all three objectives.
- Improving one area should not create major weakness in another.
- The triad gives you a simple checklist for evaluating security.

**Exam focus**

- Match each term to its core meaning.
- Confidentiality protects access.
- Integrity protects correctness.
- Availability protects access to systems and data.
- Remember that the CIA triad is about security objectives, not the U.S. government agency.
