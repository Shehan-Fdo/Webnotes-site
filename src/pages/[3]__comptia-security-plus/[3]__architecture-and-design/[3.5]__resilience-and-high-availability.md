## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**Resilience and recovery in security architecture**

- **Resilience** helps you keep systems available during disruptions.
- **Recovery** focuses on restoring services after an outage or failure.
- These concepts support continuity of operations in security architecture.
- Availability and uptime are central goals in this topic.

**High availability**

- **High availability (HA)** keeps systems running and always available.
- HA uses multiple systems at the same time.
- If one system fails, another system continues service.
- HA reduces downtime compared with waiting to replace a failed component.
- HA is a practical way to improve service continuity.

**Redundancy and fault tolerance**

- **Redundancy** adds extra components so a backup is ready if one fails.
- Redundant systems improve resilience.
- **Fault tolerance** supports continued operation when a component fails.
- These designs help protect critical business processes.
- Cloud-native architectures are often associated with built-in redundancy and failover.

**Common resiliency approaches**

- **Server clustering** groups servers to support availability.
- **Load balancing** distributes work across systems.
- **Site resiliency** helps services survive site-level disruption.
- **Multi-cloud** systems spread services across more than one cloud environment.
- These approaches aim to maintain service during failure or outage.

**Planning for outages**

- Plan for component failure before it happens.
- Keep secondary systems ready for use.
- Avoid designs that require long manual replacement steps.
- Consider how quickly you can restore service after a disruption.
- Match the design to the availability needs of the business.

**Key exam focus**

- Know the difference between resilience, recovery, and high availability.
- Recognize that HA emphasizes continuous service.
- Recognize that redundancy supports failover.
- Recognize that cloud-native design can improve redundancy and resiliency.
- Focus on uptime, continuity, and rapid restoration.
