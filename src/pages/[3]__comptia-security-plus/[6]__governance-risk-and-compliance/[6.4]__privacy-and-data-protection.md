## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**Privacy and data protection overview**

- Data protection supports **confidentiality**, integrity, and availability.
- Security+ emphasizes practical controls for real-world data handling.
- Organizations protect data based on its type, sensitivity, and regulatory requirements.
- Some data is governed by external rules, laws, or industry standards.

**Data types and classifications**

- **Regulated data** follows third-party rules for protection.
- Credit card data must align with Payment Card Industry standards.
- Government laws can define how long you store data and how you protect it.
- Trade secrets need protection because they contain organization-specific knowledge.
- Data handling changes based on who has the data and how you use it.

**Sensitive data categories**

- **PII** means personally identifiable information.
- **PHI** means protected health information.
- **PCI** means payment card industry data.
- These categories need heightened controls because they are sensitive.
- Privacy laws and standards often drive how you protect these data types.

**Privacy-enhancing techniques**

- **Tokenization** replaces sensitive data with a different token.
- Tokenization lets you use data without exposing the original value.
- A private mapping database links the token back to the original data.
- **Data minimization** limits the amount of personal data you collect or use.
- **Data masking** hides sensitive values from view.
- **Anonymization** removes identifying value from data.
- **Obfuscation** makes data harder to understand or misuse.

**Protecting data in practice**

- Use **geographic restrictions** to control where data is accessed or stored.
- Use location-based policy decisions when data access depends on region.
- IP address and subnet information can help estimate location.
- Wireless devices may need extra checks because location is less certain.
- Apply encryption and hashing where appropriate to protect data.
- Choose controls that match the data’s sensitivity and use case.

**Compliance and governance**

- **GRC** means governance, risk, and compliance.
- GRC aligns IT and business goals.
- GRC helps you manage risk while maintaining business operations.
- GRC also supports adherence to applicable laws and standards.
- Compliance requirements can shape how you store, process, and retain data.

**Key exam takeaways**

- Identify the data type before you choose a control.
- Treat regulated data according to the applicable standard.
- Use privacy controls to reduce exposure of sensitive information.
- Match the protection method to the data’s purpose and risk.
