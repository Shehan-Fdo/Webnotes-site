## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**Physical security basics**

Physical security protects assets by limiting who can physically reach systems, rooms, and equipment. The evidence emphasizes that digital security is not enough if attackers can walk into a facility and remove or access hardware.

A practical physical security plan focuses on controlled entry, monitoring, and deterrence. Common controls in the evidence include access control vestibules, mantraps, badge readers, video surveillance, and alarm systems.

- **Access control vestibule**: a small room that controls entry into a sensitive area such as a data center.
- **Mantrap**: a controlled entry space where only one person or very few people pass at a time.
- **Badge readers**: devices that verify credentials before granting access.
- **Video surveillance**: cameras that monitor activity and support investigation.
- **Alarm systems**: alerts that help detect unauthorized entry or tampering.

**Access control vestibules and mantraps**

An access control vestibule gives you a checkpoint between the outside area and the protected space. In the evidence, this is described as a small room with two or more doors that lets an organization verify credentials before allowing entry.

A mantrap works on the same general idea, but the key rule is stricter: you never allow both doors to be open at the same time. This creates a controlled flow so only one person, or a very small number of people, can move through the area at once.

> A mantrap is designed to prevent tailgating and uncontrolled group entry by forcing sequential door access.

- The first door opens to admit one person.
- The second door stays locked until the first door closes.
- Some implementations lock all other doors after the first person enters.
- The control point can verify identity before the next door opens.

**Badge-based access control**

Badge systems help manage who can enter a facility or restricted area. The evidence describes badge access as a modern way to control entry while allowing authorized people to move through the building efficiently.

Badge credentials may use technologies such as RFID, magnetic strips, or smart technologies. The core purpose is the same: verify identity and grant or deny access based on authorization.

- **RFID**: radio frequency identification used in some badge systems.
- **Magnetic strip**: a swipe-based credential format.
- **Smart credential**: a more advanced electronic badge or token.

Badge systems are useful because they replace or supplement traditional lock-and-key access with a more manageable credential process. They also support tracking of entry actions in some implementations.

**Monitoring and deterrence**

Physical security is not only about blocking entry. It also includes observing activity and discouraging unauthorized behavior. The evidence specifically mentions video surveillance and alarm systems as part of the control set.

These controls help you detect suspicious activity, respond to incidents, and review what happened after an event. They are most effective when combined with access controls rather than used alone.

- **Surveillance** helps you see who entered and when.
- **Alarms** help you detect forced entry or unauthorized access attempts.
- Combined controls improve both prevention and response.

**Practical exam focus**

For A+ exam prep, focus on what each control does and where it fits in a layered physical security strategy. The exam may ask you to identify the best control for a controlled entry point, a monitored area, or a restricted room.

1. Identify the threat or access problem.
2. Match the control to the goal.
3. Choose the option that best limits physical access while supporting verification or monitoring.

**Quick comparison**

| Control | Main purpose | Key idea |
|---|---|---|
| Access control vestibule | Controlled entry checkpoint | Verify access before entering the secure area |
| Mantrap | Strict one-person-at-a-time flow | Never allow both doors open together |
| Badge reader | Credential verification | Grant or deny entry based on badge data |
| Video surveillance | Observation and review | Record or monitor activity |
| Alarm system | Alerting | Notify on unauthorized access or tampering |

**Remember**

Physical security supports every other layer of defense. If someone can physically reach a device, cabinet, or room, they may bypass many digital protections. Use layered controls to reduce that risk.
