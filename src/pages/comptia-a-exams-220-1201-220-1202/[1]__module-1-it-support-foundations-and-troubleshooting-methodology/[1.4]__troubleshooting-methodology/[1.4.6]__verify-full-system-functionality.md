## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**What this step means**

`Verify full system functionality` is the final troubleshooting step after you implement a solution or escalate the issue. At this stage, you confirm that the original problem is resolved and that the system still works normally in the areas affected by the fix.

This step matters because a solution can remove one symptom while creating another. Verification helps you catch lingering issues, confirm the user’s experience, and make sure the repair did not disrupt other functions.

- Confirm the original issue no longer occurs.
- Check related features and services for side effects.
- Make sure the system behaves as expected in normal use.
- If needed, apply preventive measures to reduce repeat failures.

**How to verify the fix**

Use practical checks that match the problem you solved. The goal is not just to see one symptom disappear, but to confirm the whole system is stable enough for normal operation.

1. Test the specific function that failed before.
2. Check dependent components, services, or workflows.
3. Monitor performance if the issue involved speed, stability, or repeated failures.
4. Ask the user whether the system now meets their needs.
5. Escalate again if the problem remains or a new issue appears.

> Verification should be thorough enough to prove the solution worked without introducing new problems.

**Document the outcome**

Documentation is part of the troubleshooting closeout. It creates a record that helps future support efforts and builds a knowledge base for similar incidents.

Include the following in your notes:

- The original symptoms.
- The theory you tested.
- The actions you took.
- The final result of verification.
- Any preventive measures or follow-up steps.

A clear record helps you avoid repeating work and gives other technicians a faster path when the same issue returns.

**Where this fits in the troubleshooting methodology**

CompTIA’s troubleshooting flow places verification near the end, after you establish a plan, implement the solution, and before you close the incident. In practice, this means you do not consider the job complete until the system is confirmed functional and the resolution is documented.

- Identify the problem.
- Establish a theory of probable cause.
- Test the theory to determine the cause.
- Establish a plan of action.
- Implement the solution or escalate as necessary.
- Verify full system functionality and, if applicable, implement preventive measures.

**Practical exam focus**

For exam questions, look for clues that the technician must confirm success after a fix. The best answer usually includes testing the affected feature, checking for unintended effects, and recording the resolution.

- `Verify` means confirm the issue is resolved.
- `Document` means record what happened and what you changed.
- `Preventive measures` means reduce the chance of the same issue returning.
