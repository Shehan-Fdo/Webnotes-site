## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**What this step means**

`Establish a plan of action` is the point in the troubleshooting methodology where you decide how to resolve the issue and think through what the fix might affect. It comes after you have identified the problem, formed a theory of probable cause, and tested that theory.

This step is practical, not theoretical. You use the information you already gathered to choose the next move, reduce risk, and avoid creating a new problem while solving the original one.

- **Plan** the fix before you make changes.
- Consider possible effects on users, systems, and services.
- Keep the solution aligned with the evidence you have already verified.

**Where it fits in the troubleshooting process**

CompTIA’s troubleshooting methodology places this step after testing the theory and before implementation. The full flow is:

1. Identify the problem.
2. Establish a theory of probable cause.
3. Test the theory to determine the cause.
4. Establish a plan of action to resolve the problem and identify potential effects.
5. Implement the solution or escalate as necessary.
6. Verify full system functionality and, if applicable, implement preventive measures.

This sequence matters because it keeps you from jumping straight to changes without understanding the likely cause. It also helps you decide whether the issue is something you can safely fix or whether you should escalate.

**What to include in the plan**

A good plan answers two questions: what will you do, and what might happen when you do it?

- Choose the most likely corrective action based on your test results.
- Identify any potential side effects before you proceed.
- Decide whether the issue can be resolved locally or needs escalation.
- Prepare for implementation so you can act efficiently once the plan is approved.

**Practical mindset**

Use a conservative approach when the impact is unclear. If a fix could disrupt service, affect multiple users, or change system behavior in an unexpected way, account for that before you proceed. The goal is to solve the issue without introducing unnecessary risk.

> If the likely fix has uncertain impact, pause and evaluate the consequences before implementation.

**How this step supports the next one**

Once you have a clear plan, implementation becomes straightforward. You already know the intended action and the possible effects, so you can carry out the fix or escalate with better context. That makes verification easier afterward because you know what changed and why.

**Key takeaway**

`Establish a plan of action` is the decision point between diagnosis and repair. You use the evidence you have, choose the safest effective fix, and think ahead about consequences before you act.
