## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**Compliance and policy basics**

Licensing and compliance awareness focuses on how you handle software, data, and evidence in a way that meets organizational rules and legal requirements. In A+ contexts, this includes policy enforcement, privacy handling, and documenting actions so you can show what happened and who was responsible.

- **Compliance** means your systems, processes, and data handling align with applicable requirements.
- **Policy enforcement** matters because licensing misuse and policy lapses can lead to penalties.
- **Internal audits** help you verify that users and systems follow established rules.
- **Privacy incidents** require careful handling of sensitive information and documented response steps.

**Software licensing concepts**

Software licensing defines how software can be used, installed, and tracked. A practical compliance approach is to keep visibility into license keys, installations, and usage so you can confirm that deployments match the license terms.

- **Perpetual licenses** are one license type you should recognize.
- **Digital rights management (DRM)** helps control how digital content or software is used.
- **License tracking** supports compliance by tying installations to valid entitlements.
- **Monitoring application launch frequency** is not a substitute for license management.

> Track licenses and installations directly rather than relying on indirect usage signals.

**Privacy, regulated data, and agreements**

Privacy and compliance work often involves handling regulated data and following formal agreements. You should know that organizations may require specific controls for sensitive information and may use agreements to define how that information is shared or protected.

- **Non-disclosure agreements (NDAs)** restrict disclosure of protected information.
- **Payment card security** and **protected health information** are examples of regulated data concerns.
- **Acceptable use policies (AUPs)** define what users may and may not do with organizational resources.
- **User compliance** monitoring helps confirm that people follow privacy and usage rules.

**Incident response and chain of custody**

When evidence is involved, you need to preserve integrity from the start. Chain of custody documents everyone who handles the evidence, which helps prevent tampering and supports later review.

1. Document the evidence and label it clearly.
2. Record every person who accesses or transfers it.
3. Preserve physical evidence in a sealed container when appropriate.
4. Use hashing for digital evidence to verify it has not changed.

> If evidence integrity is questioned, incomplete chain-of-custody records weaken your case.

**Internal audits and enforcement**

Audits and enforcement turn policy into practice. You use them to check whether licensing, privacy, and acceptable use controls are actually working, then correct gaps before they become larger compliance problems.

- **Oversight** keeps policy from becoming a paper-only requirement.
- **Audit findings** can reveal weak license tracking or poor user adherence.
- **Enforcement** should be consistent so users understand expectations.
- **Documentation** gives you a record for review, investigation, and accountability.

**Quick comparison**

| Concept | Practical focus |
|---|---|
| Licensing | Track valid use of software and installations |
| DRM | Restrict or control digital content usage |
| NDA | Limit disclosure of protected information |
| AUP | Set user behavior expectations |
| Chain of custody | Preserve evidence integrity and accountability |

**Exam focus reminders**

A+ questions in this area usually test whether you can choose the control or process that best supports compliance. Look for the answer that directly addresses tracking, documentation, policy enforcement, or evidence integrity.

- Choose **direct tracking** for licensing compliance.
- Choose **chain of custody** for evidence handling.
- Choose **AUPs** for user behavior rules.
- Choose **internal audits** for ongoing verification.
