## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**USB as a network of connected devices**

USB (Universal Serial Bus) is a common way to connect peripherals and move data between devices. In A+ terms, think of USB as a controller-managed connection system on the motherboard that lets multiple devices communicate through the same host.

- A **USB controller** on the motherboard manages USB communication.
- USB devices often “just work” because the controller handles the connection path.
- USB is widely used for peripherals such as keyboards, mice, and portable devices.

**USB connector types**

USB connectors come in several physical forms. You should recognize the common types and associate them with typical device use.

- **Type-A** is the most common connector and is often used for peripherals like keyboards and mice.
- **Type-B** is often used for larger devices such as printers.
- **Mini-USB** and **Micro-USB** are used on smaller portable devices.
- **USB-C** is the newest and most versatile connector.

USB-C is notable because it supports broader use across devices and can provide faster data transfer, greater power delivery, and the ability to carry audio and video signals.

**USB-to-Ethernet adapters**

A USB-to-Ethernet adapter adds a wired network port to a computer that lacks one or needs an external option. This is useful when you need a wired connection on a device without built-in Ethernet.

- Use it to add an Ethernet port to a supported computer.
- It can provide a wired alternative when Wi-Fi is unavailable or disabled.
- Wired connections are generally more stable, more reliable, and more secure than wireless connections.

> Use the adapter type that matches the device’s available USB port, such as USB-A or USB-C.

**USB Ethernet standards**

Some USB Ethernet adapters rely on driver standards that affect how network traffic is handled. Two main standards are mentioned in the evidence.

- **USB CDC-ECM** (Communication Device Class - Ethernet Control Model) is an older standard.
- **USB CDC-NCM** (Network Control Model) is newer and more advanced.
- NCM uses a more direct packet path than ECM, which is described as hardware-optimized.

**Practical exam takeaways**

For exam prep, focus on matching connector types to device categories and understanding why a USB Ethernet adapter is used.

- Identify **USB-A** as the common peripheral connector.
- Identify **USB-B** as common on printers and similar larger devices.
- Identify **USB-C** as the most versatile modern connector.
- Recognize that a USB-to-Ethernet adapter provides wired networking when built-in Ethernet is missing.
- Remember that wired networking is typically more stable and secure than wireless.

**Troubleshooting context**

When a USB network adapter is involved, confirm the physical connection first. If the device is not communicating, the issue may be with the cable, the port, or the adapter itself.

- Check that the adapter is seated correctly.
- Verify the correct connector type for the device.
- Confirm that the wired link is active before moving to deeper network troubleshooting.
