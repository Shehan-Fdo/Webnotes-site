## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**IaaS overview**

Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) is a cloud service model that delivers virtualized infrastructure over the internet or a private network. The provider supplies core resources such as compute, storage, and networking, while you use them on demand instead of buying and maintaining physical hardware.

IaaS is often the first cloud step for organizations moving workloads out of their own data centers. It helps reduce the need to procure equipment, build out physical space, and manage all underlying infrastructure internally.

- **Compute** resources provide the processing power for workloads.
- **Storage** resources hold data and application files.
- **Networking** resources connect systems and support traffic flow.
- **Virtualization** lets the provider present infrastructure as flexible services.

**How IaaS works**

With IaaS, you request the infrastructure you need and configure it for your applications and IT systems. The provider maintains the physical infrastructure, while you remain responsible for deploying, maintaining, and supporting your applications.

This model is commonly described as pay-as-you-go or subscription-based. That makes it easier to scale resources up or down as demand changes without overbuilding for peak usage.

1. Choose the required infrastructure resources.
2. Configure the virtual environment for your workload.
3. Deploy and manage your applications on top of that infrastructure.
4. Scale resources as usage grows or shrinks.

> IaaS does not remove your responsibility for the software you run. It shifts the hardware burden to the provider.

**Why organizations use IaaS**

IaaS is attractive because traditional infrastructure acquisition can take time and capital. Organizations may need procurement cycles, physical rooms with power and cooling, and staff to manage and maintain systems. IaaS reduces those barriers by making infrastructure available on demand.

It also supports growth and changing demand. If an application needs more resources, you can add capacity quickly. If demand drops, you can remove excess resources and avoid paying for infrastructure you do not use.

- Faster access to infrastructure than buying hardware
- Better elasticity for changing workloads
- Less need to manage physical data center equipment
- More practical scaling for spikes in demand

**Common use cases**

IaaS is useful when you need infrastructure flexibility without owning the underlying hardware. It fits workloads that need servers, storage, and networking but still require you to control the operating system and application layer.

Typical use cases include:
- Hosting application workloads that need scalable infrastructure
- Supporting data storage needs without local hardware purchases
- Moving workloads from on-premises environments to cloud resources
- Spinning up environments quickly for testing or temporary demand

**IaaS compared with other cloud models**

Cloud computing includes multiple service models, and IaaS is one of the foundational ones. It provides the infrastructure layer, while other models abstract more of the stack.

- **IaaS** gives you virtualized infrastructure.
- **PaaS** (Platform as a Service) adds a managed platform for building and running applications.
- **SaaS** (Software as a Service) delivers complete software applications over the internet.

A practical way to remember the difference is that IaaS gives you the building blocks, while higher-level models take on more of the management burden.

**Key exam takeaways**

- IaaS means infrastructure delivered as a service.
- The provider handles the physical hardware.
- You manage the operating system, applications, and data you place on the infrastructure.
- The model is designed for flexibility, scaling, and reduced hardware ownership.
