## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**Impact printer basics**

An **impact printer** creates output by physically striking an inked ribbon against paper. The most common example in A+ study material is the **dot-matrix printer**. This printer type uses a print head with pins to form characters and images as a pattern of dots.

Impact printing is older technology, but it still appears in environments that need legacy compatibility or multi-part forms. The output is not as clean as modern inkjet, laser, or thermal printing, but the mechanical process gives it a practical niche.

- **Dot-matrix** output is made from small dots arranged in a matrix.
- The print head moves across the page and strikes the ribbon to mark the paper.
- Multiple copies can be produced at the same time when carbon paper or multi-part forms are used.
- The printed result is usually lower resolution than newer printer types.

**Why organizations still use them**

Impact printers are valued for specific operational needs rather than print quality. They are often chosen when a business needs to print on multi-page forms, continuous paper, or other legacy media. The evidence also points to their relatively low cost per page because the ribbon supplies the ink used for printing.

These printers are not ideal for every setting. The striking action creates noticeable noise, and the output quality is limited compared with more modern printer technologies.

- **Low cost** is a major advantage.
- **Multi-part forms** are a common use case.
- **Continuous paper** and tractor-feed media are associated with dot-matrix printing.
- **Noise** is a common drawback because the printer physically impacts the page.

**Common dot-matrix components**

A dot-matrix printer relies on a few recognizable parts. The print head contains the pins that create the dot pattern. The ribbon provides the ink, and tractor-feed paper helps move continuous forms through the printer.

<dl>
<dt>Print head</dt>
<dd>The moving assembly that contains the pins used to strike the ribbon.</dd>
<dt>Ribbon</dt>
<dd>The inked material that transfers the mark to the paper.</dd>
<dt>Tractor-feed paper</dt>
<dd>Continuous paper designed to move through the printer using feed holes.</dd>
</dl>

**What to remember for exam prep**

When you see a question about impact printing, focus on the physical striking process and the use of dot patterns. If the scenario mentions carbon copies, multi-part forms, or continuous paper, an impact printer is a strong match.

> Impact printers trade print quality for specialized form handling and low-cost operation.

**Quick recognition cues**

- **Impact printing** means the printer strikes through a ribbon.
- **Dot-matrix** printers are the most common impact printer type in the evidence.
- **Multiple copies** and **carbon paper** point toward impact printing.
- **Loud operation** is a practical downside you should expect.
