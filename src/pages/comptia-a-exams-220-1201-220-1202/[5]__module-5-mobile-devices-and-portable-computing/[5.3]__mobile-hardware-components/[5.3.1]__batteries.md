## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**Battery basics**

Mobile devices, laptops, and tablets depend on batteries for portable power. Over time, battery capacity degrades with repeated charging, so a battery that once held a charge well may no longer perform the same way. In many cases, the practical fix is to replace the battery.

- **Battery wear** is normal and cumulative.
- A battery that no longer maintains charge may need replacement.
- Poor battery life is not always caused by a defective battery.

**Common causes of rapid drain**

A device can lose power quickly even when the battery itself is still usable. One common cause is weak cellular reception. When a phone is just outside network range, it keeps searching for a signal, which consumes power.

- **Poor reception** can cause constant reconnect attempts.
- Airplane mode can reduce unnecessary radio activity when you do not need connectivity.
- Background activity and other device usage can also increase drain.

**Charging and power-on troubleshooting**

When a device will not charge or turn on, start with the simplest checks. Verify the charger, cable, power source, and port before assuming the battery has failed. A damaged, unsupported, or loose charging accessory can prevent charging or make it very slow.

1. Hold the power button for 5-7 seconds to try a restart.
2. Check that the cable is firmly connected to the adapter and device.
3. Inspect the charging port for dust or lint.
4. Try a different compatible cable or power adapter.
5. Test wireless charging if the device supports it.

> Use approved or compatible charging accessories. Unsupported chargers or cables can cause slow charging or no charging at all.

**Symptoms that point to battery or charging issues**

Battery problems often show up as fast drain, failure to charge past a certain point, or a device that turns off unexpectedly. Some devices may also charge poorly when connected to a PC or dock.

- **Worn cables** can mimic battery failure.
- A device that drains quickly and does not charge normally may need a battery or accessory check.
- Charging behavior can differ between wired, wireless, PC, and dock connections.

**Troubleshooting approach**

Use a practical, step-by-step process so you can separate battery wear from charging-path problems.

1. Confirm the symptom: no charge, slow charge, rapid drain, or power loss.
2. Check the charger, cable, and outlet or power source.
3. Inspect the port for debris or damage.
4. Test with known-good compatible accessories.
5. If the battery still does not hold charge, consider replacement.

**Exam focus**

For A+ troubleshooting questions, look for clues that distinguish battery aging from external causes. A battery that has been recharged many times may simply be worn out, while a device in a weak signal area may drain quickly because it keeps searching for connectivity.

- **Age and wear** suggest replacement.
- **Signal searching** suggests a usage-related drain.
- **Accessory problems** suggest checking the charger, cable, or port first.
