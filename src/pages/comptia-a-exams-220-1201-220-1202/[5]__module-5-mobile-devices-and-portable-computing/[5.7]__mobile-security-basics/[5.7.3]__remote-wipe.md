## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**Remote wipe overview**

Remote wipe is a mobile device security feature that lets you erase data on a device you no longer control. It is used when a phone or tablet is lost or stolen, especially when the device contains sensitive personal or company data.

The goal is simple: reduce the chance that someone can access stored information after the device leaves your possession. In exam scenarios, remote wipe is often the best answer when the question asks how to protect data on a misplaced device.

- Use **remote wipe** to remove data from a lost or stolen mobile device.
- It is a data-loss response, not a recovery feature.
- It is commonly paired with other protections such as screen locks and encryption.

**When remote wipe is appropriate**

Remote wipe is most useful after a device is missing and you cannot physically secure it. Evidence from mobile security guidance shows that modern platforms support remote finding, securing, and erasing features, which makes remote wipe a practical last step when recovery is unlikely.

You should think of it as a containment measure. If the device may still be reachable over a network, remote wipe can help prevent further exposure of stored data.

- Best for **lost** devices.
- Best for **stolen** devices.
- Useful when the device still has network access and can receive the wipe command.

> Remote wipe depends on the device being reachable. If the device is powered off or offline, the erase action may not complete until it reconnects.

**How it fits with other mobile security controls**

Remote wipe works best as part of a layered approach. The evidence highlights screen locks, full device encryption, and passcodes or PINs as related protections. These controls reduce risk before a device is lost, while remote wipe helps after loss occurs.

Encryption is especially important because it protects stored data even if the device cannot be wiped immediately. A passcode, PIN, pattern, or password also helps support device security and may be required for some remote management features.

- **Screen locks** limit casual access.
- **Encryption** protects data at rest.
- **Remote wipe** removes data after loss or theft.
- **Passcodes/PINs** help secure the device and support remote security features.

**Platform examples**

The evidence shows that Android and Apple ecosystems both support remote security actions. Android guidance describes the ability to find, secure, or erase a device remotely through account-based services. Institutional guidance for iOS also describes enabling remote wipe through device-finding services.

This means the concept is platform-agnostic for exam purposes: the exact menu path may differ, but the security objective is the same.

- Android devices can be found, secured, or erased remotely.
- iOS and iPadOS devices can also be configured for remote wipe.
- The feature usually requires the device to be signed in, online, and managed through the vendor’s service.

**Exam focus**

For CompTIA A+, remote wipe is the correct choice when the question asks how to protect data on a misplaced or stolen mobile device. If the question emphasizes preventing unauthorized access after loss, remote wipe is usually the strongest answer.

If the question instead asks about protecting data while the device is still in use, consider encryption or screen locks first. Remote wipe is a response action, not a day-to-day access control.

- Choose **remote wipe** for lost or stolen devices.
- Choose **encryption** for protecting data stored on the device.
- Choose **screen locks** for preventing unauthorized local access.

**Practical workflow**

1. Confirm the device is lost or stolen.
2. Try to locate or secure it through the vendor’s device-finding service.
3. If recovery is unlikely, issue the remote wipe command.
4. Treat the device as compromised and review any data that may have been exposed.

**Key takeaway**

Remote wipe is a mobile security safeguard that erases data from a device you no longer control. On the exam, it is the best answer for protecting data on a misplaced or stolen phone or tablet.
