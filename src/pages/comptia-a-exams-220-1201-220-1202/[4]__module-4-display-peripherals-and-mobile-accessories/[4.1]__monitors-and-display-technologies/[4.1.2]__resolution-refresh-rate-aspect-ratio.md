## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**Display basics**

When you evaluate a monitor, focus on how the display will be used. Different tasks benefit from different display specifications, such as gaming, video playback, presentations, or general office work. The main specs in this topic are **resolution**, **refresh rate**, and **aspect ratio**.

- **Resolution** is the total number of pixels on the screen, usually written as horizontal by vertical pixels, such as `1920x1080`.
- Higher resolution generally gives you more detail and sharper images.
- **Refresh rate** is how many times per second the display updates, and it is measured in hertz (`Hz`).
- **Aspect ratio** describes the relationship between width and height, such as `4:3`, `16:9`, or `21:9`.
- The aspect ratio affects the shape of the display and how content scales.

**Resolution**

Resolution tells you how much visual information the display can show at once. A screen with more pixels can render finer detail, which is why higher-resolution displays usually look sharper.

<dl>
<dt>Example</dt>
<dd>`1920x1080` means 1920 pixels across and 1080 pixels down.</dd>
</dl>

- Use resolution to compare the amount of detail different displays can present.
- Match the display resolution to the task so the image is clear enough for the content you view.
- Remember that resolution is separate from refresh rate; a display can have a high resolution without having a high refresh rate.

**Refresh rate**

Refresh rate describes how often the screen updates each second. It is commonly expressed in `Hz`, which means cycles per second. Older cathode ray tube (CRT) displays refreshed the image by redrawing it repeatedly, and the term remains in use for modern displays.

> A low refresh rate can cause visible flicker, especially on larger displays.

- Higher refresh rates generally produce smoother motion.
- Refresh rate matters when you want a display that feels stable during fast movement or frequent screen changes.
- On Windows, you can change the refresh rate in display settings when the monitor and graphics hardware support it.

**Aspect ratio**

Aspect ratio is the proportional relationship between width and height. It helps define the physical shape of the display and influences how content fits on the screen.

- `4:3` is a more square-shaped format.
- `16:9` is common for widescreen displays.
- `21:9` is an ultrawide format.
- Choose an aspect ratio that matches the content and the way you want the screen to present it.

**How the specs work together**

Resolution, refresh rate, and aspect ratio are related but not the same. You need to consider all three when selecting a display.

- Resolution affects detail.
- Refresh rate affects how often the image updates.
- Aspect ratio affects screen shape and content fit.
- A display should support the resolution you need at a refresh rate that works well for the task.

**Practical selection tips**

When you choose a monitor, start with the use case and then compare the specifications.

1. Identify the primary task, such as gaming, presentations, or general productivity.
2. Check the resolution needed for the amount of detail you want.
3. Verify the refresh rate for smoothness and flicker reduction.
4. Confirm the aspect ratio matches the content and workspace.
5. Make sure the display and system can support the chosen settings.

**Troubleshooting reminders**

If a display looks wrong, check whether the resolution or refresh rate is set incorrectly. A monitor may appear blurry, flicker, or fail to show the expected image if the settings do not match the display’s capabilities.

- Verify the cable and power connection first if the screen is blank or shows no signal.
- Confirm the monitor is set to a supported resolution.
- Check the refresh rate if you see flickering or unstable output.
- Update display settings when the image does not match the expected shape or clarity.
