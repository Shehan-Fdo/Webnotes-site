## **Course:** CompTIA A+ (Exams 220-1201 & 220-1202)

**Recovery and reset overview**

Recovery and reset are last-resort options when a Chromebook’s operating system (OS) does not work properly. Recovery removes and reinstalls the OS, while a factory reset is a less invasive step to try first if you can still sign in.

- **Recovery** reinstalls the OS when normal troubleshooting does not fix the problem.
- **Factory reset** is a step to try before recovery if the device still signs in.
- Recovery can be performed with an internet connection on newer Chromebooks.
- Older Chromebooks may not support updates, so recovery may not be possible.

> Recovery permanently erases everything on the Chromebook’s hard drive, including downloaded files.

**When to use recovery**

Use recovery when the device shows a serious OS failure or when other troubleshooting steps fail. A common indicator is the message `ChromeOS is missing or damaged`. Recovery is also appropriate when recommended by support resources after other fixes do not work.

- The OS is missing or damaged.
- Other troubleshooting steps have failed.
- Expert guidance recommends recovery.

Before you recover, back up any files you can still access. If the Chromebook is used for work or school, involve the administrator before proceeding.

**Recovery mode basics**

Recovery mode is the state a Chromebook enters when something goes wrong, and you can also access it manually. Its main purpose is to reload the OS and any modified RW (read-write) firmware components. It is also the mechanism used to switch a device from Normal Mode to Developer Mode.

- Recovery mode can start automatically after a failure.
- You can enter it manually on Chromebooks with `ESC` + `Refresh` + `Power`.
- On some convertible devices, the upper-right key may be a screen lock key rather than a power key.

**Practical recovery workflow**

1. Turn the Chromebook off and back on again.
2. If the error remains, try a factory reset.
3. If the problem still persists, proceed with recovery.
4. Back up any recoverable files before recovery starts.
5. Follow the recovery process for the device model and support guidance.

**Key exam takeaways**

Recovery is not the same as a simple restart. It is a reinstall process that can restore a broken OS, but it also destroys local data. For exam purposes, focus on the order of escalation: restart first, factory reset next, and recovery only when the issue remains unresolved.

- **Restart** is the least invasive step.
- **Factory reset** is the next step if sign-in is still possible.
- **Recovery** is the most destructive option and should be used carefully.
- Manual recovery mode access uses a key combination, not a software menu.
