## **Course:** CISSP Complete Course (Exam ISC2 CISSP – 8 Domains)

**How to use this practice exam**

The CISSP exam tests judgment, not memorization. You should answer from the perspective of a security manager who prioritizes business risk, policy, and least disruption while still protecting confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA). This practice set is designed to simulate that mindset with scenario-driven questions, answer choices, and explanations.

Use the exam in timed mode first, then review every explanation. For each item, ask:
- What is the question really asking?
- Which answer is the most complete and least disruptive?
- Which option addresses root cause, governance, or risk reduction rather than a narrow technical fix?

**Exam simulation guidance**

The real CISSP exam is adaptive and time-bound. A practical simulation should still train pacing, endurance, and decision quality.

- Target a steady pace of about 1.5 minutes per question in a 100-question block.
- Build a 2-pass strategy:
  - Pass 1: answer confident questions immediately.
  - Pass 2: return to marked questions and eliminate distractors.
- Practice under realistic conditions:
  - No notes.
  - No internet.
  - One sitting.
  - Minimal interruptions.
- Review missed questions by domain and by failure mode:
  - Knowledge gap
  - Misread scenario
  - Wrong priority
  - Overly technical answer
  - Ignored governance or policy

> The best CISSP answer is often the one that is correct at the organizational level, not the one that is most technically impressive.

**Question distribution and domain balance**

CISSP questions are distributed across the eight domains, but the exam does not publish a fixed per-domain count. Your preparation should still cover all domains evenly because weak areas can appear in any adaptive sequence.

- Security and Risk Management
- Asset Security
- Security Architecture and Engineering
- Communication and Network Security
- Identity and Access Management
- Security Assessment and Testing
- Security Operations
- Software Development Security

A strong practice exam includes:
- Broad coverage across all domains
- Mixed difficulty
- Scenario-based wording
- Multiple plausible distractors
- Explanations that justify why the best answer wins

**Scoring and test-taking strategy**

The CISSP exam is pass/fail, and the exact scoring model is not exposed in a simple percentage format. That means your strategy should focus on consistency and risk reduction rather than chasing a numeric target.

- Eliminate answers that are:
  - Too narrow
  - Too expensive without justification
  - Too technical for a governance question
  - Reactive when a preventive control is better
- Prefer answers that:
  - Align with policy and process
  - Reduce risk sustainably
  - Preserve evidence and chain of custody when relevant
  - Escalate appropriately
  - Address root cause before symptoms
- Watch for keywords:
  - **Best**, **first**, **most appropriate**, **primary**, **initial**
- Treat absolutes carefully:
  - “Always,” “never,” and “guarantee” are often distractors unless the context truly supports them

**Full-length practice exam**

### **Question 1**
A company is preparing to launch a new customer portal that will store personal data from multiple regions, including the European Union. Which action should the security team prioritize first?

A. Deploy database encryption  
B. Identify applicable legal and regulatory requirements  
C. Implement multifactor authentication  
D. Purchase cyber liability insurance  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** You should first identify legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements because they drive the security and privacy controls that follow. For a portal handling personal data across regions, privacy obligations such as data residency, lawful processing, retention, and breach notification can shape architecture and operations. Encryption and MFA are important, but they come after requirements analysis.

**Question 2**
A data owner wants to dispose of old solid-state drives (SSDs) containing sensitive information. Which method is most appropriate?

A. Overwrite the drives once with zeros  
B. Use degaussing  
C. Physically destroy or use vendor-approved secure erase for SSDs  
D. Reformat the drives and return them to inventory  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** SSDs handle data differently from magnetic media. Degaussing is not effective for SSDs, and simple overwriting may not reliably remove all data because of wear leveling and remapped blocks. Secure erase tools approved by the vendor or physical destruction are appropriate depending on sensitivity and reuse requirements.

**Question 3**
A security architect is selecting a model for a system that must prevent users from reading data above their clearance level. Which model is most appropriate?

A. Biba  
B. Bell-LaPadula  
C. Clark-Wilson  
D. Brewer-Nash  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Bell-LaPadula focuses on confidentiality with rules such as “no read up” and “no write down.” Biba focuses on integrity, Clark-Wilson focuses on well-formed transactions and separation of duties, and Brewer-Nash addresses conflict of interest.

**Question 4**
Which OSI layer is responsible for routing between networks?

A. Data Link  
B. Network  
C. Transport  
D. Session  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The Network layer, Layer 3, handles logical addressing and routing. In TCP/IP terms, this maps primarily to the Internet layer.

**Question 5**
A user needs access to a cloud application using their corporate identity after authenticating to the company portal. Which federation standard is commonly used for browser-based single sign-on?

A. SFTP  
B. SAML  
C. SNMP  
D. RDP  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Security Assertion Markup Language (SAML) is widely used for federated identity and browser-based single sign-on. OAuth and OpenID Connect are also common in modern environments, but SAML remains a classic CISSP-relevant answer when enterprise federation is described.

**Question 6**
An organization wants to verify that a firewall rule set matches approved policy without changing the device. Which assessment method is best?

A. Penetration test  
B. Configuration review  
C. Vulnerability scan  
D. Red team exercise  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** A configuration review compares the implemented settings to the approved baseline or policy. A vulnerability scan looks for known weaknesses, a penetration test attempts exploitation, and a red team exercise simulates adversarial objectives.

**Question 7**
A ransomware incident has encrypted file servers. Which action should the incident response team take first?

A. Restore all systems immediately from backup  
B. Preserve volatile evidence and isolate affected systems  
C. Notify the media  
D. Reimage every endpoint in the organization  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The first priority is containment and evidence preservation. You isolate affected systems to stop spread and preserve volatile data for forensics. Restoration comes later, after containment and analysis.

**Question 8**
A development team wants to reduce security defects earlier in the lifecycle. Which practice best supports this goal?

A. Post-release penetration testing only  
B. DevSecOps with automated security checks in the CI/CD pipeline  
C. Annual policy review  
D. Manual code review after production deployment  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** DevSecOps integrates security into continuous integration and continuous delivery/deployment (CI/CD), shifting detection left. Automated static analysis, dependency scanning, and policy checks catch issues earlier and cheaper.

**Question 9**
A company allows employees to use personal phones for email. Which control best reduces the risk of corporate data loss if a phone is lost?

A. Screen savers  
B. Mobile device management with remote wipe  
C. DNS filtering  
D. Network segmentation only  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Mobile device management (MDM) can enforce encryption, screen lock, and remote wipe. This is especially important for bring your own device (BYOD) scenarios.

**Question 10**
Which control best supports accountability for privileged actions on a production server?

A. Shared administrator accounts  
B. Detailed logging tied to unique user IDs  
C. Disabling audit logs to improve performance  
D. Using only password complexity rules  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Unique IDs and detailed logs support accountability and nonrepudiation. Shared accounts weaken attribution and make investigations difficult.

**Question 11**
A company is deciding whether to accept, transfer, avoid, or mitigate a risk. Which activity is this?

A. Risk analysis  
B. Risk treatment  
C. Risk monitoring  
D. Risk identification  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Risk treatment is the selection of a response strategy such as mitigate, transfer, avoid, or accept. Identification discovers risks, analysis evaluates them, and monitoring tracks them over time.

**Question 12**
Which is the best example of a detective control?

A. Security awareness training  
B. Intrusion detection system alerts  
C. Data classification policy  
D. Network segmentation  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** An intrusion detection system (IDS) detects suspicious activity after or during occurrence. Training is administrative and preventive, policy is governance, and segmentation is preventive.

**Question 13**
A company needs to ensure that only approved changes reach production. Which process is most important?

A. Change management  
B. Asset disposal  
C. Data masking  
D. Threat hunting  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Change management controls, reviews, approves, tests, and documents changes before production deployment. It reduces outages and unauthorized modifications.

**Question 14**
A security analyst sees repeated login attempts from many IP addresses against one account. What is the most likely attack?

A. Password spraying  
B. Man-in-the-middle  
C. SQL injection  
D. ARP poisoning  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Password spraying uses a small number of common passwords across many accounts or attempts to avoid lockouts. Repeated attempts against one account can also indicate brute force, but the “many IPs” and low-and-slow pattern often point to spraying or distributed guessing depending on wording.

**Question 15**
Which protocol is commonly used to securely manage network devices?

A. Telnet  
B. SSH  
C. FTP  
D. HTTP  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Secure Shell (SSH) encrypts administrative sessions. Telnet and FTP are unencrypted and should be avoided for management.

**Question 16**
A company wants to ensure that a backup can be restored after a site disaster. Which test is most appropriate?

A. Tabletop exercise only  
B. Restore test  
C. Policy review  
D. Vulnerability scan  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** A restore test verifies that backups are usable and that recovery procedures work. Tabletop exercises validate planning and decision-making, but not actual data restoration.

**Question 17**
A developer asks how to protect against SQL injection. Which control is best?

A. Input validation and parameterized queries  
B. Increasing server RAM  
C. Using stronger Wi-Fi encryption  
D. Disabling backups  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Parameterized queries prevent user input from being interpreted as executable SQL. Input validation helps, but parameterization is the primary defense.

**Question 18**
Which security principle requires that users only have the access needed to perform their job?

A. Least privilege  
B. Separation of duties  
C. Defense in depth  
D. Fail secure  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Least privilege limits access to the minimum necessary. Separation of duties divides critical tasks among multiple people to reduce fraud and error.

**Question 19**
A company wants to reduce the risk of a single administrator abusing power. Which control is best?

A. Role-based access control  
B. Separation of duties  
C. Mandatory access control  
D. Single sign-on  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Separation of duties prevents one person from completing all critical steps alone. It is especially important for financial, change, and privileged operations.

**Question 20**
Which wireless security protocol is the strongest commonly deployed option for enterprise Wi-Fi today?

A. WEP  
B. WPA2-Personal  
C. WPA3-Enterprise  
D. Open authentication  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** WPA3-Enterprise is the strongest common enterprise option among these choices. WEP is obsolete, open authentication provides no protection, and WPA2-Personal is weaker than enterprise-grade 802.1X-based deployments.

**Question 21**
A company wants to verify that a control is operating as intended by examining evidence and records. What is this?

A. Audit  
B. Penetration test  
C. Data classification  
D. Threat modeling  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Audits evaluate compliance and control effectiveness through evidence review, interviews, and testing. Penetration tests focus on exploitation, while threat modeling is a design-time analysis.

**Question 22**
A security team needs to protect data while it is being transmitted over an untrusted network. Which control is most appropriate?

A. Full-disk encryption  
B. TLS  
C. Hashing only  
D. Data classification labels  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Transport Layer Security (TLS) protects data in transit. Full-disk encryption protects data at rest, and hashing alone does not provide confidentiality.

**Question 23**
Which term describes the unauthorized disclosure of information?

A. Integrity loss  
B. Availability loss  
C. Confidentiality breach  
D. Nonrepudiation failure  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Unauthorized disclosure is a confidentiality breach. Integrity concerns unauthorized modification, and availability concerns access disruption.

**Question 24**
A company wants to ensure that a user cannot deny having sent a message. Which control supports this most directly?

A. Nonrepudiation through digital signatures  
B. Data compression  
C. Network address translation  
D. RAID  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Digital signatures support nonrepudiation by binding the signer to the message with cryptographic proof. Logs and timestamps can help, but signatures are the classic answer.

**Question 25**
A security engineer is designing a system that must preserve integrity of financial transactions. Which model is most relevant?

A. Bell-LaPadula  
B. Biba  
C. Brewer-Nash  
D. Clark-Wilson  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:** Clark-Wilson is designed for commercial integrity through well-formed transactions, separation of duties, and auditing. Biba is also integrity-focused, but Clark-Wilson is the stronger fit for transaction processing.

**Question 26**
A company wants to prevent a user from accessing records belonging to a competitor after previously accessing another client’s records in the same conflict class. Which model applies?

A. Biba  
B. Brewer-Nash  
C. Bell-LaPadula  
D. DAC  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Brewer-Nash, also called the Chinese Wall model, prevents conflicts of interest by restricting access based on prior activity.

**Question 27**
Which physical control is most effective for preventing unauthorized entry into a server room?

A. Antivirus software  
B. Mantrap with badge access  
C. Password expiration policy  
D. Data loss prevention  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** A mantrap physically controls entry and exit, reducing tailgating and unauthorized access. Physical security is a core CISSP topic and often appears in architecture questions.

**Question 28**
A company is selecting a security framework to structure governance and control mapping. Which is the best general-purpose choice?

A. NIST Cybersecurity Framework  
B. SMTP  
C. RAID 10  
D. OAuth 2.0  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** The NIST Cybersecurity Framework helps organize security governance around identify, protect, detect, respond, and recover. The other options are not governance frameworks.

**Question 29**
Which is the best example of a preventive administrative control?

A. Security policy  
B. IDS alert  
C. CCTV recording  
D. Log review after an incident  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** A policy sets expectations and rules before an event occurs. It is administrative and preventive.

**Question 30**
A company wants to reduce the risk of malware spreading through email attachments. Which control is most effective?

A. Email filtering and sandboxing  
B. Increasing monitor size  
C. Using static IP addresses  
D. Disabling user training  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Email filtering blocks malicious content and sandboxing detonates attachments in a controlled environment. User awareness also helps, but technical filtering is the primary control.

**Question 31**
Which port is commonly associated with HTTPS?

A. 21  
B. 22  
C. 80  
D. 443  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:** HTTPS uses TCP port 443. HTTP uses 80, SSH uses 22, and FTP uses 21.

**Question 32**
A company needs to ensure that only authorized devices can connect to the internal network. Which technology is most appropriate?

A. 802.1X  
B. NAT  
C. DHCP  
D. DNS  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** 802.1X provides port-based network access control and is commonly used with RADIUS for authentication before network access is granted.

**Question 33**
A security team wants to reduce the impact of a compromised workstation reaching sensitive servers. Which design is best?

A. Flat network  
B. Network segmentation  
C. Larger subnets  
D. Disabling firewalls  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Segmentation limits lateral movement and contains compromise. It is a core defense-in-depth measure.

**Question 34**
A company is implementing identity proofing for remote onboarding. What is the main purpose of identity proofing?

A. To encrypt user passwords  
B. To verify that a person is who they claim to be before account issuance  
C. To rotate access tokens every hour  
D. To disable inactive accounts  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Identity proofing establishes confidence in a claimed identity before credentials are issued. It is important in joiner processes and remote verification.

**Question 35**
Which process best describes removing access when an employee leaves the company?

A. Joiner process  
B. Mover process  
C. Leaver process  
D. Federation process  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The joiner-mover-leaver lifecycle governs identity changes. Leaver processes revoke access promptly to reduce orphaned accounts and insider risk.

**Question 36**
A manager wants quarterly review of privileged accounts to ensure access remains appropriate. What is this called?

A. Access recertification  
B. Tokenization  
C. Degaussing  
D. Baseline imaging  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Access recertification, also called access review, confirms that permissions remain valid and aligned with job duties.

**Question 37**
Which control is most appropriate for managing highly privileged accounts?

A. Shared root password in a spreadsheet  
B. Privileged access management (PAM)  
C. Guest Wi-Fi isolation  
D. Screen resolution policy  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** PAM vaults credentials, brokers sessions, records activity, and reduces standing privilege. It is a strong CISSP answer for administrative control of elevated access.

**Question 38**
A company uses SSO and wants users to authenticate once to access multiple services. Which concept is this?

A. Federation  
B. Hashing  
C. Tokenization  
D. Obfuscation  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Federation allows trust between identity providers and service providers, enabling single sign-on across domains.

**Question 39**
A security team wants to test whether a control is designed correctly before implementation. What should they do?

A. Design review  
B. Post-incident review  
C. Asset inventory  
D. Backup rotation  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** A design review evaluates whether the proposed control meets requirements before deployment. This is different from operational testing after implementation.

**Question 40**
Which is the best example of a corrective control?

A. Patch management after a vulnerability is identified  
B. Security guard at the door  
C. Firewall rule blocking traffic  
D. Security policy document  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Corrective controls fix or reduce the impact of an issue after detection. Patch management corrects known vulnerabilities.

**Question 41**
A company needs to protect data stored on laptops. Which control is most appropriate?

A. Data at rest encryption  
B. DNSSEC  
C. Port mirroring  
D. NAT  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Full-disk or file-level encryption protects data at rest on portable devices. This is especially important for lost or stolen laptops.

**Question 42**
Which media sanitization method is most appropriate for a hard disk drive that will be reused internally?

A. Degaussing or secure erase depending on media and policy  
B. Rebooting the system  
C. Changing the file name  
D. Compressing the data  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** For magnetic hard drives, degaussing can be effective, though it destroys usability. Secure erase or overwrite may be used depending on policy and assurance needs. Reuse requirements and media type determine the method.

**Question 43**
A company wants to ensure that backups are protected from ransomware. Which strategy is best?

A. Keep all backups online for easy access  
B. Use immutable or offline backups  
C. Store backups on the same server  
D. Disable backup encryption  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Immutable or offline backups reduce the chance that ransomware can encrypt or delete them. This is a key recovery control.

**Question 44**
Which disaster recovery metric measures the maximum acceptable data loss in time?

A. RTO  
B. RPO  
C. MTBF  
D. SLA  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Recovery Point Objective (RPO) defines how much data loss is acceptable, measured in time. Recovery Time Objective (RTO) defines how quickly systems must be restored.

**Question 45**
A business wants to know how long a system can be unavailable before causing unacceptable harm. Which metric is this?

A. RTO  
B. RPO  
C. ALE  
D. SLE  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Recovery Time Objective (RTO) is the maximum tolerable downtime. It helps determine recovery architecture and priorities.

**Question 46**
A security analyst is investigating a suspected breach. Which action best preserves evidence?

A. Reboot the system immediately  
B. Document chain of custody and collect volatile data first  
C. Delete suspicious files  
D. Change all passwords before imaging  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Forensics requires careful evidence handling. Volatile data such as memory and active connections should be captured before shutdown when appropriate, and chain of custody must be maintained.

**Question 47**
Which is the best example of a technical detective control?

A. Security awareness training  
B. File integrity monitoring  
C. Acceptable use policy  
D. Background checks  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** File integrity monitoring detects unauthorized changes to critical files. It is technical and detective.

**Question 48**
A company wants to verify that a web application is vulnerable to known issues without exploiting them. Which test is most appropriate?

A. Vulnerability scan  
B. Red team exercise  
C. Social engineering test  
D. Disaster recovery drill  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Vulnerability scanning identifies known weaknesses non-destructively. Penetration testing goes further by attempting exploitation.

**Question 49**
A security manager wants to understand how a new system could be attacked before deployment. Which activity is best?

A. Threat modeling  
B. Log archiving  
C. Asset disposal  
D. Backup restoration  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Threat modeling identifies assets, trust boundaries, threats, and mitigations during design. It is a proactive architecture activity.

**Question 50**
A company uses a third-party payroll provider. What is the most important security concern?

A. Vendor risk management and contractual controls  
B. Screen brightness settings  
C. Printer driver updates only  
D. Physical desk layout  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Third-party risk includes data handling, access, breach notification, audit rights, service levels, and compliance obligations. Contracts and due diligence are essential.

**Question 51**
Which control best reduces the risk of a vendor compromise affecting your environment?

A. Trust all vendor accounts permanently  
B. Limit vendor access, monitor sessions, and use least privilege  
C. Share admin credentials by email  
D. Disable logging for vendor sessions  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Vendor access should be tightly scoped, time-bound, and monitored. This is a common scenario in third-party risk questions.

**Question 52**
A company wants to ensure that security requirements are built into software from the start. Which approach is best?

A. Secure SDLC  
B. End-user training only  
C. Annual password reset  
D. Network address translation  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** A secure software development life cycle (SDLC) integrates security activities such as requirements, design review, code review, testing, and release gates.

**Question 53**
Which testing method is best for finding coding flaws before deployment by analyzing source code?

A. Static application security testing (SAST)  
B. Dynamic application security testing (DAST)  
C. Penetration testing only  
D. User acceptance testing  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** SAST analyzes source or compiled code without executing it. DAST tests a running application from the outside.

**Question 54**
A company wants to test a running web application for vulnerabilities from an attacker’s perspective. Which method is best?

A. SAST  
B. DAST  
C. Code signing  
D. Threat modeling  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** DAST evaluates the application in execution and can reveal runtime issues such as authentication flaws and injection vulnerabilities.

**Question 55**
A security team needs to ensure that software dependencies are trustworthy. Which concern is most relevant?

A. Supply chain risk  
B. Screen lock timeout  
C. DNS caching  
D. VLAN tagging only  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Supply chain risk includes compromised libraries, build systems, package repositories, and third-party components. This is increasingly important in software assurance.

**Question 56**
Which is the best example of a compensating control?

B. A policy that has never been implemented  
C. A vulnerability scan with no remediation  
D. A backup that cannot be restored  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** A compensating control provides equivalent risk reduction when the primary control is not feasible. It should be documented and justified.

**Question 57**
A company wants to classify data based on business impact and handling requirements. What should it create first?

A. Data classification policy  
B. Firewall rule base  
C. Incident response playbook  
D. Patch schedule  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** A classification policy defines categories such as public, internal, confidential, and restricted, along with handling rules. This drives labeling, retention, and disposal.

**Question 58**
Which data state is protected by TLS during a web session?

A. At rest  
B. In transit  
C. In use  
D. Archived  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** TLS protects data in transit between endpoints. Data in use refers to data being processed in memory or by the CPU.

**Question 59**
A company wants to reduce the risk of sensitive data lingering on reused media. What is this risk called?

A. Data remanence  
B. Token replay  
C. Side-channel leakage  
D. Session fixation  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Data remanence is residual data that remains after deletion or sanitization. It is a key concern in media disposal and reuse.

**Question 60**
Which control best protects data in use in a highly sensitive environment?

A. Full-disk encryption alone  
B. Trusted execution environment or secure enclave, where appropriate  
C. DNS filtering  
D. Port security  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Data in use is harder to protect than data at rest or in transit. Hardware-backed isolation such as secure enclaves can help in specific use cases, though the exact control depends on the environment.

**Question 61**
A company wants to ensure that only approved software runs on endpoints. Which control is best?

A. Application allowlisting  
B. Password reuse  
C. Open Wi-Fi  
D. NAT traversal  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Allowlisting permits only approved executables and scripts, reducing malware risk and unauthorized software execution.

**Question 62**
Which security principle is most closely associated with minimizing the impact of a compromise by dividing systems into smaller trust zones?

A. Segmentation  
B. Obfuscation  
C. Tokenization  
D. Compression  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Segmentation limits blast radius and supports defense in depth. It is used in networks, applications, and data environments.

**Question 63**
A company wants to ensure that a control is operating continuously over time. Which activity is best?

A. Monitoring  
B. Asset disposal  
C. Data labeling  
D. Initial risk assessment only  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Monitoring provides ongoing visibility into control performance and security events. It is essential for detection and assurance.

**High-yield exam patterns**

- If the question is about governance, choose policy, risk, or management actions before technical fixes.
- If the question is about an incident, contain and preserve evidence before eradication and recovery.
- If the question is about access, think lifecycle:
  - Joiner
  - Mover
  - Leaver
  - Recertification
  - Privileged access management
- If the question is about data, identify the state:
  - At rest
  - In transit
  - In use
- If the question is about testing, match the method to the goal:
  - Review for design or configuration
  - Scan for known weaknesses
  - Test for exploitability
  - Exercise for process readiness
- If the question is about architecture, consider segmentation, trust boundaries, and defense in depth.

**Mini-scenarios for judgment practice**

- A finance team wants one person to create and approve payments. The best answer is to enforce separation of duties and dual approval, not simply add logging.
- A hospital needs to share patient data with a specialist network. The best answer is to verify legal and privacy requirements first, then design the federation or data-sharing controls.
- A developer wants to store API keys in source code for convenience. The best answer is to use a secrets manager and rotate credentials, not rely on obscurity.
- A remote worker loses a laptop with encrypted storage. The best answer is that encryption reduces exposure, but you still assess whether keys, cached data, or session tokens were present.

**Common traps in CISSP-style questions**

- Choosing a technical fix when the question asks for the first managerial step
- Selecting detection when prevention is the better answer
- Ignoring legal, regulatory, or contractual obligations
- Confusing RTO with RPO
- Mixing up confidentiality and integrity models
- Treating vendor controls as fully outsourced
- Forgetting that evidence preservation matters in incident response
- Overlooking the difference between policy, standard, procedure, and guideline

**Final exam approach**

1. Read the last line of the question first when the stem is long.
2. Identify the domain and the control type.
3. Eliminate answers that are too narrow or too disruptive.
4. Choose the answer that best fits the scenario’s priority.
5. Mark uncertain questions and return with fresh context.

A strong CISSP candidate demonstrates consistent judgment across governance, architecture, operations, and development. The goal is not to memorize isolated facts; it is to recognize the most appropriate action in a business context.
