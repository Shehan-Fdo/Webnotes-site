## **Course:** CompTIA Security+ (Exam SY0-701)

**Identity lifecycle management overview**

Identity lifecycle management controls how digital identities are created, updated, and removed as people join, move within, or leave an organization. It is a core part of identity governance and administration because access should match a user’s current role and employment status.

The Joiner-Mover-Leaver (JML) model is the common way to describe this lifecycle. It helps ensure that users get the right access at the right time and that access is removed when it is no longer needed.

- **Joiner**: a new identity enters the organization, such as an employee, contractor, or partner.
- **Mover**: the person changes roles, departments, or projects.
- **Leaver**: the person leaves the organization and access should be revoked.

**Why lifecycle management matters**

Manual account handling creates delays and risk. New users may wait for access, role changes may leave users with too many privileges, and departed users may keep orphaned accounts that create audit findings.

Automating the lifecycle improves consistency and reduces administrative burden. It also supports compliance by creating a clear record of account changes and access decisions.

- **Day-1 access**: new users can be productive immediately.
- **Role updates**: permissions change when responsibilities change.
- **Revocation**: access is removed promptly at termination.
- **Audit evidence**: changes are easier to track and review.

**Joiner, mover, and leaver stages**

Each stage has a different access goal, but the same principle applies: align access with current need.

| Stage | What happens | Typical access outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Joiner | A person is onboarded into the organization | Accounts are provisioned and appropriate roles are assigned |
| Mover | A person changes job function or assignment | Access is adjusted to match the new role |
| Leaver | A person departs the organization | Accounts and access are removed or disabled |

A practical JML process often uses automated provisioning and deprovisioning. Systems can create, modify, or delete accounts based on changes in identity attributes, which reduces manual requests and helps maintain data integrity.

**Provisioning and deprovisioning**

Provisioning is the creation and assignment of access for a new identity. Deprovisioning is the removal of access when it is no longer required. In lifecycle management, both actions should be tied to identity events rather than handled as one-time tasks.

Automation is especially useful when identities change roles. Instead of leaving old permissions in place, the system can update access dynamically so the user keeps only what is needed.

> A common risk is privilege accumulation during role changes. If you do not remove old access, movers can retain permissions that no longer match their job.

**Operational benefits**

Lifecycle management supports both security and efficiency. It reduces the amount of manual work for administrators and helps keep access aligned with business needs.

- **Security**: limits unnecessary access and reduces exposure.
- **Efficiency**: lowers the workload for IT and service desk teams.
- **Compliance**: supports auditability and access control requirements.
- **Consistency**: applies the same rules across onboarding, transfers, and offboarding.

**Exam focus points**

For Security+ purposes, focus on the relationship between identity status and access control. The key idea is that access should change as the user’s relationship with the organization changes.

- Joiners need timely account creation and role-based access.
- Movers need access adjustments, not just new access added.
- Leavers need immediate removal of access to prevent lingering accounts.
- Automation improves accuracy and reduces administrative overhead.

**Quick review**

Identity lifecycle management is the process of managing identities from creation through retirement. The JML model organizes that process into joiner, mover, and leaver events. When you automate these stages, you improve security, reduce delays, and keep access aligned with current responsibilities.
