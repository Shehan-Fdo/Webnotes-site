## **Course:** CompTIA Security+ (Exam SY0-701)

**Post-incident review purpose**

A post-incident review is a structured debrief that evaluates how an incident was handled and what outcomes followed. It helps you understand what worked, what failed, and what should change before the next event.

In security operations, this review is part of the incident response process and supports continuous improvement. It also helps teams strengthen resilience by learning from disruption instead of repeating the same mistakes.

- **Structured debrief**: review the incident from start to finish.
- **Effectiveness check**: assess the response actions and their results.
- **Improvement focus**: identify lessons that can improve future handling.

**What you evaluate**

A good review looks at the incident itself, the response, and the impact on people, processes, and technology. The goal is to understand the full scope of the event and the root cause where possible.

You also look for weaknesses that were exposed or manipulated during the incident. This can include vulnerabilities, internal process gaps, and indicators tied to the attack.

- **Root cause**: determine why the incident happened.
- **Scope**: identify what systems, teams, or processes were affected.
- **Weaknesses**: note vulnerabilities and control failures.
- **Indicators**: capture relevant indicators of compromise and attacker techniques when available.

**After Action Report (AAR)**

An <abbr title="After Action Report">AAR</abbr> is the formal document that records the review. It captures the incident summary, response actions, decision-making, performance, and recommendations for improvement.

Use the AAR to document what happened and to support future incident management. It is useful after both real incidents and exercises because it turns observations into actionable changes.

- **Objective record**: document the response without relying on memory.
- **Actionable output**: include recommendations, not just observations.
- **Future value**: use the report to improve readiness and response capability.

**How the review supports improvement**

Post-incident reviews help you validate incident response objectives and identify gaps in communication, coordination, and documentation. They also help teams update procedures when the event reveals missing steps or unclear responsibilities.

The review should lead to practical changes. If the team finds a recurring weakness, the response plan, playbooks, or training should reflect that lesson.

1. Conduct the review with the team after the incident or exercise.
2. Document the findings in an AAR or similar template.
3. Share the results with the coordinating organization or stakeholders.
4. Update documentation, procedures, and training based on the findings.

**Key takeaways for Security+**

For the exam, focus on the purpose of the review and the value of the AAR. The main idea is simple: incident handling does not end when recovery is complete.

A strong post-incident process helps you:
- learn from each event,
- identify gaps in response,
- improve documentation and procedures,
- and reduce the chance of repeating the same failure.

> A post-incident review is not just a summary of what happened; it is a tool for measurable improvement.
