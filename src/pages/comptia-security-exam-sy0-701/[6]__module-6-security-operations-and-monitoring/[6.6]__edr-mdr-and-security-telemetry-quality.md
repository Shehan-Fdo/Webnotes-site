## **Course:** CompTIA Security+ (Exam SY0-701)

**EDR and MDR overview**

Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) focuses on endpoint visibility, threat detection, and response. Managed Detection and Response (MDR) adds a managed service layer, where security expertise helps operate detection and response on your behalf.

- **EDR** emphasizes the endpoint itself, including devices such as desktops, laptops, tablets, and mobile devices.
- **MDR** emphasizes service delivery, combining detection and response with external management and expertise.
- Both support a stronger security posture by improving how you detect and react to suspicious activity.

EDR is useful when you want direct control over endpoint monitoring and response actions. MDR is useful when you need broader operational support and do not want to run the full detection workflow internally.

**How EDR works**

EDR tools collect endpoint activity and correlate events so you can identify suspicious behavior faster. They are designed to help you visualize risk, monitor continuously, and respond with manual or automated actions.

- **Real-time monitoring** helps you see activity as it happens.
- **Data collection** supports later review and investigation.
- **Correlation** connects related events into a clearer incident picture.
- **Response options** may be manual, automated, or both.

EDR is especially relevant because endpoints are common attack targets. Security teams use EDR to watch for malicious software, unusual outbound activity, and signs that an attacker is trying to move through a device.

**How MDR differs from EDR**

MDR is not just a tool; it is a managed security capability. The main difference is who operates the detection and response process.

| Aspect | EDR | MDR |
|---|---|---|
| Primary focus | Endpoint technology | Managed security service |
| Operating model | You manage it | A provider helps manage it |
| Main value | Visibility and response at the endpoint | Expertise, scale, and operational support |
| Best fit | Teams with internal security operations | Teams needing outsourced detection and response support |

Use EDR when you want endpoint-level control and have the staff to monitor and act on alerts. Use MDR when you need help handling alerts, investigations, and response activities.

**Security telemetry quality**

Telemetry is the data you collect from systems, endpoints, logs, and monitoring points. Good telemetry improves detection, alerting, and investigation because it gives you enough context to understand what happened.

- **Coverage** matters because you need data from the right systems and endpoints.
- **Timeliness** matters because real-time or near-real-time data supports faster response.
- **Consistency** matters because dashboards and correlation depend on reliable inputs.
- **Context** matters because isolated events are harder to interpret than linked activity.

Poor telemetry can create blind spots, weak alerts, and slower investigations. Strong telemetry supports dashboards, log aggregation, and better security monitoring overall.

**Monitoring and telemetry sources**

Security monitoring relies on multiple data sources, not just endpoint alerts. You should watch for activity across systems and services so you can verify whether behavior is legitimate.

- Authentication and login activity
- Remote access usage
- Firewall rule activity
- System and endpoint events
- Scanning and alerting outputs

These sources help you build a more complete view of your security posture. When you roll them into dashboards, you can quickly see whether something needs attention.

**Choosing between EDR and MDR**

Your choice depends on your internal capability, visibility needs, and response expectations.

1. Assess whether you have staff to monitor alerts and investigate incidents.
2. Decide whether you need direct endpoint control or managed support.
3. Confirm that your telemetry sources are complete enough to support detection.
4. Choose the model that matches your operational maturity and response speed needs.

If you already have a capable security team, EDR may fit well. If you need outside expertise and continuous operational coverage, MDR is often the more practical choice.

**Exam focus**

For Security+ purposes, connect these ideas:

- **EDR** is endpoint-focused detection and response.
- **MDR** is a managed service for detection and response.
- **Telemetry quality** affects how well monitoring, alerting, and investigation work.
- **Dashboards** and log aggregation help you interpret security posture quickly.
- **Endpoints** are high-value targets and should be monitored closely.
