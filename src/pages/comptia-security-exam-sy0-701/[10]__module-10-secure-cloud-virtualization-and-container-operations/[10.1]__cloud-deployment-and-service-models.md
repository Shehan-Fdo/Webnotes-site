## **Course:** CompTIA Security+ (Exam SY0-701)

**Cloud deployment models**

A cloud deployment model describes where the cloud infrastructure lives, who owns and manages it, and how users access it. It also shapes how much you can customize, how resources are shared, and what trade-offs you make in governance, cost, security, and management.

For Security+ purposes, focus on the practical impact of the model you choose. The decision affects operational efficiency, scalability, and the level of control you retain over the environment.

- **Public cloud** delivers infrastructure and services over the internet to general users.
- **Private cloud** is dedicated to a single organization and typically offers greater control.
- **Hybrid cloud** combines environments, letting you use more than one deployment approach together.
- **Multi-cloud** uses services from more than one cloud provider and can span different environments.

> Choose the model based on workload needs, control requirements, and security expectations rather than cost alone.

**Core service models**

Cloud service models describe how much of the stack the provider manages versus how much you manage. The main models are Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS), and Software as a Service (SaaS).

These models differ in control, flexibility, and management burden. As you move from IaaS to SaaS, the provider handles more of the underlying environment and you manage less.

| Model | What you get | Typical control level |
|---|---|---|
| IaaS | Virtualized computing resources such as servers, storage, and networking | Highest |
| PaaS | A managed platform for building and deploying applications | Moderate |
| SaaS | Ready-to-use software delivered over the internet | Lowest |

- **IaaS** gives you the most flexibility and control over the digital foundation of your IT operations.
- **PaaS** reduces operational overhead by abstracting the platform layer.
- **SaaS** is the most hands-off option because the application is already delivered and managed for you.

**How to compare deployment and service models**

Deployment models answer where and under whose control the cloud runs. Service models answer how much of the technology stack you manage. Keep those questions separate when studying or evaluating a design.

A simple way to remember the difference is that deployment models describe the environment, while service models describe the delivery layer. A private cloud can still use IaaS, PaaS, or SaaS services, depending on how the provider and customer divide responsibilities.

- Deployment models focus on location, ownership, and access.
- Service models focus on management responsibility and abstraction.
- Both influence security posture, scalability, and cost.

**Exam-focused takeaways**

Security+ expects you to recognize the trade-offs among cloud options rather than memorize marketing language. The most important themes are control, scalability, cost-efficiency, and management responsibility.

- Public cloud is often associated with scalability and cost efficiency.
- Private cloud is often chosen for greater control.
- Hybrid cloud supports mixed requirements across environments.
- Multi-cloud reduces dependence on a single provider.
- IaaS, PaaS, and SaaS represent increasing provider management and decreasing customer control.

**Study approach**

When you see a scenario, identify two things first:

1. Whether the question is asking about a deployment model or a service model.
2. Which party manages the infrastructure, platform, or application layer.

Then match the scenario to the model that best fits the required balance of control, flexibility, and operational effort.
