## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**Wireless attack overview**

- Wireless networks are susceptible to multiple attack types.
- Attacks can disconnect users from the network.
- Attacks can impersonate legitimate wireless infrastructure.
- Attacks can interfere with radio frequency (RF) communication.

**Deauthentication attacks**

- A **deauthentication attack** is a denial-of-service attack.
- It disconnects users from a wireless network without warning.
- It can repeatedly force clients off the network.
- The weakness relates to wireless management frames.
- Management frames are normally not visible to users.

**Rogue access points and evil twins**

- A **rogue access point** is an unauthorized access point on or near the network.
- An **evil twin** is a malicious access point that imitates a legitimate one.
- These attacks can trick users into connecting to the wrong network.
- A poorly placed access point can create security risk.

**RF jamming**

- **RF jamming** disrupts wireless communication by interfering with the signal.
- It can prevent normal connectivity on the wireless network.
- It is a form of wireless denial of service.

**Practical impact**

- Wireless attacks can cause repeated loss of internet access.
- Wireless attacks can affect normal work on the network.
- Wireless attacks may be difficult to stop once they start.
