## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**Compliance overview**

- Compliance means meeting required standards.
- Standards can come from laws, regulations, policies, or third-party agreements.
- Requirements vary by business type and location.
- Compliance can apply at local, national, or international levels.
- Organizations align compliance with business objectives and risk management.

**Legal and regulatory impact**

- Legal requirements shape how you collect, store, and protect data.
- Privacy laws often apply to personal and sensitive information.
- Health-related data may fall under specific national regulations such as `HIPAA`.
- Cross-border operations may require attention to multiple privacy regimes.
- You should treat legal obligations as part of security planning, not an afterthought.

**Penalties and consequences**

- Noncompliance can lead to fines.
- Noncompliance can lead to loss of employment.
- Severe violations can lead to incarceration.
- Reputational damage can follow compliance failures.
- Penalties depend on the rule, agreement, and jurisdiction.

**Monitoring and internal checks**

- Organizations often perform internal compliance checks.
- A `CCO` (`Central Compliance Officer`) may oversee compliance activities.
- Compliance monitoring helps confirm that controls stay aligned with requirements.
- Regular checks help identify gaps before external review.
- Monitoring supports ongoing adherence, not one-time approval.

**Privacy and data responsibilities**

- Privacy laws affect how organizations handle collected data.
- You should limit data collection to what is needed.
- You should track data inventory and retention requirements.
- You should protect data confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
- Data responsibilities often extend across the full data lifecycle.

**Practical study focus**

- Know the difference between a law, a regulation, and a policy.
- Know that agreements can also create compliance obligations.
- Remember that compliance failures can affect both the organization and individuals.
- Connect compliance to governance, risk, and business continuity.
- Expect questions about legal impact, monitoring, and privacy duties.
