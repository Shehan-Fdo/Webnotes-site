## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**Security awareness basics**

- Security awareness training helps users recognize and reduce common risks.
- Proactive training gives end users a role in cybersecurity defense.
- Good awareness habits support data **integrity**, confidentiality, and availability.
- Training should focus on practical, real-world behavior.

**Why training matters**

- Security+ emphasizes practical, hands-on skills for real-world challenges.
- Security skills matter across many IT roles, not only dedicated security jobs.
- Broad security knowledge helps you build a foundation before specializing.
- Awareness training supports a security-first culture.

**Phishing detection**

- Phishing often uses fake email addresses and fake websites.
- Check whether the domain after the `@` sign matches the organization.
- Compare the sender domain with the expected company URL.
- Treat mismatched or unfamiliar URLs as suspicious.
- Verify links before you click them.

**User behavior and risk reduction**

- Users who understand security risks can take action to prevent them.
- Training should help users spot suspicious messages and websites.
- Clear guidance improves vigilance against cybersecurity threats.
- Awareness programs reduce the chance of social engineering success.

**Security+ context**

- CompTIA Security+ is a globally recognized certification for core security functions.
- The certification covers threats, attacks, and vulnerabilities, including social engineering.
- It also supports secure access, risk management, and security technologies.
- Awareness and training fit into a broader security foundation.

**Study focus**

- Learn how to identify fake email addresses.
- Learn how to identify fake websites.
- Practice checking domains and URLs.
- Review common social engineering patterns.
- Connect awareness training to everyday security decisions.
