## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**Third-party risk overview**

- Third-party risk management is part of **Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC)**.
- You assess external parties because they can affect your data, operations, and reputation.
- Common third parties include vendors, service providers, and subprocessors.
- Shared data increases exposure when you rely on outside organizations.

**Why it matters**

- A vendor breach can expose your information.
- A third-party failure can disrupt business operations.
- Weak oversight can create compliance gaps.
- Regular evaluation helps you reduce avoidable risk.

**Assessment focus**

- Review how the third party protects the data you share.
- Check whether the service matches your security expectations.
- Evaluate supply chain dependencies when the third party supports critical functions.
- Monitor vendors over time instead of treating assessment as a one-time task.

**Contract and agreement controls**

- Use contracts to define security expectations.
- Include a **right-to-audit** clause when you need verification.
- Make responsibilities clear for handling shared data.
- Tie security requirements to the services the third party provides.

**Monitoring and review**

- Perform vendor monitoring after onboarding.
- Reassess risk when the service, data, or threat landscape changes.
- Use audits and assessments to confirm adherence to standards.
- Look for vulnerabilities that affect the relationship.

**Practical approach**

1. Identify the third party and the data it receives.
2. Determine the business impact if the third party fails.
3. Review the third party’s security controls and practices.
4. Add contractual requirements for oversight and audit rights.
5. Monitor the relationship and repeat assessments as needed.

**Key terms**

- **Vendor assessment**: review of a third party’s security posture and practices.
- **Supply chain risk**: risk introduced by dependencies on external providers.
- **Subprocessor**: a third party used by another third party to support a service.
- **Right-to-audit**: contract language that allows verification of controls.

**Exam reminders**

- Focus on external dependencies, not only internal controls.
- Expect questions about vendor monitoring and contractual safeguards.
- Connect third-party risk to GRC and compliance.
- Treat shared data as a primary risk driver.
