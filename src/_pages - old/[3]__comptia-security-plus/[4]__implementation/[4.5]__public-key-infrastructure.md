## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**PKI overview**

- **PKI** stands for Public Key Infrastructure.
- PKI manages digital certificates and the trust behind them.
- PKI includes policies, procedures, hardware, software, and people.
- PKI supports secure communication with public key cryptography.
- PKI covers the certificate lifecycle from creation to revocation.

**Core components**

- Digital certificates carry public keys.
- Certificates help you verify that a message or identity is from the expected source.
- A certificate authority, or **CA**, creates trust by signing certificates.
- A registration authority, or **RA**, can verify identity before certificate issuance.
- PKI often centers on certificate creation, distribution, storage, and management.

**Certificate trust and use**

- A digital certificate contains a public key and a digital signature.
- You can treat a certificate like a digital identification card.
- Certificates help provide trust when you access devices or services.
- A CA signature lets you trust the certificate more confidently.
- PKI can also support a web of trust model.

**Key management lifecycle**

- PKI includes the full key management lifecycle.
- The lifecycle starts with certificate creation.
- The lifecycle continues through distribution and use.
- The lifecycle ends with certificate revocation when trust changes.
- Revocation is part of certificate lifecycle management.

**Revocation concepts**

- A certificate can become untrusted before its validity period ends.
- A **CRL** is a certificate revocation list.
- A CRL lists revoked certificates.
- **OCSP** stands for Online Certificate Status Protocol.
- OCSP helps check certificate status.
- OCSP stapling is another revocation-related concept.

**Implementation considerations**

- PKI implementation requires planning.
- PKI is not a quick software install.
- You usually need conversations and meetings before deployment.
- PKI decisions affect users, devices, and organizational trust.
- Daily PKI work often involves creating certificates and associating them with users or devices.

**Practical focus**

- Use PKI to establish trust for certificates.
- Use certificates to support authentication and encryption.
- Use revocation processes when a certificate is no longer trusted.
- Keep PKI management centralized around the CA when possible.
