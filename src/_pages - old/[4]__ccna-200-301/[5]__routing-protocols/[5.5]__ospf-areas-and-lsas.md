## **Course:** 200-301 CCNA

**OSPF area basics**

- OSPF uses **areas** to organize the link-state database.
- An area contains routers and the networks between them.
- LSAs, or **link-state advertisements**, describe the topology inside and between areas.
- Understanding LSA types helps you understand how OSPF builds routes.

**Core LSA roles**

- **Type 1** is the Router LSA.
- A Router LSA describes a router’s links inside an area.
- **Type 2** is the Network LSA.
- A Network LSA describes a multiaccess network and the routers attached to it.
- **Type 3** is the Summary LSA.
- A Summary LSA carries reachability information between areas.

**How OSPF uses LSAs**

- OSPF exchanges LSAs to learn the topology.
- Routers use the LSA information to build the area database.
- The database reflects routers, links, and networks in the area.
- Inter-area information comes from summary information rather than full internal detail.

**Practical study focus**

- Match each LSA type to its job.
- Remember that Type 1 and Type 2 describe internal area topology.
- Remember that Type 3 carries information across area boundaries.
- Use area boundaries to separate detailed internal topology from summarized reachability.

**Exam reminders**

- Know the acronym **LSA** on first sight.
- Know that OSPF area design affects what information a router learns.
- Keep the distinction between router, network, and summary roles clear.
- Focus on what each LSA describes, not just its number.
