## **Course:** 200-301 CCNA

**Ethernet overview**

- Ethernet is the most used networking technology for **LANs**.
- It defines wiring and signaling at the Physical layer.
- It defines frame formats and protocols at the Data Link layer.
- It is described by the **IEEE 802.3** standard.
- It supports high-speed local communication without a leased line or service provider.
- It can use coaxial, twisted-pair, and fiber-optic cables.

**LAN context**

- A **LAN** is a group of devices in a limited geographic area.
- LANs typically support higher data rates than wide area networks.
- Common LAN endpoints include PCs, servers, IP phones, video devices, and consoles.
- Switches and routers connect devices within and between LAN segments.
- An Ethernet LAN combines devices and cables that use the Ethernet standard to communicate.

**Ethernet frames**

- An Ethernet frame is the encapsulated data used at the Network Access layer.
- The frame starts with a header.
- The header includes source and destination **MAC** addresses.
- The frame carries payload data in the middle.
- The frame ends with a **Frame Check Sequence (FCS)**.
- The FCS uses a 32-bit **Cyclic Redundancy Check (CRC)** to detect corruption.

**Frame fields**

- The **Preamble** alerts the receiver that a frame is starting.
- The **Start Frame Delimiter (SFD)** marks the start of the destination MAC address field.
- The destination MAC identifies the receiving system.
- The source MAC identifies the sending system.
- The Type field identifies the encapsulated protocol, such as IPv4 or IPv6.
- Data and Pad carry the payload.
- Padding helps meet the minimum frame length requirement.

**Ethernet standards and speeds**

- Ethernet uses multiple IEEE 802.3 variants.
- `10Base-T` uses 10 Mbps over category 3 unshielded twisted pair (UTP) up to 100 meters.
- `100Base-TX` uses Fast Ethernet over category 5, 5e, or 6 UTP up to 100 meters.
- `100Base-FX` uses multimode fiber up to 412 meters.
- `1000Base-CX` uses copper twisted-pair cabling up to 25 meters.
- Ethernet supports speeds up to 100 Gbps.

**Switching context**

- Ethernet switching is a core CCNA topic.
- Switches use MAC addresses to forward frames.
- A switch builds a MAC address table from observed traffic.
- Wireshark can help you inspect Ethernet frames and MAC addresses.
