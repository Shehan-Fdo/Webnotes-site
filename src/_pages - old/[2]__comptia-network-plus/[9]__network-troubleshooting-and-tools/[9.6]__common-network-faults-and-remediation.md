## **Course:** Comptia Network Plus

**Troubleshooting approach**

- Use a consistent **troubleshooting methodology** to avoid skipping important details.
- Start by identifying the problem.
- Gather information from users and symptoms.
- Duplicate the issue if you can.
- Form a theory of probable cause.
- Test the theory to confirm the cause.
- Build a plan of action before you change anything.
- Implement the fix or escalate if needed.
- Verify full functionality after the change.
- Apply preventive measures when appropriate.

**Common network faults**

- **Attenuation** is signal loss over distance.
- Attenuation affects copper, fiber, and wireless links.
- Too much attenuation can prevent the signal from reaching the other side clearly.
- **Collisions** occur when two devices transmit at the same time on a half-duplex network.
- Collisions are normal on half-duplex Ethernet, but frequent collisions can indicate a design or legacy-device issue.
- **Broadcast storms** can overwhelm a network with excessive broadcast traffic.
- **Duplicate addresses** can cause communication conflicts on the network.
- **Asymmetric routes** can send traffic out one path and return it on another.
- Routing and IP issues can block connectivity even when physical links are up.

**Remediation focus**

- Check cable type, length, and termination when you suspect signal loss.
- Replace or rework damaged or poorly terminated copper or fiber links.
- Reduce collision problems by removing legacy half-duplex bottlenecks where possible.
- Verify addressing to avoid duplicate IP address conflicts.
- Review routing and gateway settings when traffic reaches the wrong path.
- Use the observed symptoms to narrow the fault before changing multiple variables.
- Confirm the network works after each fix.

**Practical troubleshooting habits**

- Question users for experiential details.
- Reproduce the issue before you assume the cause.
- Keep changes controlled so you can tell what fixed the problem.
- Escalate when the issue is outside your access or authority.
- Document the fault, the theory, the fix, and the verification result.
