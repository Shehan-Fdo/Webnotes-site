## **Course:** Comptia A+ 220-1201

**Expansion Cards Overview**

Expansion cards add functionality that is not already built into the motherboard.

- **Add-on cards** install into expansion slots on the motherboard.
- You use them to customize a system for a specific need.
- Common examples include:
  - **Sound cards**
  - **Video adapters**
  - **Capture cards**
  - **Network interface cards (NICs)**

**Expansion Slot Basics**

Motherboard expansion slots provide the connection point for add-on cards.

- **Peripheral Component Interconnect (PCI)** and **PCI Express (PCIe)** are the key slot technologies.
- PCIe is the most important interface to remember for the A+ exam.
- PCIe slots come in different lane widths, such as:
  - `x1`
  - `x4`
  - `x8`
  - `x16`

**Card Installation and Driver Support**

You can usually install expansion cards yourself.

1. Power off the system and open the case.
2. Insert the card into the correct expansion slot.
3. Secure the card in place.
4. Boot the system and let the operating system identify the hardware.
5. Install the proper **device drivers** if the system does not do it automatically.

- Many systems detect the card and load drivers automatically.
- Some cards need vendor-provided drivers for full function.
- Driver support is part of the normal installation workflow.

**Sound Cards**

Sound cards handle audio input and output.

- They convert digital audio data into sound.
- They convert microphone or other audio input into digital data.
- A separate sound card may provide better quality or extra features than onboard audio.

**Video Adapters**

Video adapters extend or improve display output options.

- Motherboards may already provide onboard video outputs.
- A video adapter can add different display interfaces or improve graphics capability.
- Common display connectors include:
  - `HDMI`
  - `DisplayPort`
  - `DVI`
  - `VGA`

**Network Interface Cards**

Network interface cards provide network connectivity.

- A **network interface card (NIC)** adds or improves wired network access.
- Some systems already include onboard networking.
- You add a NIC when you need additional or replacement network capability.

**Capture Cards**

Capture cards bring external video or audio sources into the computer.

- They are used when you need to record or process incoming media.
- They expand the system beyond the motherboard’s built-in features.

**Ports, Connectors, and Adapters**

Ports and adapters help you connect devices when the exact interface is not available.

- `HDMI` carries both audio and video over one digital connection.
- `USB-C` can support video in some configurations.
- Adapters and converters can bridge different connector types.
- Some adapters are temporary tools, while others become part of a permanent setup.

**Practical Connection Notes**

Use the right interface for the device and the task.

- Match the card to the available slot type.
- Match the connector to the display or peripheral.
- Use adapters when the needed cable or port is not available.
- Verify that the operating system recognizes the hardware after installation.
