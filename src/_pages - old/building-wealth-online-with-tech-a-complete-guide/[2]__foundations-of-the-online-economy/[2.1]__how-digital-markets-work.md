## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**What digital markets are**

- Digital markets are part of the **digital economy**, where economic activity moves into digital form through the internet, electronic tools, and data.
- They use digital platforms, social media, search engine optimization (SEO), and other digital marketing methods to reach users.
- They operate across connected people, devices, data, and businesses.
- They often work without geographic limits.

**How platforms create value**

- A **platform** is a business model that facilitates exchange between two or more user groups.
- Platforms connect buyers and sellers, or consumers and producers.
- They create communities and markets that support interaction and transaction.
- Their value grows when more users join.

**Network effects**

- **Network effects** occur when a platform becomes more valuable as user count increases.
- More users can create a positive feedback loop.
- That feedback can attract even more users.
- Network effects help platforms scale quickly.

**Transaction costs**

- Digital marketplaces reduce **transaction costs**.
- They lower search and information costs.
- They lower bargaining and negotiation costs.
- They lower policing and enforcement costs.
- This lower-friction exchange is a core value proposition.

**What digital marketplaces sell**

- Digital marketplaces act as intermediaries.
- They provide trust, efficiency, and infrastructure.
- They monetize through commission fees, subscriptions, promoted listings, and value-added services.
- Their business model depends on making exchange easier and more reliable.

**Technology and market operations**

- Digitalization improves efficiency, accuracy, and client service in market-facing systems.
- AI, machine learning, and blockchain support more complex digital operations.
- Cloud computing and API-driven ecosystems can improve scalability and reliability.
- Marketplace architecture needs automation, security, and seamless multi-sided interaction.

**Practical takeaway**

- Focus on how a market reduces friction.
- Look for the user groups it connects.
- Check whether growth depends on network effects.
- Identify the costs the platform removes from exchange.
