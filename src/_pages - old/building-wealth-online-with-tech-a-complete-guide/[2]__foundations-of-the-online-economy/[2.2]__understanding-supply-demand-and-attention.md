## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Supply, demand, and attention**

- The **attention economy** shifts focus to the consumption of attention and the limits of focus.
- In this framing, attention is a scarce resource.
- Digital markets connect supply and demand in the same space.
- Platforms act as suppliers of online goods and services.
- Users supply attention through their time and focus.
- Demand matters because platforms compete for limited user attention.

**Why attention matters**

- Attention shapes what people notice, trust, and share.
- Hype can dominate when platforms optimize for monetization.
- Truth can be hard to build, easy to lose, and easy to get wrong.
- Nuance can disappear when everything becomes a spectacle.
- The question is not only what gets built, but who benefits.

**Supply and demand in digital spaces**

- Supply describes what platforms, creators, and services offer.
- Demand describes what users are willing to consume.
- Attention links the two because users cannot focus on everything.
- Scarcity makes attention valuable.
- Value rises when a platform captures sustained user focus.

**Practical implications**

- Build for user benefit, not only for hype.
- Treat trust as an asset you must protect.
- Avoid designs that reward speculation over usefulness.
- Look for gaps where users want more value than platforms provide.
- Ask whether a product earns attention or merely borrows it.

**Key takeaway**

- Online wealth depends on understanding what is scarce.
- In many digital markets, the scarce resource is not content.
- The scarce resource is attention.
