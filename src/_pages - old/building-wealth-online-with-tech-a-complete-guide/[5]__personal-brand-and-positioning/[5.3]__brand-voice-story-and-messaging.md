## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Brand voice**

- Your brand voice is how you speak, the perspectives you take, and the messages you put out.
- A unified voice helps you stay distinct and authentic across audiences.
- Keep your voice consistent across platforms.
- Avoid cliché, jargony, overly embellished, or overly complicated language.
- Use a tone that fits your brand personality and purpose.
- Define your voice with a few clear traits and apply them everywhere.

**Brand story**

- Your brand story gives people a reason to remember you.
- Strong storytelling makes your brand more memorable and powerful.
- Use consistent themes in your storytelling.
- Let your story reflect your values, strengths, and point of view.
- Build a narrative that feels genuine and relatable.
- Use your story to connect your message to a larger purpose.

**Messaging**

- Messaging should clearly communicate who you are and the value you bring.
- Good messaging helps you stand out and build credibility.
- Keep messages intentional and audience-aware.
- Match the level of detail to the audience.
- Use a clear tagline or guiding idea when it supports the message.
- Make sure every message reinforces the same core idea.

**How to shape your message**

1. Talk to real people and learn what they struggle with.
2. Identify the problems, wants, and frustrations they share.
3. Turn those insights into messaging and content themes.
4. Pick one clear problem for one type of person.
5. Write in a natural, human voice.
6. Keep refining as you learn more.

**Practical brand principles**

- Build a brand that works for you, not just one that gets attention.
- Focus on clarity before volume.
- Use your skills, experience, and personality to define your presence.
- Treat your online presence as part of your digital reputation.
- Every post, article, and comment shapes how others perceive you.
- Consistency across website, social media, content, and interactions matters.

**What strong brand communication does**

- It increases visibility.
- It supports credibility.
- It helps you stand out in a competitive market.
- It can create meaningful opportunities.
- It can compound over time when you stay intentional.

**Common mistakes to avoid**

- Do not rely on vague or generic language.
- Do not confuse consistency with sounding robotic.
- Do not build messaging without talking to real people.
- Do not try to speak to everyone at once.
- Do not let your story drift away from your actual strengths.
