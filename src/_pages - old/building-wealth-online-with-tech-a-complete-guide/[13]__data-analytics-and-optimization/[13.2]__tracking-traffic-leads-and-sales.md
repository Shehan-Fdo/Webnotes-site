## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Why tracking matters**

- Track marketing performance to see which efforts drive valuable customer activity.
- Use **conversion measurement** to connect clicks with outcomes like purchases, sign-ups, or phone calls.
- Monitor results consistently so you can react and optimize campaigns.
- Treat analytics as a decision tool, not just a reporting layer.
- Use traffic, lead, and sales data together to understand the full funnel.

**What to measure**

- Measure website traffic to understand how people reach your site.
- Measure leads to see which actions create potential customers.
- Measure sales to identify which campaigns produce revenue.
- Track return on investment, or **ROI**, to judge whether spend is worthwhile.
- Review keyword, ad, ad group, and campaign performance to find what drives value.

**Google Ads and analytics**

- Use Google Ads conversion measurement to evaluate ad performance.
- Import Google Analytics conversions into Google Ads when you want a broader view of customer activity.
- Use Google Analytics as the source of truth when you need tracking across app or website activity.
- Use Google Ads conversion measurement when you want to focus on conversions from Google Ads sources.
- Add conversion goals when you create campaigns so the platform can optimize toward your objective.

**Campaign setup**

1. Choose your advertising objective, such as `Sales`, `Leads`, or `Website traffic`.
2. Add the conversion goals that match that objective.
3. Use account-default goals when they fit your campaign.
4. Add a custom conversion goal when your business needs a specific action.
5. Remove goals that do not support the campaign outcome.

**Optimization signals**

- Use conversion data to guide bidding strategies.
- Apply Smart Bidding when you want automated optimization toward business goals.
- Review cross-device and cross-browser conversions to capture delayed purchases or sign-ups.
- Check reporting columns such as `All conversions` for a fuller view of performance.
- Compare campaigns over time to spot trends and weak points.

**Practical reporting habits**

- Monitor campaigns regularly instead of waiting for results to stall.
- Use reports to identify productive keywords and ads.
- Check Quality Score when you evaluate ad efficiency.
- Combine transactional data with location or audience data when you need deeper insight.
- Keep your measurement setup aligned with the action you value most.

**Key takeaway**

- Good tracking shows what attracts traffic, what creates leads, and what closes sales.
- Better measurement supports better spending decisions.
- Clear conversion goals make optimization more effective.
