## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Overview**

- Wealth building starts with clear financial goals.
- You can define wealth in practical terms, such as paying bills on time, funding college, buying a home, starting a business, or saving for retirement.
- A systematic approach helps you make better choices over time.
- Budgeting, saving, investing, credit, debt control, and insurance all support long-term financial progress.

**Core metrics to track**

- **Goal progress** shows whether your actions match your financial plan.
- **Budget adherence** shows whether you spend within planned limits.
- **Savings rate** shows how much income you set aside for future needs.
- **Debt level** shows how much you still owe and how quickly you reduce it.
- **Credit health** shows how well you manage borrowing.
- **Investment growth** shows whether assets are building value over time.
- **Protection coverage** shows whether basic insurance helps shield your wealth.

**How to use metrics**

- Set specific financial goals before you measure anything.
- Compare actual spending with your budget on a regular schedule.
- Track savings consistently so you can fund emergencies and future goals.
- Monitor debt so you can avoid letting it block progress.
- Review investments with a long-term view instead of chasing short-term guesses.
- Check credit and insurance because both affect financial resilience.

**Why metrics matter**

- Metrics turn vague intentions into measurable progress.
- Data helps you make decisions instead of relying on guesses.
- Regular tracking supports better planning and course correction.
- A simple system is often more useful than a complex one.

**Practical focus**

- Start with the metrics that match your current goals.
- Keep the process simple enough to maintain.
- Use the numbers to guide action, not just observation.
- Revisit your definition of wealth as your goals change.
