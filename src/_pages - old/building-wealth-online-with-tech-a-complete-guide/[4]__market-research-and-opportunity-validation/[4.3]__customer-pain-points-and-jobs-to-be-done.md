## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Customer pain points and JTBD**

- JTBD stands for **Jobs-to-be-Done**.
- The framework shifts focus from the product to the progress a customer wants to make.
- Customers do not buy products only for the item itself.
- They “hire” products or services to complete a specific job.
- A job is the problem or opportunity a person is trying to solve.
- The job exists in a specific circumstance.
- The framework helps you understand why customers choose one solution over another.
- It supports product development, marketing, and business strategy.

**What to look for**

- Identify the core **functional job**.
- Define the emotional benefit the customer seeks.
- Note any **social job** tied to how the customer wants to be seen.
- Separate the main job from related jobs.
- Focus on the outcome the customer wants, not just the feature they request.
- Use customer behavior and context to infer the real need.
- Treat demographics as secondary to the job itself.

**Why it matters**

- Market research helps you find customers.
- Competitive analysis helps you make your business unique.
- Combining both can reveal a competitive advantage.
- JTBD can improve innovation outcomes by making needs clearer.
- JTBD can reduce the risk of building the wrong offer.
- A clear job helps you align positioning with customer intent.
- A clear job can improve product, pricing, and messaging decisions.

**How to apply it**

1. Observe the customer’s situation and desired progress.
2. Ask what outcome the customer is trying to achieve.
3. Separate the functional, emotional, and social dimensions.
4. Compare the job with current alternatives.
5. Use the insight to shape the offer and message.

**Practical reminders**

- Do not assume the stated request is the full need.
- Do not start with demographics alone.
- Do not focus only on features.
- Keep the analysis tied to a real circumstance.
- Use JTBD to uncover the underlying motivation behind behavior.
