**What a North Star Metric is**

A **North Star Metric (NSM)** is a single metric that best captures the value customers get from your product. It acts as a shared reference point for product strategy and helps teams focus on what matters most.

The framework is powerful because it creates clarity. When teams understand the same metric, they can align on priorities, reduce wasted work, and make decisions faster. The metric should reflect customer value, not just internal activity.

- It is **single** by design, so it creates focus.
- It is **customer-centric**, meaning it reflects how users get value.
- It is **quantitative**, so you can measure it with numbers.
- It is **actionable**, so changes in the metric lead to decisions.
- It serves as a **leading indicator** of long-term business success.

**Why it matters**

A good North Star Metric helps coordinate multiple teams around one outcome. It gives the organization a point of reference for measuring success and tracking progress over time.

It also supports better prioritization. Instead of chasing many dashboards at once, you use one metric to judge whether product work is moving the business in the right direction. This keeps strategy tied to customer value and the product vision.

> A North Star Metric should not be confused with a vanity metric. If it does not reflect real customer value or guide action, it is not doing its job.

**Characteristics of a strong North Star Metric**

A useful NSM is easy to understand and easy to communicate. Everyone should be able to speak the same language around it.

It should also represent sustainable value. In practice, that often means the metric connects to habits or repeated use, not just one-time activity. It should show progress toward the mission and be something the team can influence directly.

- **Simple** enough for the whole team to understand.
- **Customer-focused** rather than company-only focused.
- **Sustainable** in the sense that it reflects ongoing value.
- **Mission-aligned** so it points toward the product vision.
- **Actionable** so teams can respond when it changes.

**Common misconceptions**

One common mistake is assuming the North Star Metric can be revenue. The evidence emphasizes that the metric should capture customer value and guide product action, so revenue alone is usually too narrow for the framework.

Another mistake is treating the NSM as just another KPI. A KPI can measure performance, but the North Star Metric is the central measure that organizes strategy and prioritization. If you choose too many metrics, you lose the focus the framework is meant to create.

**How to use it in product strategy**

You use the North Star Metric to guide decisions about what to build, improve, or remove. When a proposed change does not improve the metric or support it indirectly, it deserves closer scrutiny.

A practical approach is to connect the NSM to supporting metrics that explain movement in the main number. Those supporting metrics help you understand what is driving progress without replacing the central metric.

1. Identify the core value your product delivers to customers.
2. Choose one metric that best reflects that value.
3. Check that the metric is measurable, actionable, and easy to explain.
4. Use supporting metrics to diagnose changes in the main metric.
5. Revisit the metric if it no longer reflects customer value or strategy.

**Selecting the right metric**

Start with the product’s value proposition and the customer journey. Ask what behavior best shows that customers are getting meaningful value from the product.

In more complex environments, the metric should still connect to the business model and customer outcomes. For example, a product tied to a larger organization should support the organization’s goals while still staying centered on the product experience and customer value.

- Focus on the behavior that best represents value delivery.
- Avoid metrics that are too broad to guide action.
- Avoid metrics that are too narrow to reflect the full product experience.
- Make sure the metric can be tracked consistently over time.

**Key takeaway**

The North Star Metric is a simple but high-leverage framework. When chosen well, it improves alignment, sharpens prioritization, and keeps teams focused on the value customers actually receive.
