## **Course:** Comptia Network Plus

**Troubleshooting methodology**

- Use a **systematic process of elimination** when you diagnose network issues.
- Follow the CompTIA sequence to keep your response organized.
- The methodology helps you resolve problems promptly and avoid costly damage or repairs.
- Experience helps, but the framework gives you a repeatable path.

**Troubleshooting steps**

1. Identify the problem.
2. Establish a theory of probable cause.
3. Test the theory to determine the cause.
4. Establish a plan of action to resolve the problem and identify potential effects.
5. Implement the solution or escalate as necessary.
6. Verify full system functionality and, if applicable, implement preventive measures.

**Step details**

- Start by gathering the user’s symptoms and the visible impact.
- Build a **probable cause** from the evidence you have.
- Test one theory at a time to confirm or reject it.
- Plan the fix before you change the system.
- Consider side effects before you implement the solution.
- Escalate when the issue is outside your control or scope.
- Verify that the system works after the fix.
- Add preventive measures when they apply.

**Verification and follow-up**

- Confirm that the original issue is gone.
- Check related functions to make sure nothing else broke.
- Document findings, actions, outcomes, and lessons learned.
- Record the solution so future troubleshooting is faster.

**Exam focus**

- Know the order of the troubleshooting steps.
- Expect questions about the next action in the sequence.
- The correct next step depends on where you are in the process.
- Do not skip verification after implementation.
