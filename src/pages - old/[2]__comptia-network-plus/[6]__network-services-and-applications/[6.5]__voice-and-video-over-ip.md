## **Course:** Comptia Network Plus

**Voice and Video over IP basics**

- **Voice over IP (VoIP)** carries voice communication in IP packets.
- VoIP replaces older analog telephone service such as **POTS** (Plain Old Telephone Service).
- VoIP phones are network devices on wired or wireless networks.
- VoIP phones often connect through Ethernet.
- Many VoIP phones use **PoE** (Power over Ethernet) for power.

**Unified communications**

- **Unified communications (UC)** combines voice, video, instant messaging, and desktop sharing.
- UC has no exact formal definition.
- UC lets you receive and view communication in different forms.
- VoIP is a major technology behind UC.
- Video streaming and presence can work with VoIP in a UC platform.

**Network considerations**

- Voice and video services depend on reliable network delivery.
- **QoS** (Quality of Service) helps prioritize some applications over others.
- QoS helps manage application priority and bandwidth.
- Voice and video traffic often need special handling to stay usable.
- Network design should support the devices and services that carry real-time communication.

**Practical device context**

- Modern offices often use VoIP phones instead of analog telephones.
- Voice communication can be critical for businesses that rely on it.
- Networked voice devices behave like individual computers on the network.
- Cameras and other connected devices can share the same modern network environment.

**Key takeaways**

- IP networks carry more than data; they also support voice and video.
- VoIP moves telephony into the network layer of the modern infrastructure.
- UC brings multiple communication methods into one environment.
- QoS and PoE help make voice and video services practical.
