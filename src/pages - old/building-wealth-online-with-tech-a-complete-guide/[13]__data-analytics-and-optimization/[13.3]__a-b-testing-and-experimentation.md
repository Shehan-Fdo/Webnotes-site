## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**A/B testing basics**

- A/B testing compares two versions of a web page, app, or page element.
- You use it to see which variation performs better on a measurable metric.
- It is an online experimentation process that supports product and marketing decisions.
- The **control** is the existing version.
- The **variation** or **treatment** is the new version you test against the control.

**Core workflow**

- Start with a clear **hypothesis** about what you change and what you expect.
- Randomly divide traffic into two or more groups.
- Show each group a different version for a set period.
- Compare performance using metrics such as conversions or sales.
- Analyze the result before you decide whether to implement the change permanently.

**Key experimentation terms**

- **Statistical significance** means the observed difference is unlikely to be due to chance.
- **Confidence level** is the certainty threshold you want before calling a result significant.
- A common confidence level is `95%`.
- **Sample size** is the number of visitors or users in the test.
- **Test duration** is how long the experiment runs.
- **Variance** is how much results change over time or across segments.

**Planning and interpretation**

- Build tests on careful planning, execution, and analysis.
- Choose variables that match the decision you want to make.
- Use a representative sample when possible.
- Interpret results conservatively when data quality is weak.
- Treat strong-looking results with caution if noise or flawed assumptions may bias them.

**Common pitfalls**

- Poor data accuracy can distort experiment results.
- Weak statistical rigor can lead you to ship ineffective experiences.
- Bad assumptions can cause you to misallocate resources.
- Low data can make a result look significant when it is not reliable enough.
- A result may need more data before you call a winner.

**Practical decision rules**

- Use the control as the baseline for comparison.
- Prefer measurable outcomes over vague opinions.
- Check whether the observed lift is large enough to matter.
- Confirm the result meets your confidence threshold.
- Keep the test running long enough to support the sample size you need.

**Why it matters**

- A/B testing helps you improve user experience.
- It can improve conversion rates on websites, apps, and other touchpoints.
- It gives you a structured way to compare ideas before you commit to them.
- It supports better decisions in marketing, product, design, and analysis.
