## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Discounts and promotions**

- Discounts and promotions change how customers judge value.
- Pricing can signal quality, not just cost.
- Shoppers often compare the current price with a reference price.
- Clear upfront pricing helps buyers evaluate offers faster.
- Promotions can appear earlier than expected in retail cycles.

**Anchoring basics**

- **Anchoring** is the tendency to rely on the first price or reference point seen.
- A higher reference price can make the actual price feel like a better deal.
- The original price often serves as the anchor in sale pricing.
- Customers may judge later options against the first number they see.
- Anchors shape perceived value before deeper comparison starts.

**High-anchor pricing**

- Show the premium option first when you want to frame value.
- Use a higher-priced comparison to make the main offer look more attractive.
- Add decoy products or reference points to create context.
- Make sure the anchor has real value behind it.
- Use tangible benefits to justify the higher comparison price.

**Pricing experiments**

- Test price points to find the balance between revenue and demand.
- A pricing experiment helps you compare higher and lower prices.
- Trial and error can reveal which price maximizes revenue.
- Customer behavior can change when prices change.
- Use experiments to reduce guesswork in pricing decisions.

**Practical takeaways**

- Use discounts with a clear reference price.
- Keep comparisons simple and easy to understand.
- Avoid anchors that feel unsupported or misleading.
- Match the pricing message to the product’s real value.
- Review how customers respond before scaling a pricing change.
