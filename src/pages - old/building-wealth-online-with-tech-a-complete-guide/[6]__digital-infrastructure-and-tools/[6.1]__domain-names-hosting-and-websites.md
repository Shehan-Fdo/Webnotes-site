## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Domain names**

- A **domain name** is your website’s human-friendly address on the internet.
- It helps people find your site and supports your online brand.
- Short, clear domains are easier to remember and can be more valuable.
- Popular top-level domains, such as `.com`, are often in higher demand than newer extensions.
- A domain can have value even without a website attached.
- Domain trading focuses on buying and selling domain names that others want.

**Web hosting**

- **Web hosting** stores your website’s files on a server.
- It makes your site accessible to visitors online.
- If you want to launch a website, online store, or blog, you need hosting.
- Hosting providers keep your site online and support security and reliability.
- Most people rent server space instead of buying their own server.

**Domain names vs. hosting**

- A domain name is the address.
- Web hosting is the storage and delivery layer.
- Visitors use the domain to reach the site, and the server delivers the content.
- The two services work together, but they are not the same thing.

**Building a website**

- A website combines your domain, hosting, and content.
- Your site can support a blog, store, or business presence.
- A website is the base layer for publishing content and earning online.
- Website monetization turns traffic into revenue through ads, products, services, affiliate partnerships, or subscriptions.

**Monetization basics**

- Website monetization converts traffic into revenue.
- Common models include advertising, selling products, selling services, affiliate partnerships, and subscriptions.
- A monetized site can generate income around the clock.
- Revenue depends on traffic, offer fit, and how well the site serves visitors.

**Choosing and managing a domain**

- Pick a memorable name that fits your brand.
- Check whether the name is short, clear, and easy to type.
- Review the extension and the likely demand for it.
- Register the domain through a provider that supports setup and management.
- Keep your domain details organized for long-term control.

**Practical setup considerations**

- Use one platform when possible to simplify domain, hosting, and site management.
- Look for security features such as SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) and DNSSEC (Domain Name System Security Extensions).
- Check whether the provider includes support, privacy options, and easy transfer tools.
- Confirm that hosting performance and uptime match your site’s needs.

**Key takeaways**

- A domain gives your site an address.
- Hosting gives your site a place to live online.
- A website turns those pieces into a working presence.
- Monetization turns that presence into a potential income stream.
