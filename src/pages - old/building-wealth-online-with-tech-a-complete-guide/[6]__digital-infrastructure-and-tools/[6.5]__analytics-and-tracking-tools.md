## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**What these tools do**

- Use tracking tools to measure financial progress over time.
- Track **net worth** with a balance sheet or net worth form.
- Record daily spending to see where money goes.
- Build a budget from income and expenses.
- Set financial goals and review them regularly.
- Plan an investment strategy and monitor it.
- Track debt to understand interest and repayment needs.

**Common tool types**

- Use web forms for quick online completion.
- Use spreadsheets when you want editable calculations.
- Use blank PDF forms when you prefer manual entry.
- Use dashboards or platforms when you want real-time visibility.
- Use digital platforms and tools to support analysis and reporting.

**Core tracking areas**

- Define wealth before you measure it.
- Compare assets and liabilities to estimate net worth.
- Separate short-term goals from long-term goals.
- Track spending before you try to optimize it.
- Review income and expenses together for a full budget view.
- Monitor debt alongside savings and investing.

**How to use the tools**

1. Define what wealth means to you.
2. Create a balance sheet to calculate net worth.
3. List your financial goals.
4. Track your spending day to day.
5. Build a budget from your spending and income.
6. Write down your investment strategy.
7. Calculate debt interest and repayment steps.
8. Review progress and adjust your plan.

**Practical benefits**

- Make financial decisions with clearer data.
- See progress instead of guessing.
- Keep goals realistic and time-bound.
- Improve budgeting discipline.
- Support saving, investing, and debt control.
- Reduce manual effort with reusable forms.

**Wealth management context**

- Wealth tech stacks combine multiple tools for different tasks.
- Some platforms focus on real-time net worth tracking.
- Some systems support automation and continuous monitoring.
- Cloud-native, SaaS-based platforms can improve productivity.
- Automation can reduce operational risk in financial workflows.

> Use conservative assumptions when data is incomplete.
> Track consistently so your comparisons stay meaningful.

**Key takeaway**

- Analytics and tracking tools turn financial activity into usable information.
- The most useful tools help you define, measure, plan, and review.
