## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Core distinction**

- **Value creation** is the benefit your product or service provides to customers.
- It aligns with **use value**, meaning the utility or satisfaction customers get from using the offering.
- **Value capture** is the profit your business keeps for shareholders.
- It aligns with **exchange value**, meaning the price paid for the offering.
- The two concepts are related, but they are not the same.

**Why the distinction matters**

- Confusing the two can lead to weak product decisions.
- A team can improve monetization without improving customer value.
- A team can create strong customer value and still fail to capture enough of it.
- Long-term success depends on both creating value and capturing some of it.

**Product and business implications**

- Product work should start with customer problems, useful technology, or a better solution than competitors.
- Pricing tests, advertising optimization, and cost reduction are capture activities.
- These activities matter, but they do not replace value creation.
- You should avoid treating extraction as the same thing as innovation.
- Sustainable advantage depends on delivering something customers value and building a way to earn from it.

**Practical lens**

- Ask whether your work improves the customer outcome.
- Ask whether your business model converts that outcome into revenue or profit.
- A product can create real value without capturing much of it.
- A business can capture money without adding much new value.
- The strongest models do both well.

**Common pattern**

- In crowded markets, firms often compete on price, features, or marketing.
- In less contested spaces, a company can have more room to think long term.
- When value creation is obvious but capture is weak, investors may benefit less than customers or users.
- When capture dominates, the business may look profitable while contributing little new value.

**Key takeaway**

- Treat value creation as the foundation.
- Treat value capture as the mechanism that sustains the business.
- Use both to build durable products and healthier economics.
