## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Business model basics**

- A **business model** explains how a business creates, delivers, and captures value.
- In the digital age, technology changes how value reaches customers and how revenue flows.
- Digital business models often rely on software, networks, data, and online platforms.
- You should separate the product or service from the model used to monetize it.

**Digital-age shifts**

- Digital tools reduce friction in distribution and customer access.
- Online channels can expand reach beyond local markets.
- Data can improve targeting, personalization, and decision-making.
- Digital delivery can lower marginal costs for some offerings.
- Innovation often comes from changing the model, not only the product.

**Common model patterns**

- **Platform** models connect different user groups and create value through interaction.
- **Subscription** models charge recurring fees for ongoing access.
- **Freemium** models offer a basic version free and charge for premium features.
- **Transaction** models earn revenue from each sale or exchange.
- **Service** models monetize expertise, support, or implementation.

**Value creation and capture**

- Value creation focuses on solving a real customer problem.
- Value capture focuses on how the business earns from that solution.
- A strong model aligns customer benefit with business revenue.
- You should test whether customers will pay for the value you provide.
- You should check whether the delivery method supports sustainable margins.

**Design considerations**

- Match the model to customer behavior and buying frequency.
- Use digital channels where they improve speed, scale, or convenience.
- Keep the offer simple enough for customers to understand quickly.
- Build trust when the business depends on online transactions.
- Review whether the model can adapt as technology and competition change.

**Practical evaluation**

1. Identify the customer problem you solve.
2. Define how you deliver the solution digitally.
3. Choose the revenue mechanism.
4. Check whether the model can scale.
5. Review risks from competition, platform dependence, or changing user needs.

**Key takeaway**

- Digital business models are not just online versions of old businesses.
- They often change the way value is created, delivered, and monetized.
- The best model fits the customer, the channel, and the economics of delivery.
