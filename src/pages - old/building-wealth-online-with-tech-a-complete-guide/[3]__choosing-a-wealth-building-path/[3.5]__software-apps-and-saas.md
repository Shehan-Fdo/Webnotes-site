## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**Software and SaaS in wealth management**

- Wealth management software supports financial planning, data management, goal management, and risk management.
- These systems help firms deliver personalization and real-time insights.
- They can improve customer experience, productivity, and security.
- Market growth signals strong demand for these tools, with one source projecting the market to reach `$26.98 billion` by `2035` at a `12.76%` CAGR.

**AI in wealth management**

- **AI** is increasingly used in financial services and wealth management.
- AI can streamline calculations, forecast trends, and support investment research.
- Trusted AI can help mitigate risk, improve efficiency, and personalize client experiences.
- Generative AI has shown productivity gains in financial services, with one source citing a `26%` increase.
- Virtual advisors can respond to client questions and help keep clients engaged.

**Why firms adopt custom platforms**

- Financial markets change quickly, so firms need tools that support fast decisions.
- Clients expect instant insights and digital-first service.
- Custom software helps firms safeguard client assets and improve decision-making.
- Modern wealth management software is a business driver, not just an IT expense.
- Firms also use software to support compliance and consistent growth.

**SaaS architecture basics**

- **SaaS** stands for Software as a Service.
- Multi-tenant SaaS serves multiple customers in one architecture.
- Each tenant keeps a separate environment within the shared system.
- Multi-tenancy improves scalability and cost efficiency through shared resources.
- Centralized maintenance lets updates and fixes reach all tenants at once.

**Tenant isolation and risk control**

- Tenant isolation helps prevent data leakage and unauthorized access.
- Isolation can use database segregation.
- Isolation can also use application-level controls.
- Strong isolation matters when multiple customers share the same platform.

**Platform selection and product fit**

- Wealth management platforms often include financial planning, portfolio management, risk handling, and client communication.
- Demand is rising for platforms that integrate AI, automation, and secure client portals.
- The right platform should match business needs and growth goals.
- Legacy systems may struggle with digital-first expectations from newer clients.

**Practical takeaways**

- Use software when you need better data use, faster decisions, and stronger client service.
- Use AI where it improves efficiency, forecasting, or client engagement.
- Use multi-tenant SaaS when you need scale without separate systems for each customer.
- Prioritize security, compliance, and isolation when building or buying a platform.
