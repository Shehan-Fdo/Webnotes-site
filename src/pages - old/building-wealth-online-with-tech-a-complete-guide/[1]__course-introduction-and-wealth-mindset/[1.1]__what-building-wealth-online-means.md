## **Course:** Building Wealth Online with Tech: A Complete Guide

**What building wealth means**

- Building wealth means creating **financial wealth** that supports your goals.
- Wealth can mean different things to different people.
- Common goals include paying off debt, buying a home, funding college, starting a business, and saving for retirement.
- Wealth is not just a high income.
- If you earn well but spend everything, you do not become wealthier.
- Building wealth takes planning, good choices, and the right information.

**Core money language**

- **Assets** are things you own that have value.
- **Liabilities** are things you owe.
- **Net worth** equals `assets - liabilities`.
- A wealth-creating asset can produce income or increase in value.
- Examples of personal assets include savings, retirement plans, stocks, bonds, and a house.
- Some possessions, like a car or clothes, are assets but do not usually build wealth.
- A car can lose value quickly after purchase.

**How wealth grows**

- Wealth grows when you spend less than you earn.
- Saving creates the base for future investing.
- **Compound interest** helps savings and investments grow over time.
- Investing puts saved money to work.
- Investments can grow through income, such as interest or dividends, or through appreciation.
- Good investments can increase wealth.
- Bad investments can reduce wealth.

**Practical habits that support wealth**

- Set clear financial goals.
- Create a budget.
- Track day-to-day spending.
- Save regularly.
- Plan your investment strategy.
- Control debt.
- Protect wealth with basic insurance.
- Build credit carefully.
- Use licensed or registered advisers when you need guidance.

**Starting from little or nothing**

- You do not need a large salary or a windfall to begin.
- Small, consistent actions can add up over time.
- A clear plan matters more than waiting for perfect conditions.
- Start with an emergency fund if you can.
- An emergency fund helps cover unexpected costs and reduces the need for debt.
- A common target is 3 to 6 months of living expenses.

**Using tools and forms**

- Use a form to define your own idea of wealth.
- Use a balance sheet to calculate net worth.
- Use a goal list to record financial targets.
- Use a spending tracker to see where money goes.
- Use a budget form to match income and expenses.
- Use an investment strategy form to plan how you will invest.
- Use a debt tool to calculate interest and manage repayment.

**Key takeaway**

- Building wealth online still follows basic financial principles.
- You build wealth through steady saving, careful investing, debt control, and consistent planning.
- The process is gradual, but it is achievable with disciplined choices.
