## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**Overview**

- Social engineering targets the vulnerability of people.
- Attackers use human traits such as complacency, sympathy, and trust.
- The goal is often to collect intelligence, gain access, or install malicious software.
- Awareness programs help reduce the success of these attacks.

**Core idea**

- Social engineering is a non-technical attack tactic.
- It uses psychological manipulation to influence actions.
- It often relies on social interaction with a user.
- It can target coworkers, acquaintances, strangers, or anyone who may respond to a request.

**Common techniques**

- **Phishing** uses deceptive messages to trick users into revealing sensitive information.
- Phishing often arrives through email.
- Phishing messages may include a link or attachment.
- Phishing often creates urgency and appears legitimate.
- **Smishing** uses text messages.
- **Vishing** uses voice calls.
- **Pretexting** uses a believable scenario to gain trust.
- **Tailgating** involves following someone into a restricted area.
- **Shoulder surfing** involves observing information as it is entered or displayed.
- **Hoaxes** use false claims or fake warnings to manipulate behavior.
- **Pharming** redirects users to fraudulent destinations.

**Why these attacks work**

- Attackers impersonate trusted or legitimate sources.
- They exploit helpful behavior and routine trust.
- They pressure users to act before verifying details.
- They may use emotional triggers or a sense of urgency.

**Defensive practices**

- Verify the sender before sharing sensitive information.
- Inspect links and attachments before opening them.
- Use caution with unexpected requests.
- Confirm unusual requests through a separate trusted channel.
- Train users to recognize common social engineering patterns.
- Reinforce cybersecurity awareness regularly.

**Exam focus**

- Identify the attack by the communication method and the attacker’s goal.
- Distinguish social engineering from technical attacks.
- Remember that the target is the person, not the system.
- Link awareness training to prevention.
