## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**Digital forensics overview**

- Digital forensics supports investigations by handling digital evidence in a controlled way.
- It focuses on **documentation**, acquisition, integrity maintenance, preservation, e-discovery, data recovery, and non-repudiation.
- It helps incident response teams support investigations with reliable evidence and strategic intelligence.
- It fits within Security+ operations and incident response topics.

**Evidence handling**

- Document evidence before and during collection.
- Preserve evidence so you do not alter its state unnecessarily.
- Maintain **integrity** during acquisition and storage.
- Support **non-repudiation** by keeping evidence traceable and defensible.
- Use careful handling for devices, files, and network data.

**Acquisition and preservation**

- Acquire data from relevant sources such as computers, mobile phones, and network systems.
- Collect evidence in a way that supports later analysis.
- Preserve original evidence whenever possible.
- Recover data when it is needed for the investigation.
- Keep acquisition and preservation steps consistent with the investigation plan.

**Incident response support**

- Use digital forensics as part of incident response.
- Align forensic work with incident response plans and processes.
- Support tabletop sessions, walkthroughs, and simulations when preparing teams.
- Consider related plans such as communication plans, disaster recovery plans, business continuity plans, and continuity of operation planning (`COOP`).
- Use forensic findings to inform response and recovery decisions.

**Practical investigation focus**

- Treat digital forensics as a practical skill area.
- Focus on real-world evidence handling rather than theory alone.
- Use structured processes to reduce mistakes during investigations.
- Keep records clear so others can review your work.
- Support both technical and administrative investigation needs.

**Security+ context**

- Security+ emphasizes practical, hands-on skills for real-world security challenges.
- The certification covers threats, tools, secure systems, access control, risk, and cryptography alongside incident response.
- Digital forensics connects to these skills by helping you investigate security events and protect evidence.
- Strong forensic habits support confidentiality, integrity, and availability goals.
