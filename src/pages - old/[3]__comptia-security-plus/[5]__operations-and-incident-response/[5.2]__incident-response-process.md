## **Course:** CompTia Security Plus

**Incident response overview**

- Incident response is a structured process for handling security incidents.
- You use it to limit damage and restore normal operations.
- Incidents can include malware, denial-of-service attacks, data theft, and unauthorized software.
- A planned and tested process supports business continuity.
- A Computer Security Incident Response Team (**CSIRT**) may handle larger or more complex incidents.

**Why preparation matters**

- Cyberattacks are nearly inevitable.
- You prepare before an incident happens.
- Staff need to know how to respond.
- Front-line employees and IT staff both play a role.
- Preparation helps you act quickly when systems behave abnormally.

**Common incident response phases**

- **Preparation** sets roles, procedures, and training before an event.
- **Detection and analysis** help you identify an incident and understand what is happening.
- **Containment, eradication, and recovery** limit spread, remove the threat, and restore services.
- **Post-incident activity** captures lessons learned and improves future response.
- Different frameworks may group these phases differently, but the core goals stay the same.

**What you look for**

- A user may click a malicious attachment and install malware.
- A distributed denial-of-service (**DDoS**) attack may overwhelm network resources.
- Data may be stolen or exfiltrated.
- A user may install software that opens access from the outside.
- You treat these as security events that may require response.

**Planning and classification**

- An incident response plan defines the steps you take for abnormal system behavior.
- You base your response on the criticality of the information involved.
- You also consider whether other business operations are affected.
- Documented incident types and category definitions help responders choose the right action.
- Clear categories improve consistency during response.

**Team and coordination**

- A CSIRT may be needed for major incidents.
- Existing IT staff still need response responsibilities.
- Coordination across teams supports faster isolation and recovery.
- A security operations center (**SOC**) may help identify and prioritize issues before full response begins.

**Practical response goals**

- Stop further damage as early as possible.
- Preserve continuity of operations.
- Recover affected systems safely.
- Communicate clearly during the incident.
- Review the event after recovery to improve the process.

**Key exam takeaways**

- Incident response is not optional in modern security planning.
- You need preparation, not just reaction.
- You respond based on the incident type and business impact.
- Containment and recovery are as important as detection.
- Testing the process improves readiness.
