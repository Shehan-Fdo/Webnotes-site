## **Course:** 200-301 CCNA

**Network types**

- Network types are commonly grouped by the area they cover.
- A **PAN** (Personal Area Network) connects personal devices over a short range.
- A **LAN** (Local Area Network) connects devices within a limited local area.
- A **MAN** (Metropolitan Area Network) spans a city or larger campus area.
- A **WAN** (Wide Area Network) connects networks over large geographic distances.
- Network type choice depends on coverage needs and communication requirements.

**Architectural design**

- Network architecture describes how devices and links are arranged.
- A **two-tier** design separates the network into two main layers.
- A **three-tier** design adds another layer for more structured scaling.
- A **spine-leaf** design uses a predictable fabric-style layout.
- Design selection affects scalability, simplicity, and traffic flow.

**Practical comparison**

- Smaller networks usually favor simpler designs.
- Larger networks often need more structured architectures.
- Wireless technologies often support short-range personal connections.
- Wired and wireless links can both appear in LAN designs.
- The best design balances reach, performance, and manageability.

**CCNA focus**

- Know the common network categories by coverage area.
- Recognize basic architecture names when you see them in diagrams.
- Match the design to the size and purpose of the network.
- Use conservative definitions when a source does not provide exact limits.
