## **Course:** 200-301 CCNA

**Network topology basics**

- Network topology describes the arrangement of devices and links in a network.
- It shows how nodes connect and how data flows between them.
- Topology choice affects performance, cost, reliability, and security.
- A well-planned topology helps you optimize traffic flow and resource sharing.

**Physical and logical topology**

- **Physical topology** is the actual layout of cables and devices.
- **Logical topology** is how data moves across the network.
- The logical view can differ from the physical layout.
- Cisco describes topology as both the physical and logical structure of a network.

**Common topology types**

- **Point-to-point** connects two devices directly.
- It is the simplest form of communication between two nodes.
- It supports direct sender-to-receiver traffic.
- It can provide high bandwidth.

- **Bus** uses a shared communication line.
- Devices share the same medium.
- It is cost-effective.
- It can be prone to collisions.

- **Star** connects devices to a central hub or switch.
- It is widely used in modern networks.
- It simplifies troubleshooting because the center point is easy to inspect.
- A failure at the central device can affect the network.

- **Ring** connects devices in a loop.
- Data follows a circular path through the network.
- It is a physical topology.
- Logical behavior may use token passing in some ring networks.

- **Mesh** provides multiple paths between devices.
- It improves fault tolerance.
- It can increase cost and complexity.
- It is useful when redundancy matters.

- **Tree** uses a hierarchical structure.
- It combines characteristics of other topologies.
- It supports branching network designs.
- It is common in larger, layered networks.

- **Hybrid** combines two or more topology types.
- It lets you mix design features to fit requirements.
- It is useful when one topology alone does not meet needs.

**Why topology matters**

- Topology helps you plan where to place nodes.
- It helps you match design to service requirements.
- It makes faults easier to locate.
- It supports better maintenance and troubleshooting.

**CCNA study focus**

- Know the difference between physical and logical topology.
- Recognize the common topology types by their layout.
- Match each topology to its main advantage or limitation.
- Use topology knowledge to reason about traffic flow and resilience.
