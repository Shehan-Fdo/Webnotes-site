## **Course:** 200-301 CCNA

**VPN overview**

- A **VPN** (Virtual Private Network) creates a private communication path over a public network.
- You use it to protect traffic between endpoints that do not share a direct private link.
- VPNs support secure connectivity for users and sites.

**Common VPN use cases**

- **Remote access** VPN connects an individual user to a private network.
- **Site-to-site** VPN connects two networks through a VPN tunnel.
- VPNs help extend private network access across untrusted networks.

**Security goals**

- VPNs aim to provide confidentiality for traffic in transit.
- VPNs also help preserve integrity of data.
- Authentication helps verify the identity of the VPN peer.

**IPsec basics**

- **IPsec** (Internet Protocol Security) is a common VPN technology.
- IPsec is used for secure site-to-site VPN deployments.
- It supports encrypted communication between VPN endpoints.

**VPN design considerations**

- You need matching VPN settings on both ends of the tunnel.
- You should plan for the network path between peers.
- You should verify that the VPN type matches the use case.

**Practical exam focus**

- Know the difference between remote access and site-to-site VPNs.
- Know that VPNs provide secure communication over an untrusted network.
- Know that IPsec is associated with site-to-site VPNs.

> Use conservative wording when recalling VPN features: the exam focus is on the concept and purpose, not on vendor-specific implementation details.
