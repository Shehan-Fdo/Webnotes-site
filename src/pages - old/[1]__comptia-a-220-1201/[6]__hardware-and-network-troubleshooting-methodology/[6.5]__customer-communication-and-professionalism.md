## **Course:** Comptia A+ 220-1201

**Customer Communication and Professionalism**

CompTIA A+ includes customer support skills alongside technical troubleshooting. You need to communicate clearly, act professionally, and follow a structured process when resolving issues.

**Troubleshooting Methodology**

CompTIA describes a repeatable troubleshooting methodology that helps you solve issues efficiently.

1. Identify the problem.
2. Establish a theory of probable cause.
3. Test the theory to determine the cause.
4. Establish a plan of action to resolve the problem and identify potential effects.
5. Implement the solution or escalate as necessary.
6. Verify full system functionality and, if applicable, implement preventive measures.

- Use the process as a guide when you diagnose problems.
- Test your theory before you change the system.
- Verify the fix after you apply it.

**Professional Behavior**

Professionalism affects how customers and coworkers perceive your work.

- Arrive on time.
- Maintain a positive attitude.
- Dress appropriately for the workplace.
- Follow through on commitments.
- Protect confidentiality.
- Follow company policies.
- Respect workplace diversity.
- Avoid inappropriate comments or behavior.

**Customer Communication**

Effective communication helps non-technical users understand what you are doing and why.

- Use clear, simple language.
- Avoid jargon when speaking with customers.
- Listen actively before you respond.
- Match your tone and volume to the situation.
- Use appropriate body language and eye contact.
- Write clear emails, tickets, and documentation.

**Customer Service Practices**

Good customer service supports both the technical fix and the customer experience.

- Explain technical concepts in plain terms.
- Keep the customer informed during troubleshooting.
- Escalate when the issue is outside your scope.
- Confirm the system works after the repair.
- Use preventive measures when appropriate.

**Workplace Expectations**

CompTIA A+ emphasizes support roles such as IT support specialist and help desk technician.

- You support users while solving technical problems.
- You balance technical accuracy with professional communication.
- You follow best practices for safety and effective communication.
