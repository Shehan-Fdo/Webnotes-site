## **Course:** Comptia A+ 220-1201

**Mobile Device Troubleshooting Overview**

Mobile devices often fail in ways that affect power, input, display, or environmental tolerance.

- **Battery** issues are common on phones and tablets.
- **Charging** problems can come from the device or the power source.
- **Touchscreen** faults can look like slow, inaccurate, or missing input.
- **Liquid damage** and **overheating** can cause broad device failure.

**Battery Problems**

- A battery may no longer hold a charge.
- A battery may drain quickly even when it is not old.
- Poor reception can increase battery use because the device keeps searching for a signal.
- You can reduce unnecessary drain by using airplane mode when connectivity is not needed.

**Charging Issues**

- Check whether the device charges at all.
- Verify the charger, cable, and power source before replacing the device battery.
- Improper charging can point to a bad accessory or a device-side fault.

**Display and Screen Problems**

- Broken screens can prevent normal use.
- Display issues can appear as missing output or damaged visuals.
- A damaged screen may also affect touch behavior.

**Touchscreen and Digitizer Issues**

- An inaccurate touchscreen can register touches in the wrong place.
- A non-responsive touchscreen can ignore input.
- A **digitizer** converts touch into input for the device.
- Slow or jagged touch response can also happen when the system is overloaded.

**Cursor Drift**

- Cursor drift can make the pointer move without user input.
- Touch-related drift can come from a faulty digitizer or screen assembly.
- Confirm whether the issue is hardware-related or caused by system load.

**Liquid Damage**

- Liquid exposure can damage internal components.
- You should treat liquid damage as a serious hardware issue.
- If liquid damage is present, avoid normal power-on testing until you assess the device safely.

> Do not keep testing a device that shows signs of liquid damage or severe overheating.

**Overheating**

- Overheating can cause instability or shutdowns.
- Heat problems may come from heavy use, blocked ventilation, or internal failure.
- Check whether the device feels unusually hot during normal operation.

**Practical Troubleshooting Flow**

1. Identify the symptom.
2. Check power, charging, and battery behavior.
3. Test touch and display response.
4. Look for signs of liquid damage or overheating.
5. Verify whether the issue persists after basic isolation steps.

**Common Mobile Device Symptoms**

- Battery drains too fast.
- Device does not charge.
- Screen is cracked or blank.
- Touch input is inaccurate.
- Device overheats during use.
- Device shows signs of liquid exposure.
